About

Built from first principles, for families who hold Bitcoin.

The Bitcoin Family Office is the only advisory built exclusively for families with significant Bitcoin wealth. We combine deep Bitcoin knowledge with institutional estate planning, custody architecture, and tax strategy — without the compromises that come from serving both Bitcoin and legacy finance.

The Problem We Solve

Most estate attorneys have never held a private key. Most CPAs treat Bitcoin as a line item, not a monetary system. And most wealth advisors default to diversification narratives that miss what Bitcoin actually is.

This creates a gap for families with meaningful Bitcoin positions: the professionals they trust don't understand what they own, and the Bitcoin community doesn't have the legal and tax framework to structure wealth properly.

The Bitcoin Family Office exists at that intersection. We understand both the technical realities of Bitcoin custody and the legal structures that protect wealth across generations.

Our Operating Principles

Self-Custody First

Bitcoin's value proposition is native custody. We design estate plans that preserve self-custody while solving the succession problem — heirs can inherit without creating a central point of failure during your lifetime.

Long Time Horizon

Bitcoin family office planning operates on decades-long timescales. We structure for the 30-year horizon: dynasty trusts designed to outlast regulatory uncertainty, custody architectures that survive hardware cycles, governance frameworks for the next generation.

No Product Sales

We do not manage assets, sell financial products, or take custody of Bitcoin. Our revenue comes from advisory engagements only. This eliminates the conflicts of interest that compromise most wealth advisory relationships.

Jurisdictional Intelligence

State law matters enormously for Bitcoin wealth. Wyoming and South Dakota offer structural advantages unavailable elsewhere. We stay current on every state-level development affecting Bitcoin trusts, digital asset property rights, and estate tax exposure.
