Frequently Asked Questions
How does the advisory engagement process work?+

The engagement begins with a confidential consultation to understand your holdings, family structure, and planning goals. From there, our coordination team assembles the right combination of specialists — wealth advisors, tax professionals, attorneys, custody architects, and mining operators — tailored to your situation. Each provider works independently within their licensed discipline, while we ensure the overall strategy is cohesive and Bitcoin-native.

How much Bitcoin do you need to work with a family office?+

We typically work with families holding $1 million or more in Bitcoin in direct custody - not ETF shares or exchange balances. At this threshold, custody architecture, estate planning, and tax strategy justify a structured advisory approach. Families with $5M-$25M in Bitcoin holdings represent our most common engagement profile, where the stakes of poor structuring are highest and tax planning opportunities are most significant.

How are trust jurisdictions selected?+

Jurisdiction selection should be reviewed with qualified legal counsel. Our legal partners can assess jurisdictional considerations as part of the planning process.

How can families address estate tax exposure?+

Several strategies can help address estate tax exposure, including dynasty trusts, entity structuring, insurance planning, and Bitcoin mining for depreciation benefits. Estate tax planning should be coordinated with qualified legal and tax professionals. Our provider network can assess the appropriate strategies as part of the engagement.

How is custody coordination handled?+

Our provider network supports clients in establishing custody architecture frameworks appropriate to their holdings size and risk profile.

Is Bitcoin mining a tax strategy for high-net-worth families?+

Yes - Bitcoin mining is one of the most powerful tax strategies available to high-net-worth families. Mining equipment qualifies for bonus depreciation (100% in year one under current law), creating immediate tax offsets against ordinary income. Operating expenses are fully deductible. Mining income is taxed as ordinary income at receipt, establishing a lower cost basis than purchasing Bitcoin outright. For families in high brackets, a properly structured mining operation can generate substantial paper losses that offset other income while simultaneously accumulating Bitcoin. How are Bitcoin family office services different from traditional wealth management?+

Traditional wealth managers operate on an AUM fee model — they earn more when your portfolio is larger, which creates misalignment with a conviction-hold Bitcoin strategy. They are trained in custodial assets and have no expertise in key management, custody architecture, or Bitcoin-specific estate law. Coordinated Bitcoin advisory services align specialists who understand Bitcoin natively — the entire structure is optimized for long-duration holding, secure custody, and generational transfer through vetted provider networks.
