What We Do

Advisory Services for Bitcoin Families

We work with a small number of families holding significant Bitcoin positions. The work is structural — custody architecture, estate planning, tax strategy, governance, and generational transfer.

The advisory model for traditional family offices was built for equities, real estate, and fixed income. Bitcoin does not behave like any of these assets. It is a bearer instrument. It has no counterparty. It cannot be custodied by a bank. It can be inherited, but only if the technical and legal infrastructure is deliberately constructed.

This requires a different kind of advisor — one who understands both the monetary architecture of Bitcoin and the legal structures available to preserve wealth across time. We have built our practice around that intersection.

We do not manage portfolios, offer price targets, or advise on trading. We advise on structure — the foundational decisions that determine whether significant Bitcoin wealth endures or erodes across generations.

Core Practice Areas

A framework built around Bitcoin's unique properties

01

Custody Architecture

Custody is not a technical detail — it is the foundational decision from which everything else follows. A poorly designed custody architecture is an existential risk. A well-designed one provides sovereignty, resilience, and succession continuity across generations. We design multi-signature frameworks, hardware selection, geographic distribution strategies, and operational security protocols for families holding significant Bitcoin positions.

  • Multi-signature quorum design (2-of-3, 3-of-5, and custom configurations)
  • Hardware wallet selection and hardening
  • Geographic key distribution frameworks
  • Operational security and access control protocols
  • Custody provider evaluation and due diligence
  • Inheritance-ready custody architecture
02

Estate Planning & Succession

Traditional estate planning assumes custodial assets held by institutions. Bitcoin breaks that model entirely. The mechanisms of control — cryptographic keys, seed phrases, multi-signature quorums — have no direct analog in traditional estate law. We work alongside estate attorneys to design succession frameworks that respect the bearer nature of Bitcoin while ensuring orderly transfer of control across generations.

  • Bitcoin succession protocol design
  • Trust structures for Bitcoin holdings (revocable, irrevocable, dynasty)
  • Key inheritance frameworks and executor protocols
  • Multi-generational governance structure
  • Coordination with estate attorneys and trustees
  • Heir education and onboarding frameworks
03

Tax Strategy & Structuring

The tax treatment of Bitcoin is complex, evolving, and jurisdiction-dependent. Significant positions require thoughtful structuring — not to evade obligation, but to deploy legal mechanisms that align the timing of recognition events with family priorities. Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts, Charitable Remainder Trusts, direct gifting programs, and entity selection each carry meaningful consequences over a family's time horizon.

  • Cost basis management and lot-selection strategy
  • GRAT structuring for high-appreciation assets
  • Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT) implementation
  • Annual gifting program design
  • Entity structure selection (LLC, trust, family limited partnership)
  • Jurisdiction analysis for estate and income tax optimization
04

Family Governance & Policy

Wealth that endures across generations is governed deliberately. Family offices that operate from written constitutions, investment policy statements, and formalized decision frameworks outlast those that rely on informal consensus. We help Bitcoin families design the governance infrastructure that codifies values, distributes decision rights, and establishes the protocols that will guide stewardship when the founding generation is no longer present.

  • Investment Policy Statement (IPS) drafting
  • Family constitution framework
  • Custody governance and committee structure
  • Decision rights mapping and delegation protocols
  • Dispute resolution frameworks
  • Heir education program design
05

Family Office Formation

For families at the scale where a formal family office structure makes sense, we advise on the organizational, legal, and operational design of a Bitcoin-native family office — one built from first principles around self-custody, long time horizons, and sound money principles rather than inherited assumptions from the traditional wealth management industry.

  • Entity structure and jurisdiction selection
  • Investment mandate and policy design
  • Custody infrastructure build-out
  • Vendor and service provider selection
  • Reporting and compliance framework
  • Staff or advisor team structuring

Common Questions

Do you manage assets or hold custody of Bitcoin?

No. We are an advisory firm. We do not take custody of client assets and we do not manage discretionary accounts. Our work is to design the structures, frameworks, and protocols that govern how you hold and steward your Bitcoin — the execution remains with you and your qualified custodians.

What is the minimum holding to work with you?

We work with families and individuals holding at least $1M in Bitcoin. Above $5M, the complexity of custody, estate architecture, and governance typically justifies a full family office engagement. Our initial conversation is complimentary and helps us assess fit before proposing any engagement structure.

Are you a registered investment advisor (RIA)?

We provide structural, strategic, and educational advisory services. Nothing we provide constitutes investment advice under applicable securities regulations. We coordinate with your existing legal, tax, and financial advisors rather than replacing them. Full disclosures are available on our disclosures page.

How do engagements typically work?

We begin with an initial consultation to understand your holdings, current structures, and specific concerns. From there, most engagements fall into one of two categories: a focused custody or estate review (typically a defined-scope project), or an ongoing advisory relationship for families that want regular governance support, annual custody reviews, and coordination with their attorney and CPA network.

How is this different from talking to my existing financial advisor?

Most financial advisors have limited experience with Bitcoin's unique custody, tax, and succession requirements. A standard wealth management framework was designed for liquid, fungible, institutionally-held securities. Bitcoin is bearer, self-custodied, technically complex, and creates structural challenges that require specific expertise. We specialize in exactly this gap.
