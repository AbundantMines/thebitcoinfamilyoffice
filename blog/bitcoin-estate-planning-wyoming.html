Bitcoin Estate Planning in Wyoming: The Most Bitcoin-Friendly State

February 25, 2026 By Hal Turner ~2,200 words · 10 min read

When estate planning attorneys speak of "trust situs" — the legal home state of a trust — they are talking about which state's law governs the trust, which state's courts have jurisdiction, and which state's tax treatment applies. For Bitcoin families who hold significant wealth in self-custodied or institutionally-held Bitcoin, the choice of situs is not merely administrative. It is one of the most consequential decisions in the entire estate plan.

Wyoming has, through a series of deliberately crafted legislative acts spanning 2019 through 2021, positioned itself as the most legally sophisticated and Bitcoin-friendly trust situs in the United States. This guide explains what that means in practical terms — the specific statutes, the planning structures they enable, how to establish a Wyoming trust as a non-resident, and how Wyoming compares to its closest competitors, South Dakota and Nevada.

Wyoming's Tax Advantages: The Baseline

Wyoming imposes no state estate or inheritance tax. Assets held in a Wyoming trust at the death of a settlor are not subject to Wyoming-level estate tax, regardless of the settlor's domicile at death. (Federal estate tax still applies based on the value of the gross estate and applicable exemptions.) Wyoming also imposes no state income tax, which means trust income — including Bitcoin held in a Wyoming trust that generates income from lending, staking, or sale — is not subject to state income tax in Wyoming. For residents of high-income-tax states like California, New York, or Massachusetts who establish Wyoming trusts, income accumulated within the trust and not distributed to in-state beneficiaries may achieve state income tax deferral, though this requires careful structuring and legal guidance.

For Bitcoin specifically — an asset that does not generate ordinary income unless sold or lent — the income tax advantage is secondary. The primary advantages are structural: what Wyoming's law allows you to do with a trust that other states do not.

Wyoming Statute § 34-29-101 Through § 34-29-108: Digital Asset Trust Authority

Wyoming enacted its Digital Asset Statute in 2019 as part of a broad blockchain legislative package that gave Wyoming the most comprehensive statutory framework for digital assets of any U.S. state. Wyoming Statute § 34-29-101 through § 34-29-108 establish clear legal definitions for digital assets, including virtual currency, digital securities, and digital consumer assets, and provide explicit authority for trustees to hold, manage, and transfer digital assets on behalf of a trust.

This matters because the absence of such authority in most states creates genuine legal uncertainty. When a trustee in a state without digital asset statutes holds Bitcoin, there may be ambiguity about whether the trustee has the authority to manage the private keys, execute on-chain transactions, or engage with Bitcoin custody vendors. Wyoming removes that ambiguity. A Wyoming trustee has clear statutory authority to hold and transact Bitcoin as a trust asset, and Wyoming courts have jurisdiction to resolve disputes about digital asset trust administration under a legal framework that was designed with these assets in mind.

Key point: Wyoming's digital asset statutes give Wyoming trustees explicit authority to hold Bitcoin and manage private keys on behalf of the trust. No other state provides comparable statutory clarity. This alone is a sufficient reason to consider Wyoming as your trust situs.

The Rule Against Perpetuities — Abolished

Most states impose a "Rule Against Perpetuities" (RAP) that limits how long a trust can hold assets before it must terminate and distribute to beneficiaries. The traditional RAP required trusts to vest within 21 years of the last life in being at the trust's creation. Modern versions extend this to 90 or 100 years. Wyoming abolished the Rule Against Perpetuities entirely for trusts with a Wyoming trustee.

This means a Wyoming dynasty trust can hold Bitcoin in perpetuity — across multiple generations, indefinitely, without a mandatory termination date. The trust can pay income and principal to your children, grandchildren, great-grandchildren, and beyond, compounding Bitcoin wealth across generations without triggering estate tax at each generational transfer. Once assets are transferred into a properly structured dynasty trust (subject to gift and GST tax on funding), they are shielded from estate tax in all subsequent generations forever.

For Bitcoin holders who believe, as many do, that Bitcoin will continue to appreciate significantly over multi-decade time horizons, the perpetual dynasty trust is the single most powerful wealth transfer structure available. The combination of Wyoming's abolished RAP and its GST-exempt trust structures makes it possible to hold Bitcoin completely outside the estate and transfer tax system for centuries — provided the initial funding is structured correctly.

Wyoming's Directed Trust Statute: Solving the Bitcoin Custody Problem

Bitcoin custody creates a structural problem for traditional trust law. A conventional trustee holds both investment authority (deciding what to buy, sell, and hold) and administrative authority (accounting, distributions, compliance). For Bitcoin held in self-custody, the trustee also controls the private keys. This concentration of authority creates problems: most institutional trustees are unwilling to hold private keys, and those who are willing may not have the investment expertise to make custody decisions consistent with the Bitcoin family's strategy.

Wyoming's Directed Trust Statute solves this problem by allowing the trust instrument to separate investment authority from administrative authority and assign each to different parties. In a Wyoming directed trust:

  • An Investment Director (or Investment Advisor) holds authority over investment decisions, including managing Bitcoin custody — controlling the private keys, executing multisig transactions, and interacting with custody vendors.
  • An Administrative Trustee (typically a Wyoming corporate trust company) handles the non-investment functions — accounting, tax reporting, distributions, record-keeping, and legal compliance.
  • A Distribution Advisor may optionally hold authority over distribution decisions — ensuring distributions go to the right beneficiaries at the right time without requiring the administrative trustee to make those judgment calls.

In practice, this means a family can retain a Bitcoin-specialist custodian (Unchained Capital, Casa, or a similar institutional-grade service) as the Investment Director, while a Wyoming corporate trustee handles all administrative functions without ever touching the Bitcoin keys. This structure is now widely used among institutional Bitcoin families and represents the gold standard for multi-generational Bitcoin custody within a trust framework.

Wyoming's Domestic Asset Protection Trust (DAPT)

Wyoming allows self-settled Domestic Asset Protection Trusts — trusts where the settlor is also a permissible beneficiary, and the trust assets are nonetheless protected from the settlor's creditors after a waiting period. Wyoming's DAPT statute is among the strongest in the country: the reach-back period for creditor claims is 4 years, and the transfer need not be to an irrevocable trust to receive some protection (though irrevocable structures offer stronger protection).

For Bitcoin holders with business or professional liability exposure, a Wyoming DAPT can protect significant Bitcoin wealth from future creditors while still allowing the settlor to benefit from the trust. Combined with a dynasty trust structure, this creates a vehicle that is simultaneously a creditor protection tool, an estate tax planning vehicle, and a multi-generational wealth transfer mechanism.

Wyoming Blockchain Laws: The 2019–2021 Legislative Package

Wyoming's blockchain legislation was not a single act but a sustained legislative campaign across three sessions. Key elements include:

  • SF0125 (2019): Defined five categories of digital assets and established that digital assets can be property under Wyoming law.
  • HB0070 (2019): Authorized Wyoming banks to provide custodial services for digital assets.
  • SF0038 (2019): Created the Special Purpose Depository Institution (SPDI) charter, allowing Bitcoin-focused banks to operate in Wyoming without FDIC insurance requirements.
  • HB0127 (2021): Established that digital assets held in Wyoming are subject to Wyoming's UCC Article 1 and established rules for perfecting security interests in digital assets.

This legislative package gives Wyoming-based trust companies and custodians a legal framework that no other state has matched. When a Wyoming trust company acts as administrative trustee for a Bitcoin family trust, it operates within a comprehensive legal environment that explicitly addresses digital assets — not as an afterthought, but as the central design goal.

How to Establish a Wyoming Trust as a Non-Resident

You do not need to be a Wyoming resident to benefit from Wyoming trust law. The key requirement is that the trust must have a Wyoming-based trustee — typically a Wyoming-licensed corporate trust company — who maintains a meaningful presence in Wyoming and performs genuine administrative functions there. The settlor (the person who creates and funds the trust) can reside anywhere.

The process typically involves:

  1. Selecting a Wyoming corporate trustee. Options include Wyoming-licensed trust companies that specialize in Bitcoin or digital assets. Your attorney should have relationships with several and provide recommendations based on your specific situation.
  2. Drafting the trust instrument. The trust document must be drafted by an attorney experienced in Wyoming trust law. If you are using a directed trust structure, the document must clearly define the roles of the Investment Director and Administrative Trustee.
  3. Funding the trust. Bitcoin is transferred to the trust by assigning custody to the trust's designated Investment Director, with on-chain transactions documenting the transfer. This transfer is a taxable event for gift tax purposes and may require filing a gift tax return.
  4. Ongoing administration. The Wyoming trustee must perform genuine administrative functions — maintaining records, producing annual accountings, approving distributions — within Wyoming. If administration is merely nominal, other states may attempt to assert jurisdiction.

South Dakota is Wyoming's primary competitor and wins on the DAPT lookback period (2 years vs. Wyoming's 4 years). For families whose primary concern is creditor protection, South Dakota may have an edge. But for Bitcoin-specific planning — particularly directed trust structure, digital asset statutory authority, and access to Bitcoin-literate corporate trustees — Wyoming is the superior choice.

What a Wyoming Corporate Trustee Must Provide for Bitcoin

Not all Wyoming trust companies are equally prepared for Bitcoin. When evaluating a potential Wyoming corporate trustee for a Bitcoin family trust, require written confirmation of the following capabilities:

  • Digital asset custody policy: A documented policy for how digital assets are held, transferred, and reported within the trust context.
  • Directed trust experience: Demonstrated experience acting as Administrative Trustee in directed trust structures where a separate Investment Director holds Bitcoin.
  • Annual accounting for digital assets: The ability to produce annual accountings that properly value and document Bitcoin held in the trust, including transaction history and cost basis records.
  • Succession procedures: A clear procedure for what happens to Bitcoin access if the Investment Director dies, becomes incapacitated, or resigns — including transition to a successor Investment Director.
  • Wyoming presence: Confirmation that the trustee performs meaningful administrative functions in Wyoming, not merely nominal services designed to claim Wyoming situs while operating from another state.

The Conclusion: Wyoming Is Not Just the Best State for Bitcoin Trusts — It May Be the Only Adequate One

When you evaluate trust situs for a family holding multi-generational Bitcoin wealth, the analysis comes down to one question: which state's legal framework was designed to handle this specific asset class with this specific set of planning goals? The answer is Wyoming — not because of tax rates or technicalities, but because Wyoming's legislature, starting in 2019, deliberately built the most comprehensive legal infrastructure for digital assets in the United States.

A Wyoming dynasty trust, properly drafted with a directed trust structure, a Wyoming corporate trustee, and a Bitcoin-specialist Investment Director, is the institutional standard for serious multi-generational Bitcoin estate planning. If your current estate plan does not include a Wyoming trust and you hold significant Bitcoin, you have a gap worth closing.
