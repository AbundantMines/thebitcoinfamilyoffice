Bitcoin Estate Planning in Indiana

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in Indiana Est. 9 min read

Indiana imposes no state estate tax and no inheritance tax — Indiana repealed its inheritance tax effective January 1, 2013, making it one of the states that proactively removed a legacy transfer tax burden. For Bitcoin families domiciled in Indiana, this means a clean planning baseline: no state-level transfer tax to navigate, no beneficiary-class calculations, no state-level cliff effects. The entire estate planning focus can be directed toward federal-level structures, custody architecture, and multi-generational succession planning.

This guide covers Indiana's trust law framework, LLC structures for Bitcoin holding, digital asset succession under Indiana law, and the federal planning strategies that allow Indiana families to protect and transfer Bitcoin across generations. Bitcoin estate planning Indiana is a discipline that rewards comprehensive structuring — Indiana's favorable tax environment creates the opportunity, and deliberate planning converts that opportunity into generational wealth preservation.

Indiana's No-Estate-Tax Advantage: Planning in a Clean Environment

Indiana's inheritance tax repeal in 2013 was a significant pro-family planning development. Prior to repeal, Indiana imposed inheritance taxes on transfers to all beneficiary classes outside the immediate family, creating planning complexity similar to what New Jersey Bitcoin families still face today. The repeal removed that complexity entirely. Indiana Bitcoin holders now face only the federal estate tax — applicable to estates above the federal exemption ($13.99 million per individual in 2025) — with no state-level tax claim on transfers at death.

Combined with Indiana's flat 3.15% individual income tax rate (as of 2023, one of the lowest flat rates among income-tax states), Indiana offers a genuinely low-cost operating environment for Bitcoin families. The income tax applies to capital gains from Bitcoin sales, but the flat rate structure eliminates the progressive income tax penalty that families in states like California (13.3%), New York (10.9%), or New Jersey (10.75%) face on large Bitcoin transactions.

Indiana's inheritance tax repeal in 2013 created a clean-slate transfer tax environment. Bitcoin families here operate without the state-level tax complexity that burdens families in higher-tax states — freeing planning energy for what matters: federal structures and custody succession.

Indiana's Trust Code Framework

Indiana enacted the Indiana Trust Code (ITC), codified at Indiana Code §§ 30-4-1-1 et seq. Indiana's trust law is generally modern and supportive of the structures Bitcoin families need, though it does not have a dedicated directed trust statute as comprehensive as Wyoming's:

  • Trust advisors and protectors: Indiana trust instruments can designate trust advisors and trust protectors with defined powers over investment decisions, trustee removal, and custody arrangements. These roles allow the separation of technical Bitcoin custody decisions from general trust administration that effective Bitcoin trust planning requires.
  • Irrevocable trusts: Indiana trust law supports the full range of irrevocable trust structures — GRATs, SLATs, IDGTs, CRTs — that allow Bitcoin families to shift appreciation out of the federal taxable estate during the grantor's lifetime.
  • Dynasty trusts: Indiana permits trusts of personal property to continue for up to 1,000 years (Indiana Code § 32-17-8-2). This extraordinary perpetuity period makes Indiana competitive for long-horizon dynasty trust planning. Bitcoin held in a properly structured Indiana dynasty trust can benefit many generations without forced termination or a generation-skipping transfer tax event at each generation (subject to proper GST planning at funding).
  • Decanting: Indiana's decanting statute (Indiana Code § 30-4-3-36) allows a trustee with absolute discretion to transfer trust assets to a new trust with updated terms, enabling modernization of Bitcoin custody provisions in older trust instruments without court proceedings.

Indiana LLCs for Bitcoin Holding

Indiana LLCs (governed by the Indiana Business Flexibility Act, Indiana Code §§ 23-18-1-1 et seq.) provide effective Bitcoin holding structures with important estate planning advantages:

Valuation discounts: Bitcoin held in a properly structured Indiana family LLC qualifies for minority interest and lack-of-marketability discounts of 20–35% when membership interests are transferred to trusts or heirs. These discounts multiply the value transferable per dollar of federal gift tax annual exclusion or lifetime exemption, allowing Bitcoin families to systematically shift wealth out of the federal taxable estate faster than outright gifting would permit.

Operating agreement governance: Indiana's LLC statute grants broad operating agreement flexibility, allowing families to specify multi-signature custody requirements, key-person succession protocols, transaction authorization procedures, and restrictions on voluntary membership transfers. The operating agreement serves as the legal governance document for Bitcoin custody decisions.

Charging order protection: Indiana LLC law (Indiana Code § 23-18-6-7) provides charging order protection for LLC membership interests. Creditors of a member cannot force dissolution, seize Bitcoin held by the LLC, or obtain the member's management rights — they are limited to a charging order against future distributions.

Single-member LLCs: Indiana permits single-member LLCs with full charging order protection, allowing a Bitcoin holder to establish an LLC solely owned by a trust — combining the valuation and governance benefits of the LLC structure with the trust's estate and succession planning benefits in a clean two-tier structure.

Federal Estate Planning Strategies for Indiana Bitcoin Holders

Indiana's clean state tax environment means federal-level strategies operate at full efficiency — no state tax leakage reduces the benefit of any federal technique:

Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs): GRATs allow Indiana Bitcoin holders to transfer appreciation above the IRS Section 7520 hurdle rate to heirs without gift or estate tax. Rolling two-year GRATs — sequenced so that the returned annuity from each GRAT is immediately re-GRATed — create a systematic transfer mechanism for high-growth Bitcoin positions. Indiana's flat income tax means grantor trust status (which causes the grantor to pay the trust's income tax) provides an additional benefit without excessive state income tax cost.

Intentionally Defective Grantor Trusts (IDGTs): An Indiana IDGT allows a grantor to sell Bitcoin (or Bitcoin LLC interests at a discounted valuation) to an irrevocable trust in exchange for a promissory note bearing the applicable federal rate. The sale removes the Bitcoin's future appreciation from the federal taxable estate while the grantor's continued income tax liability on the trust's income provides an additional gift to trust beneficiaries. The promissory note structure avoids gift tax on the transaction entirely.

Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs): Indiana Bitcoin holders with highly appreciated positions can transfer Bitcoin to a CRT, receive an annuity for a term of years or life, defer capital gains recognition on the trust's Bitcoin sale, and ultimately direct the remainder to qualified charities. Indiana allows the full federal charitable deduction for the present value of the charitable remainder interest.

Digital Asset Succession in Indiana

Indiana enacted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), codified at Indiana Code §§ 32-39-1-1 et seq. This statute creates an important legal foundation for Bitcoin succession in Indiana:

Under Indiana's RUFADAA, authorized fiduciaries — personal representatives, trustees, and agents under powers of attorney — have the legal authority to access, manage, and transfer digital assets, including Bitcoin, if the governing instruments grant that authority. The statute establishes the familiar three-tier priority: platform online tool first, governing document second, platform terms of service third.

For self-custody Bitcoin, RUFADAA creates the legal authority, but the technical access depends entirely on proper documentation of keys, seed phrases, and custody protocols maintained outside the estate plan. Every Indiana Bitcoin estate plan should include RUFADAA-compliant language in all governing documents, accompanied by a separate technical access document maintained securely and independently, and a tested succession protocol coordinated with the actual custody architecture. Wyoming Trust Situs for Indiana Families

Indiana families with significant Bitcoin positions can access Wyoming's DPTA directed trust statute, true perpetual dynasty trust capability, and Bitcoin-specific legal infrastructure by establishing a Wyoming-sited trust — with a Wyoming corporate trustee — even while remaining Indiana residents. Wyoming's more comprehensive directed trust framework gives Bitcoin families a higher degree of custody governance separation than Indiana's trust advisor framework provides. Our guide on Wyoming trusts and LLCs for Bitcoin covers the mechanics of establishing and administering an out-of-state trust situs.

Practical Priorities for Indiana Bitcoin Families

  1. Establish an Indiana LLC as the Bitcoin holding vehicle — with an operating agreement that governs custody architecture, key-person succession, and membership transfer restrictions. The LLC creates the legal governance layer that estate planning structures reference.
  2. Fund irrevocable trust structures with discounted LLC interests — through annual exclusion gifting or IDGT note sales — systematically removing Bitcoin appreciation from the federal taxable estate over time.
  3. Incorporate explicit RUFADAA authority language in all governing instruments — trust agreements, durable powers of attorney, and wills should each contain specific digital asset access authority under Indiana's RUFADAA statute.
  4. Maintain a tested technical succession protocol separate from and updated independently of the estate plan documents — covering hardware wallet access, multi-signature key holder succession, and all custody service credentials.
  5. Evaluate a Wyoming trust situs for the primary family dynasty trust if the Bitcoin position is significant — capturing Wyoming's directed trust and perpetuity advantages while Indiana's favorable tax environment removes any state tax concern from the siting decision.
