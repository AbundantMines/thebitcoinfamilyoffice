Bitcoin Estate Planning in Florida

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Article Est. 8 min read

Florida has emerged as a primary destination for high-net-worth Bitcoin families relocating from high-tax states — particularly California and New York. The combination of no state income tax, no estate tax, robust homestead protections, and a modernized Florida Trust Code creates a legal environment where Bitcoin wealth can be preserved and transferred efficiently. For families already resident in Florida, or considering Florida domicile as part of a tax strategy, understanding the full legal landscape is essential.

No State Income Tax and No Estate Tax

Florida's constitutional prohibition on a personal income tax (Article VII, Section 5 of the Florida Constitution) eliminates state-level capital gains tax entirely. For Bitcoin families realizing gains through portfolio management, trust distributions, or entity-level transactions, Florida imposes no additional state tax layer beyond federal obligations. This is not a statutory provision that a simple legislative majority can repeal — changing it requires a constitutional amendment.

Florida also imposes no state estate or inheritance tax. The state's estate tax was tied to the now-repealed federal state death tax credit (IRC §2011), which was phased out in 2005. Unless federal law is changed to reinstate the credit — or Florida enacts a standalone estate tax — there is no Florida estate tax obligation at death for residents or for Florida-sited assets. The only estate tax exposure for Florida residents is federal.

The practical implication: a Bitcoin family domiciled in Florida faces only federal estate tax (at the $13.99M per-person exemption level in 2025) and no state-level bite at death. Coupled with the absence of income tax on lifetime gains, Florida provides one of the cleanest tax environments in the country for building and transferring Bitcoin wealth.

Florida's Exceptional Homestead Protection

Florida's homestead exemption (Article X, Section 4 of the Florida Constitution) is among the strongest creditor protection provisions in any U.S. jurisdiction — unlimited in value for a Florida resident's primary residence. While Bitcoin is not real property, the homestead protection is relevant to Bitcoin estate planning in two ways.

First, it illustrates Florida's broader legal culture of robust asset protection — a culture reflected in its LLC charging order protections, spendthrift trust provisions, and tenancy by the entirety rules. Second, for families with significant real estate alongside Bitcoin holdings, the homestead exemption creates planning flexibility: real estate can be held relatively unprotected while Bitcoin is held in protective structures without sacrificing overall family security.

Florida's tenancy by the entirety (available to married couples under Florida Statute §689.115) provides similar unlimited protection for jointly held personal property — including, under Florida case law, financial assets held in joint accounts. For Bitcoin held in joint accounts or LLCs owned by a married couple as tenancy by the entirety, creditor protection can be substantial.

Florida Trust Code and Spendthrift Provisions

Florida adopted the Uniform Trust Code in 2007, codified as the Florida Trust Code (Florida Statutes Chapter 736). The framework provides strong statutory foundations for trust formation, modification, and administration. Key provisions for Bitcoin families:

  • Spendthrift trusts (§736.0502): Florida permits robust spendthrift provisions in trust instruments, preventing beneficiaries from assigning their interests and limiting creditors' ability to attach trust assets before distribution. For Bitcoin trusts designed to protect family wealth across generations, Florida's spendthrift protections are among the strongest in the nation.
  • Qualified Spendthrift Trust (Florida Statute §736.0505(3)): Florida allows a grantor to be a discretionary beneficiary of their own irrevocable trust while maintaining spendthrift protection — a form of self-settled asset protection trust. The Qualified Spendthrift Trust requires a Florida-resident trustee and other conditions, but offers meaningful creditor protection for grantors who fund the trust and retain some beneficial interest.
  • Trust modification and decanting (§736.04117): Florida permits trust decanting — the ability to "pour" assets from an existing trust into a new trust with updated terms — without requiring court approval or unanimous beneficiary consent in many circumstances. For Bitcoin trusts with outdated custody provisions, decanting offers a path to modernize the legal framework without tax consequences.
  • Directed trusts (§736.0807): Florida recognizes directed trust arrangements, allowing the trust instrument to grant investment direction authority to a person other than the trustee. The directed trustee follows those directions and is protected from liability for doing so. This structure is directly applicable to Bitcoin trusts where a custody specialist or family investment advisor directs Bitcoin custody decisions.
Florida's asset protection culture runs deep — from unlimited homestead exemption to Qualified Spendthrift Trusts. For Bitcoin families, this translates directly into structural tools for protecting generational wealth.

Florida LLCs for Bitcoin Holding

Florida LLCs (Florida Revised Limited Liability Company Act, Florida Statutes Chapter 605) provide strong charging order protection for multi-member LLCs. Florida Statute §605.0503 limits a creditor's remedy against an LLC member to a charging order on distributions — they cannot seize the membership interest or force dissolution. For single-member LLCs, Florida courts have been less consistent in extending this protection, making a multi-member structure (e.g., including a spouse or trust as an additional member) advisable for families relying on charging order protection.

A Florida LLC holding Bitcoin can employ an operating agreement that specifies multi-signature custody governance, defines who holds signing authority for Bitcoin transactions, and restricts transfers of membership interests. This separates the economic ownership of Bitcoin from operational control — particularly valuable when heirs have varying levels of technical sophistication.

Florida vs. Wyoming: When to Combine Both

Florida provides excellent tax and asset protection foundations, but Wyoming still leads on Bitcoin-specific trust infrastructure — particularly the Wyoming DPTA's directed trust framework and SPDI custodian network. Many Florida-domiciled Bitcoin families establish Wyoming-sited trusts to access Wyoming's trust advantages while benefiting from Florida's income and estate tax environment.

The key tax question: does Florida claim income tax jurisdiction over a Wyoming-sited trust with Florida-resident beneficiaries? Florida has no income tax, so the state-level question doesn't arise — Florida doesn't tax trust income regardless of trust situs. This makes Florida particularly clean for the Wyoming trust strategy: the family lives in Florida (no state income tax), and their trust is sited in Wyoming (optimal trust law), with no conflict between the two jurisdictions' tax regimes.

Establishing Florida Domicile: What It Actually Requires

For families relocating to Florida from high-tax states — particularly California, which aggressively pursues former residents — establishing genuine Florida domicile is critical and requires more than simply purchasing Florida property. The key elements include: registering to vote in Florida, obtaining a Florida driver's license, filing a Declaration of Domicile (Florida Statute §222.17), spending more than 183 days per year in Florida, updating estate planning documents to reflect Florida domicile, and moving significant personal property and financial accounts to Florida.

California in particular maintains a 10-year lookback period for former residents and will assert continuing tax jurisdiction if the domicile change is not genuine and well-documented. Bitcoin families leaving California for Florida should work with a tax advisor experienced in California residency audits before assuming the move eliminates California tax exposure.

Bitcoin Estate Planning Across States

State law matters significantly in Bitcoin estate planning. Wyoming and South Dakota stand out for trust-friendly statutes, no state estate tax, and perpetuity-exempt dynasty trust structures. Your state of domicile determines probate exposure, trust taxation, and available legal tools.
