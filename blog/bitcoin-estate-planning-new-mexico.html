Bitcoin Estate Planning in New Mexico

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  8 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in New Mexico Est. 8 min read

New Mexico sits at an interesting intersection in the Bitcoin estate planning landscape. The state imposes no estate tax and no inheritance tax, and it has adopted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act to provide statutory authority for fiduciary access to digital assets. But what makes bitcoin estate planning New Mexico particularly complex for many families is not the Bitcoin-specific legal framework — it is the interaction between Bitcoin succession and the oil, gas, and mineral rights wealth that characterizes many New Mexico family estates.

Families in the Permian Basin corridor, the San Juan Basin, and across the eastern New Mexico plains frequently hold royalty interests, working interests, and mineral rights alongside growing Bitcoin positions. These are not casual investors rotating between asset classes — they are multigenerational wealth holders who understand hard assets and who increasingly recognize Bitcoin as a digital analog to the energy and mineral wealth their families have accumulated over generations. Designing an estate plan that handles both asset categories with appropriate rigor requires specialized expertise in both domains.

New Mexico's Tax Environment: No State Estate or Inheritance Tax

New Mexico eliminated its estate tax in 2003 and has not enacted an inheritance tax. This places New Mexico in a favorable category alongside most U.S. states, leaving the federal estate tax as the primary tax planning concern for New Mexico Bitcoin families with large estates.

The federal estate tax exemption is approximately $13.99 million per individual in 2025, with the exemption scheduled to revert to roughly half that amount after December 31, 2025, unless Congress acts to extend or make permanent the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act provisions. New Mexico Bitcoin families with estates approaching or exceeding the post-2025 exemption — particularly those holding appreciated oil and gas royalties alongside Bitcoin — should treat 2025 as a planning deadline, not a distant milestone. Irrevocable trust structures and family LLC recapitalization strategies implemented before the exemption reduction can lock in today's higher exemption amounts.

Oil, Gas, Mineral Rights, and Bitcoin: The Multi-Asset Succession Challenge

Oil and gas royalties and mineral rights pass through entirely different legal mechanisms than Bitcoin. Royalty interests and working interests are real property interests governed by New Mexico property law, transferred by deed and recorded in the county where the property is located. Mineral rights may be severed from surface rights. Overriding royalty interests run with specific leases. Each requires title examination, deed preparation, and county recorder involvement to transfer at death.

Bitcoin, by contrast, transfers through cryptographic authorization — not deed, not title, not any county recording. The Bitcoin exists wherever the private keys exist. Access requires knowledge of the custody architecture: hardware wallet locations, multi-signature configurations, seed phrase storage, and passphrase protections. No deed, no title insurance, no county clerk.

A New Mexico family with mineral rights and Bitcoin holds two forms of wealth that are radically different in their succession mechanics — one passes through deed and title, the other through keys and cryptography. An estate plan must address both with equal rigor.

The practical implication: a comprehensive New Mexico estate plan for a family holding both mineral rights and Bitcoin must address both succession systems explicitly. Fiduciaries — trustees, executors, agents under durable powers of attorney — must have legal authority and practical capability to administer both asset classes. This means specific authority to execute mineral rights deeds and transfers in the trust instrument and will, and separate authority and technical access protocols for Bitcoin.

LLC Structure for Multi-Asset New Mexico Families

A New Mexico LLC is often the best initial structure for families holding both mineral rights and Bitcoin. The LLC can hold working interests, royalty interests, and Bitcoin in a single entity governed by a comprehensive operating agreement. Key provisions of the operating agreement should include:

  • Separate schedules for each asset class: Schedule A for real property and mineral interests (with recording information and title details), Schedule B for Bitcoin custody architecture (hardware wallet designations, multi-signature thresholds, signing authority), updated annually without amending the entire operating agreement.
  • Succession of management authority: Explicit designation of successor managers and the procedures for transferring management authority at death or incapacity of the current manager — covering both the authority to execute mineral deeds and the authority to initiate Bitcoin transactions.
  • Transfer restrictions: Right-of-first-refusal provisions that require any member wishing to sell their interest to offer it to other family members first — preserving family ownership of both the mineral rights and the Bitcoin through successive generations.

RUFADAA and Digital Asset Succession in New Mexico

New Mexico adopted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), codified at New Mexico Statutes Annotated §§ 45-8A-1 through 45-8A-19. RUFADAA authorizes fiduciaries to access digital assets of decedents and principals, subject to any express override in the governing documents or platform terms of service. For Bitcoin specifically, the statute's relevance is primarily to exchange accounts and wallet services with online interfaces — not to self-custody Bitcoin held on hardware wallets, where access is entirely a function of who holds the keys.

New Mexico families holding Bitcoin in self-custody must address the key succession problem directly, through a technical succession protocol that documents key locations, multi-signature configurations, hardware device details, and recovery procedures — maintained separately from the trust instrument itself and updated whenever custody arrangements change.

Trust Structures for New Mexico Bitcoin Families

New Mexico adopted the Uniform Trust Code, codified at New Mexico Statutes Annotated §§ 46A-1-101 through 46A-11-1106. The New Mexico UTC provides modern trust formation and administration infrastructure, including directed trust arrangements suitable for Bitcoin governance within trust structures.

New Mexico's rule against perpetuities currently permits trusts to run for 1,000 years under the Uniform Statutory Rule Against Perpetuities, giving New Mexico families meaningful multigenerational planning horizons. This is not perpetual trust authority — Wyoming and South Dakota both permit perpetual trusts — but 1,000 years accommodates virtually any practical planning objective. New Mexico families wanting perpetual trusts can access them through Wyoming-sited trust structures administered by Wyoming corporate trustees.

Practical Priorities for New Mexico Bitcoin Families

  1. Inventory both asset classes: Create a complete inventory of mineral rights interests (by county, lease, royalty rate, and operator) and Bitcoin custody architecture (hardware devices, multi-signature configurations, exchange accounts), maintained in a secure location accessible to your fiduciaries.
  2. Establish a family LLC to hold both mineral interests and Bitcoin, with an operating agreement that addresses succession of authority for both asset classes and imposes transfer restrictions to maintain family ownership.
  3. Act before the 2025 federal exemption sunset: If your total estate — Bitcoin plus mineral rights plus other assets — approaches or exceeds the projected post-2025 federal exemption, implement irrevocable trust and LLC gifting strategies before year-end 2025 to lock in today's higher exemption.
  4. Implement RUFADAA-compliant trust language and separate Bitcoin key succession protocols, ensuring your trustees and executors have both legal authority and technical access capability.
  5. Consider Wyoming trust siting for the family trust holding LLC interests, to capture Wyoming's perpetual trust and directed trust advantages while New Mexico law governs the mineral property underlying the LLC.
