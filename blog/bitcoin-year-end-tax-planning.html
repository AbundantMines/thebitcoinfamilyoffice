Bitcoin Year-End Tax Planning: The Complete December Checklist

Every Bitcoin tax strategy has a December 31 hard deadline or a January window that closes forever on the last day of the year. What to do, in what order, before the clock runs out.

The Bitcoin Family Office · March 2026 · 14 min read

Most Bitcoin investors think about taxes in April. That is exactly the wrong time. By April 15, every decision that mattered was made on or before December 31. Gains already realized cannot be unrealized. Losses not harvested cannot be used. Gifts not made cannot reclaim the annual exclusion. Annual exclusions expire the moment the calendar turns.

Year-end Bitcoin tax planning is not a single action — it is a coordinated sequence of decisions, each with its own hard deadline, each interacting with the others. Execute them in the right order. Coordinate between your CPA, estate attorney, and investment advisor. And start early enough in December that you have time to act, not just time to regret.

This guide covers every material year-end action available to high-net-worth Bitcoin holders — in priority order, with deadlines marked clearly.

Hard vs. Soft Deadlines: Hard Dec 31 means the opportunity expires at midnight — no extensions, no grace periods. Soft Jan 15/Apr 15 means there is a filing deadline after year-end, but the underlying transaction must occur before December 31. Know the difference.

Step 1: Run Your Year-End Tax Projection First

Before executing any of the 12 strategies below, you need a current-year tax projection. Without it, you are optimizing blind. Your CPA (or a tool like the BFO tax calculator) should give you:

  • Estimated ordinary income for the year (W-2, self-employment, business income, interest/dividends)
  • Capital gains already realized (short-term and long-term separately)
  • Current MAGI estimate (critical for NIIT threshold and Roth eligibility)
  • Estimated taxable income after deductions
  • Distance to key thresholds: 0% LTCG bracket, 15%/20% LTCG boundary, NIIT $200K/$250K MAGI, IRMAA brackets
  • Available loss carryforwards from prior years
  • Remaining lifetime gift/estate tax exemption

This projection is the input for every decision that follows. Do not skip it.

The 12 Year-End Bitcoin Tax Actions

1. Tax-Loss Harvesting Hard Dec 31

Bitcoin's no-wash-sale advantage makes year-end loss harvesting uniquely powerful. Under IRC §1091, the wash sale rule applies only to stocks and securities — not to property. Bitcoin is property. You can sell a losing Bitcoin position to realize the loss, immediately repurchase the same amount of Bitcoin, and the loss is fully valid.

What this means in practice: identify all positions where your current value is below your cost basis, sell before December 31, and capture the loss. The loss offsets gains already realized this year, reducing your tax bill dollar-for-dollar. Any excess loss carries forward indefinitely to future years.

Priority within the year: Short-term losses (held less than one year) are more valuable than long-term losses because they offset short-term gains taxed at up to 37% vs. long-term at 23.8%. Harvest short-term losses first.

Warning on ETFs: Do not repurchase spot Bitcoin ETFs (IBIT, FBTC, ARKB) within 30 days of harvesting. The IRS has not formally ruled on whether these are "substantially identical" to Bitcoin, but the risk is real. Repurchase direct Bitcoin; wait 31+ days before switching back to ETFs.

Full detail: Bitcoin Tax-Loss Harvesting Guide and Bitcoin Wash Sale Rule.

2. 0% LTCG Bracket Harvesting Hard Dec 31

The 0% long-term capital gains bracket is the most underutilized planning tool in Bitcoin taxation. In 2026, the 0% rate applies to LTCG up to approximately:

  • $48,350 for single filers
  • $96,700 for married filing jointly
  • $64,750 for head of household

If your ordinary taxable income is below these thresholds, every dollar of Bitcoin LTCG realized up to the threshold is taxed at zero percent. Even if you are well above these thresholds, there may be room between your ordinary income and the threshold to realize some gains at 0%.

Formula: 0% Window = LTCG threshold − estimated ordinary taxable income. Realize LTCG up to that amount. Then immediately repurchase the same Bitcoin — your basis resets to the higher price permanently, at zero tax cost.

Example: MFJ couple, $60,000 ordinary taxable income. Window = $96,700 − $60,000 = $36,700 of LTCG at 0% federal. Realize $36,700 in Bitcoin gains before Dec 31, repurchase immediately. Basis permanently resets — future bull market gains on those lots start from the new higher basis.

Full detail: Bitcoin Long-Term Capital Gains Guide.

3. Annual Gift Exclusion — Use It or Lose It Hard Dec 31

The annual gift tax exclusion — $19,000 per recipient in 2026 — expires December 31. Unused exclusion does not carry forward. A couple with gift-splitting can transfer $38,000 per recipient, per year, with no gift tax and no Form 709 filing required.

For a Bitcoin-holding family with multiple children and grandchildren, this is a systematic wealth transfer program:

  • 2 parents × $19,000 × 3 children = $114,000/year out of the taxable estate
  • 2 parents × $19,000 × 3 children × 3 grandchildren = $228,000/year (if grandchildren added)
  • Over 10 years: $1.14M–$2.28M removed from the taxable estate with zero tax

Caution on basis: Gifted Bitcoin carries over your basis to the recipient (not a step-up). High-basis Bitcoin is better for gifts than low-basis Bitcoin. If you intend to hold the Bitcoin until death (and the recipient will inherit it), the gift sacrifices the §1014 step-up. Coordinate gifting decisions with your overall estate plan.

Full detail: Bitcoin Gifting Strategy Guide.

4. Charitable Donation of Appreciated Bitcoin Hard Dec 31

Donating long-term appreciated Bitcoin to a qualified charity (or Donor Advised Fund) before December 31 produces a double tax benefit: you receive a deduction for the full fair market value, and you pay zero capital gains tax on the embedded appreciation. This is superior to selling Bitcoin and donating cash in every scenario where you have significant embedded gains.

The math on a $100,000 Bitcoin donation (basis $10,000):

  • Sell and donate cash: Deduction = $100,000; capital gain tax on $90,000 = $21,420 (23.8%). Net benefit = $100,000 deduction − $21,420 tax = effective $78,580 contribution.
  • Donate Bitcoin directly: Deduction = $100,000; capital gains tax = $0. Net benefit = $100,000 deduction, full value to charity. $21,420 more efficient.

DAF advantage: A Donor Advised Fund allows you to donate before December 31 (locking in the tax year deduction) and grant to specific charities over subsequent years. The Bitcoin can be held in the DAF if the DAF accepts crypto. Major DAFs accepting Bitcoin: Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, Vanguard Charitable, Bitcoin-focused DAFs (The Giving Block).

Deduction limit: Cash donations are deductible up to 60% of AGI; appreciated property donations (including Bitcoin) are deductible up to 30% of AGI. Excess carries forward 5 years.

5. NIIT / MAGI Management Hard Dec 31

The 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax applies once MAGI exceeds $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (MFJ) — frozen since 2013. If you are near these thresholds, every additional dollar of MAGI above the cutoff triggers 3.8% NIIT on net investment income. Bitcoin capital gains are NII.

Actions to take before Dec 31 if near the NIIT threshold:

  • Accelerate deductible business expenses into the current year (reduces AGI/MAGI)
  • Maximize retirement plan contributions (401k, SEP-IRA, Solo 401k) — all pre-tax contributions reduce MAGI
  • If running an active Bitcoin mining business: ensure mining income is classified as active (not passive) — active mining income is NOT NII, and mining losses can offset NII dollar-for-dollar
  • Defer income that can be legitimately shifted to next year (postpone consulting invoicing, delay year-end bonus if controllable)
  • Consider installment sale structure for any Bitcoin liquidation near the threshold: spread gains over multiple tax years to keep each year below the NIIT cutoff

Full detail: Bitcoin NIIT Guide.

6. Roth Conversion Window Hard Dec 31

Roth IRA conversions must occur by December 31 of the tax year to count for that year. A Roth conversion is taxable as ordinary income in the year of conversion — adding to your MAGI. This creates a direct competition with Bitcoin capital gain realization for available bracket room.

Coordination rule: Do not execute both a large Roth conversion and a large Bitcoin gain realization in the same year without modeling both together. Both consume bracket room. Both increase MAGI toward the NIIT threshold. The optimal approach is to decide which tool uses the available bracket capacity more efficiently and prioritize accordingly.

When to prioritize Roth conversion over Bitcoin gains:

  • You have significant traditional IRA balances that will force large RMDs at high ordinary income rates in retirement
  • Your Bitcoin position is low-basis and intended to be held until death (step-up eliminates the gain anyway — no urgency to realize)
  • Current ordinary income is temporarily lower than expected future income

When to prioritize Bitcoin gain realization (0% harvesting) over Roth conversion:

  • You have high-gain, high-basis lots that will generate significant capital gains eventually
  • IRA balances are modest relative to Bitcoin position
  • You are in an unusually low income year that creates a wide 0% LTCG window

Full detail: Bitcoin Roth IRA Conversion Guide.

7. Maximize Retirement Plan Contributions Soft Apr 15 / Oct 15

401(k) contributions must be made by December 31 for employee elective deferrals. IRA contributions can be made until April 15 of the following year (October 15 with extension). SEP-IRA and Solo 401(k) employer contributions can also be made until tax filing with extension.

For Bitcoin-wealthy individuals with business income, the Solo 401(k) is the most powerful tool: up to $70,000 in combined employee + employer contributions in 2026. This reduces MAGI dollar-for-dollar, creates tax-deferred compounding, and potentially reduces the NIIT threshold exposure.

Employee elective deferrals ($23,500 limit in 2026, $31,000 if age 50+) must be elected and contributed by December 31 from W-2 or self-employment income actually received in the year.

8. Mining Equipment and Bonus Depreciation Hard Dec 31

If you operate a Bitcoin mining business, equipment placed in service by December 31 is eligible for bonus depreciation in the current tax year under IRC §168(k). The 2026 bonus depreciation percentage depends on applicable law at the time — under current OBBBA-era rules, verify the current bonus depreciation phase-down schedule with your CPA.

The strategic implication: if you plan to purchase mining equipment, placing it in service before year-end accelerates the depreciation deduction by a full year. For significant equipment purchases ($500K+), this timing difference can represent $100,000+ in tax savings. Mining depreciation reduces both ordinary income and — if the mining activity is active — net investment income subject to NIIT.

Full detail on how Bitcoin mining integrates with the overall tax picture: Bitcoin Mining Tax Strategy.

9. Estimated Tax Payments Soft Jan 15

If you realized significant Bitcoin gains during the year and did not withhold or pay estimated taxes quarterly, you may owe a penalty on the underpayment. The fourth quarter estimated tax payment for individuals is due January 15 of the following year.

To avoid underpayment penalties, you need to have paid the lesser of:

  • 100% of prior year's tax liability (110% if prior year AGI exceeded $150,000)
  • 90% of current year's actual tax liability

If you realize a large Bitcoin gain late in the year and have not been making quarterly payments, the January 15 fourth-quarter payment is your last opportunity to reduce the underpayment penalty before the April filing date. Consult your CPA on the exact amount needed — the safe harbor calculation depends on your specific situation.

10. GRAT Timing: Lock In the §7520 Rate Hard Dec 31 for this year's GRAT

A Grantor Retained Annuity Trust (GRAT) transfers appreciation above the §7520 rate to heirs without gift tax. The §7520 rate is set monthly at 120% of the mid-term AFR — and it locks in when the GRAT is funded.

Year-end is a natural time to evaluate whether the current §7520 rate is favorable relative to your expected Bitcoin return. In a rising rate environment (as in 2025–2026), locking in the rate from a lower-rate month earlier in the year is sometimes possible by funding the GRAT in that month rather than waiting.

If you want a GRAT to count as a 2026 tax transaction, the trust must be funded and the annuity payments must begin in 2026. A GRAT funded December 31, 2026 is a 2026 GRAT. Timing matters for when the annuity stream starts and for the estate tax return reporting.

11. Annual Exclusion Gifts to Trusts — Crummey Notices Hard Dec 31

If you have an irrevocable trust structured to receive annual exclusion gifts (a "Crummey trust"), the beneficiaries must receive written Crummey notices within a reasonable time before December 31. Typically, notices should be sent by early December to allow the required withdrawal window (usually 30 days) to close before year-end.

If you intend the trust to receive this year's annual exclusion contributions and the Crummey notices have not been sent, this is urgent. Without proper notices, the contribution is not a present interest gift and does not qualify for the annual exclusion — meaning the full amount counts against your lifetime exemption.

Full detail: Bitcoin Trust for Children Guide.

12. Entity Formation Timing Hard Dec 31 for current-year tax treatment

If you intend to establish a Wyoming LLC, South Dakota dynasty trust, or other entity structure to hold Bitcoin, formation date matters for tax purposes. An entity formed December 31 is a current-year entity. An entity formed January 1 is a next-year entity. Transactions, elections, and gains/losses cannot be allocated to an entity that did not exist when they occurred.

Practical implication: if you plan to transfer Bitcoin to a new LLC or trust structure, form the entity first. Transfer gains realized inside the entity after formation — gains realized personally before formation cannot be pushed into the entity retroactively. Wyoming LLC formation typically takes 1–3 business days. South Dakota trust establishment requires a trustee and trust agreement — allow several weeks for attorney drafting.

The One Strategy That Works All Year: Bitcoin Mining

Every item in this guide is a year-end action. Bitcoin mining is the only strategy that reduces your tax burden continuously throughout the year — depreciation deductions flow during the year, mining income qualifies as active (not net investment income), and the equipment purchase can be timed for maximum bonus depreciation impact.

For Bitcoin-wealthy families already exploring tax optimization, mining is often the highest-leverage single strategy available — combining income generation, depreciation shields, NIIT reduction, and alignment with the asset class conviction that created the wealth in the first place.

12-Item Bitcoin Year-End Tax Checklist

  • Run year-end tax projection with CPA — know your MAGI, brackets, and available 0% LTCG window before acting
  • Harvest tax losses: sell underwater Bitcoin lots, immediately repurchase direct BTC (not ETFs within 30 days)
  • Fill 0% LTCG bracket: realize gains up to threshold and repurchase to reset basis — permanent tax-free step-up
  • Make annual exclusion gifts ($19K/recipient, $38K with gift-splitting) before Dec 31 — use it or lose it forever
  • Donate appreciated Bitcoin (held 12+ months) to DAF or charity before Dec 31 — full FMV deduction, $0 capital gains
  • Send Crummey notices for irrevocable trusts by early December (need 30-day withdrawal window)
  • Model Roth conversion vs. 0% LTCG bracket room — coordinate, don't stack both aggressively in same year
  • Manage MAGI: maximize retirement contributions, accelerate deductions, classify mining income as active if applicable
  • Place mining equipment in service by Dec 31 for current-year bonus depreciation deduction
  • Form new entities (WY LLC, SD trust) before year-end if 2026 treatment is needed for transactions
  • Execute Roth conversion by Dec 31 if bracket room remains after all of the above
  • Pay 4th quarter estimated taxes by January 15 — avoid underpayment penalty on large year-end gains
