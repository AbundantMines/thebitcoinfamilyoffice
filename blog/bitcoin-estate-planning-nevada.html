Bitcoin Estate Planning in Nevada

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Article Est. 8 min read

Nevada has spent decades building a legal infrastructure designed to attract wealth management, asset protection, and trust administration — and it shows. No state income tax, no estate tax, one of the country's strongest self-settled asset protection trust statutes, a sophisticated directed trust framework, and dynasty trust provisions with no rule against perpetuities collectively make Nevada a top-tier jurisdiction for Bitcoin estate planning. For families seeking the advantages of a favorable trust situs without moving to Wyoming, Nevada offers a compelling alternative with a deeper pool of trust administration professionals.

Zero State Tax at Every Level

Nevada imposes no personal income tax (Nevada Constitution, Article 10, Section 1) and no corporate income tax. Capital gains on Bitcoin are not subject to Nevada tax at any level — federal tax only. Nevada also has no state estate tax, inheritance tax, or gift tax. A Nevada-domiciled family or a Nevada-sited trust faces no state-level tax friction on Bitcoin appreciation, distributions, or transfers at death.

Nevada's constitutional prohibition on income tax makes this advantage structurally durable — it cannot be reversed by the legislature alone. For families evaluating the long-term tax environment for a multigenerational Bitcoin trust, constitutional protections matter more than statutory ones, which can change with political majorities.

Nevada Asset Protection Trusts (NAPTs)

Nevada enacted its self-settled spendthrift trust statute (Nevada Revised Statutes §166.010 et seq.) in 1999 and has systematically strengthened it in subsequent legislative sessions. A Nevada Asset Protection Trust (NAPT) allows a grantor to transfer assets to an irrevocable trust of which the grantor can be a discretionary beneficiary, while protecting those assets from future creditors — provided certain conditions are met.

The key features of Nevada's NAPT statute include:

  • Two-year fraudulent transfer lookback (NRS §166.170): Nevada's statute of limitations for creditors challenging a NAPT transfer is among the shortest in the country — only two years from when the creditor's claim arose, or six months after the creditor could have discovered the transfer, whichever is later. This is significantly shorter than the four-year lookback in some other states.
  • Qualified disposition protection: Once a transfer to a NAPT has seasoned past the fraudulent transfer period, existing creditors and future creditors whose claims arise after the transfer cannot reach the trust assets, even if the grantor is a beneficiary.
  • No exception creditors for spousal maintenance: Nevada's statute does not carve out exceptions for alimony or spousal maintenance claims against the trust — unlike some states that specifically exclude these claims from NAPT protection.
  • Retained powers allowed: Nevada permits grantors to retain the power to veto distributions, remove and replace trustees (within limits), and add or remove beneficiaries without destroying the asset protection benefits.

For Bitcoin families seeking to protect a significant holding from future creditor claims — including professional liability, business risks, or uncertain future obligations — a Nevada NAPT funded with Bitcoin is one of the strongest protection vehicles available under U.S. law.

Nevada's asset protection trust statute is one of the nation's strongest — combining a short fraudulent transfer lookback period, few exception creditors, and robust retained powers for grantors.

Nevada Directed Trust Statute

Nevada's Directed Trust statute (NRS Chapter 163, particularly §163.5547 et seq.) formalizes the separation of trust functions across multiple parties — a structure particularly well-suited to Bitcoin trusts where investment expertise and administrative responsibility require different professional competencies.

Under Nevada's directed trust framework:

  • A directed trustee holds legal title, handles distributions and administrative duties, and is expressly protected from liability for following directions of an investment trust advisor or distribution advisor — provided the directions do not violate a clear and obvious breach of fiduciary duty.
  • An investment trust advisor has exclusive authority over investment decisions — including all Bitcoin custody decisions: choice of hardware, multi-signature configuration, key storage locations, and transaction execution. The advisor bears investment fiduciary responsibility; the trustee does not.
  • A distribution trust advisor can hold independent authority over distributions to beneficiaries, further separating operational functions for complex family situations.

This structure is directly applicable to Bitcoin trusts: a Nevada-licensed corporate trustee handles administration, a Bitcoin custody specialist serves as investment trust advisor with full authority over multi-signature custody architecture, and the family retains influence through a trust protector role. The corporate trustee is protected from liability for the custody decisions it does not make.

Nevada Dynasty Trusts: No Rule Against Perpetuities

Nevada abolished the rule against perpetuities for trusts in 1995, allowing Nevada-sited trusts to hold assets for an unlimited duration. A properly structured Nevada dynasty trust can hold Bitcoin in perpetuity — distributing income and principal according to the grantor's directions across generations indefinitely.

Nevada's dynasty trust framework (NRS §21.010 et seq. and related provisions) allows trusts to accumulate income, make discretionary distributions, and continue operating without mandatory termination. For a Bitcoin family with a multi-generational investment thesis, a Nevada dynasty trust provides the legal container to match that horizon.

Using Nevada as Trust Situs Without Living There

Nevada's trust advantages are available to families domiciled anywhere. To establish a valid Nevada trust situs, the trust instrument must designate Nevada law as governing law, at least one trustee must be Nevada-resident or a Nevada-chartered trust company, and the trust must have some qualifying activity in Nevada (typically satisfied by the trustee's administrative functions).

For families living in high-tax states like California or New York, the income tax implications of Nevada trust situs depend on the home state's rules for taxing non-resident trusts with resident beneficiaries. As noted in our California analysis, California's beneficiary-nexus rules may still subject a Nevada trust's undistributed income to California tax if the trust has California-resident non-contingent beneficiaries. Nevada's lack of income tax provides no protection against the home state's claim — only the home state's rules determine whether the home state can tax the trust's income.

For beneficiaries domiciled in Nevada, Texas, Florida, or other no-income-tax states, a Nevada trust situs provides clean, frictionless accumulation. See our complete Bitcoin estate planning guide for the full framework on how to integrate trust situs selection with domicile planning.

Nevada LLCs: Strong Creditor Protection

Nevada LLCs (Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 86) provide charging order protection as the exclusive remedy of a judgment creditor against an LLC member's interest. Nevada courts have been consistent in upholding this protection even for single-member LLCs — distinguishing Nevada from states like California where single-member LLCs receive diminished protection. A Nevada LLC holding Bitcoin provides one of the strongest domestic liability shields available for concentrated Bitcoin positions.

Bitcoin Estate Planning Across States

State law matters significantly in Bitcoin estate planning. Wyoming and South Dakota stand out for trust-friendly statutes, no state estate tax, and perpetuity-exempt dynasty trust structures. Your state of domicile determines probate exposure, trust taxation, and available legal tools.
