Bitcoin Family Office in Vermont: The 8.75% Problem — and Why New Hampshire Is 15 Minutes From Burlington

Vermont has an 8.75% top income tax rate — the fourth-highest in New England, behind only Massachusetts (9% with millionaire surtax), New York (10.9%), and New Jersey (10.75%). Vermont also imposes a state estate tax. There is no municipal income tax surcharge, no inheritance tax, and Vermont's outdoor recreation economy and progressive political culture attract a distinct type of Bitcoin holder — often technology professionals who moved for lifestyle, remote workers from NYC and Boston, and second-generation wealth from Vermont's artisan food and brewing ecosystem. This guide covers the planning reality for Vermont's Bitcoin wealth and the most important decision most Vermont holders are deferring: the New Hampshire domicile analysis.

⚠ Income Tax Alert: Vermont's 8.75% top income tax rate on a $5M Bitcoin gain means approximately $437,500 in Vermont income tax — in addition to federal capital gains and NIIT. The combined rate of approximately 32.55% is the third-worst in New England. Verify current rates with a Vermont-licensed CPA.

Vermont's Tax Reality

Vermont's graduated income tax reaches 8.75% above approximately $200,000 of income. Unlike Massachusetts (which has a flat 5% + 4% surtax) or New York (which has the NYC surcharge complexity), Vermont's 8.75% applies cleanly at the state level with no city surcharge. But there is no capital gains preference — Bitcoin gains are taxed at the same rate as ordinary income.

Vermont's estate tax: Vermont imposes estate tax on estates above approximately $5 million (verify current threshold — it has been adjusted periodically). Rates reach 16% on the portion above the threshold. Unlike Rhode Island's painful $1.7M threshold, Vermont's $5M threshold means the estate tax primarily affects Bitcoin holders with larger positions. But for someone holding $5M+ in Bitcoin who lives in Vermont, the combined planning problem — 8.75% income tax on gains during life, plus estate tax at death — is severe.

The New Hampshire Option: Closer Than You Think

Burlington to Keene, NH: 2 hours. Burlington to Manchester, NH: 2.5 hours. Burlington to Portsmouth, NH (the New Hampshire Seacoast): 3.5 hours. For Vermont residents near the Connecticut River border (White River Junction, Springfield, Brattleboro), New Hampshire is across the bridge — 0% income tax, no estate tax, same rural New England lifestyle. The planning case for New Hampshire domicile is the single most important document a Vermont Bitcoin holder can read.

At Vermont's 8.75% rate versus New Hampshire's effective 0% on Bitcoin capital gains, the savings on a $5M gain are approximately $437,500. On a $10M gain: $875,000. On a $20M gain: $1.75 million. Vermont's estate tax adds additional exposure for large estates. The cumulative lifetime + death tax differential between staying in Vermont and moving to New Hampshire is among the largest state-level planning arbitrage available anywhere in New England.

The practical barriers to establishing New Hampshire domicile from Vermont are minimal for remote workers and retirees: obtain a New Hampshire driver's license, register to vote in NH, spend the majority of your time in NH, and update your estate plan to reflect NH domicile. Vermont has no aggressive reciprocal audit program targeting departed residents comparable to Massachusetts or New York. The lifestyle sacrifice is modest for most Bitcoin holders — Vermont's outdoor recreation, skiing, and community are replicated in New Hampshire's lake region, White Mountains, and Connecticut River Valley communities.

Vermont Wealth Communities

Burlington Tech Corridor

Burlington is the largest city in Vermont and home to Vermont's most significant technology wealth concentration:

  • GlobalFoundries Fab 9 — Burlington, Vermont hosts a major GlobalFoundries semiconductor fabrication facility. The CHIPS and Science Act (2022) directed significant federal investment into US semiconductor manufacturing, including GlobalFoundries' Vermont operations. The professional wealth at GlobalFoundries Burlington — engineers, scientists, and senior operations staff — represents Vermont's most significant private-sector technology wealth class.
  • IBM Legacy — IBM had extensive semiconductor manufacturing operations in Essex Junction, Vermont (adjacent to Burlington) for decades. The IBM Vermont community created a professional wealth class that has diversified into startups, consulting, and investment over time.
  • University of Vermont (UVM) — Burlington's major university and largest employer, creating the usual academic wealth class (lower but stable) plus significant research commercialization activity in Vermont's biomedical and agricultural science sectors.

Vermont's Artisan Economy: Small Businesses, Large Cumulative Wealth

  • Green Mountain Coffee Roasters / Keurig Dr Pepper — founded in Waterbury, Vermont. The original GMCR built significant Vermont business wealth before the Keurig acquisition and eventual merger with Dr Pepper. The Vermont business community around GMCR's growth represents a notable local wealth creation event.
  • Ben & Jerry's — founded in Burlington; acquired by Unilever in 2000 for $326 million. Ben Cohen and Jerry Greenfield's philanthropic activities and the Vermont entrepreneurial culture they helped create represent a distinct progressively-oriented Vermont wealth profile.
  • Cabot Creamery / Agri-Mark — Vermont's cooperative dairy industry creates modest but stable agricultural wealth distributed across Vermont's farming community.
  • Vermont Ski Industry — Stowe, Sugarbush, Killington, Mad River Glen, and Bolton Valley create resort and real estate wealth. The Stowe community, in particular, attracts NYC and Boston UHNW second-home buyers who occasionally establish Vermont domicile — and then discover Vermont's 8.75% income tax.

The Remote Work Migration: NYC and Boston Expats

Vermont has been one of the primary destinations for COVID-era remote work migration from New York City, Boston, and other high-cost metro areas. Vermont's state government actively incentivized remote worker relocation with cash grants. Many of these migrants — tech workers, finance professionals, creative industry employees — arrived with Bitcoin positions accumulated during their urban careers and established Vermont domicile without understanding the income tax consequences. For this community, this guide may be the first time they've seen the 8.75% rate and estate tax side by side in the context of their Bitcoin holdings.

Vermont Trust Law

Vermont adopted the Vermont Trust Code (18 V.S.A. Title 14A). Directed trust provisions and decanting are supported. Vermont has no perpetual dynasty trust statute and no DAPT legislation. Standard architecture: Wyoming LLC + South Dakota dynasty trust. For Vermont Bitcoin holders who are not changing domicile, the SD dynasty trust removes the WY LLC interest from the Vermont taxable estate for estate tax purposes if properly structured.

Vermont Bitcoin Family Office: Summary

The mitigating factor versus Hawaii (also D−) is Vermont's $5M estate tax threshold versus Hawaii's $5.49M — both in the same range — and Vermont's no-inheritance-tax profile. But Vermont's 8.75% income tax versus Hawaii's 11% are both in the "severe cost" tier. The single most important planning action for most Vermont Bitcoin holders is the New Hampshire domicile analysis, followed immediately by establishing the WY LLC + SD dynasty trust structure regardless of domicile decision.

10-Item Vermont Bitcoin Planning Checklist

  • Verify current Vermont income tax rate and estate tax threshold with a Vermont-licensed CPA and estate planning attorney — rates and thresholds may have changed since this guide was published
  • Calculate your Vermont-vs-New Hampshire lifetime + death tax savings — for most Vermont Bitcoin holders with $2M+ positions, this number is six or seven figures and demands serious consideration
  • If near the Connecticut River (White River Junction, Brattleboro, Springfield): New Hampshire domicile is across the bridge — minimal lifestyle sacrifice for potentially hundreds of thousands in tax savings
  • If Stowe or Burlington remote worker: model the NH Lakes Region or Seacoast as primary residence — outdoor recreation, community quality, and proximity to VT remain excellent from NH
  • Form Wyoming LLC to hold Bitcoin immediately — regardless of domicile decision; removes Bitcoin from direct Vermont probate estate
  • Establish South Dakota dynasty trust — perpetual duration, DAPT, 0% trust income tax; properly structured, removes WY LLC interest from Vermont estate tax base
  • Launch annual exclusion gifting program — $19K/recipient/year, no Vermont gift tax; reduces Vermont taxable estate over time
  • If Vermont estate exceeds $5M: model ILIT for estate tax liquidity — Vermont estate tax on $10M position is $600K+; an ILIT provides liquidity to pay VT estate tax without forced Bitcoin sale at death
  • Contribute appreciated Bitcoin to Vermont charities (UVM, Vermont Community Foundation, Vermont Land Trust) — eliminates 8.75% Vermont income tax on the gain + reduces estate; best outcome for charitably-inclined Vermont holders
  • Update estate plan with Bitcoin-specific provisions: hardware wallet succession protocol, trustee authority for digital assets, multi-sig distribution procedures
