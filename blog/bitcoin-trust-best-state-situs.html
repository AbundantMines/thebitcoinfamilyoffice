Best State for a Bitcoin Trust: Wyoming vs South Dakota vs Nevada vs Delaware

Where you form your Bitcoin trust is a permanent structural decision — it determines who can serve as trustee, how long the trust can last, whether trust income is taxed at the state level, and what legal protections apply to digital assets. Most families default to their home state without examining the alternatives. Here is the full comparison.

By The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  March 13, 2026  ·  12 min read

Why Trust Situs Matters for Bitcoin

Trust situs — the legal home state of a trust — determines which state's law governs the trust's interpretation, administration, and taxation. For most assets, the situs decision primarily affects asset protection strength and dynasty trust duration. For Bitcoin, three additional factors become critical:

  • Digital asset property rights: Not all states have enacted statutory protections for digital asset ownership within trusts. Wyoming and South Dakota have explicit statutory frameworks; others rely on general property law.
  • Directed trust structure: Bitcoin families often want to separate investment management (a Bitcoin-specialist advisor) from trustee duties (a corporate trust company). Directed trust laws — strongest in Wyoming and South Dakota — enable this cleanly.
  • State income tax on trust income: Some states tax trust income; others don't. Bitcoin held in a trust that generates income (through managed liquidity events or lending) faces potential state income tax at the trust level in high-tax states.

Wyoming: The Bitcoin-Native Choice

Wyoming has been the most aggressive state in legislating for digital assets. Starting with the Digital Asset Statute in 2019 and continuing with a series of annual legislative updates, Wyoming has built the most comprehensive statutory framework for digital asset ownership, custody, and transfer in any US jurisdiction.

Key Wyoming advantages for Bitcoin trusts:

Wyoming Digital Asset Statute

Wyoming's statute explicitly classifies digital assets as property under UCC Article 8 (intangible property) or UCC Article 9 (commodity-like property), providing a clear legal framework for ownership, perfection of security interests, and transfer. This matters when a trustee holds Bitcoin — the legal basis for that custody is unambiguous under Wyoming law.

Wyoming Trust LLC

Wyoming allows a unique hybrid structure: the Wyoming Trust LLC, where an LLC serves as the trustee of a trust while the LLC's operating agreement provides governance flexibility unavailable to traditional corporate trustees. Bitcoin families with complex multi-signature custody arrangements can use the Wyoming Trust LLC to create a custom governance structure around key management, succession of control, and investment authority — all within a statutory framework designed for exactly this purpose.

Directed Trust Act

Wyoming's Uniform Directed Trust Act allows the grantor to appoint an "investment adviser" (a Bitcoin-specialist advisor or family member with technical expertise) separate from the trustee (a Wyoming trust company handling administrative duties). The trustee follows the investment adviser's directions without fiduciary liability for those directions. This is the cleanest legal framework for separating Bitcoin technical management from trust administration.

No State Income Tax

Wyoming has no individual or corporate income tax. Trust income — including capital gains from Bitcoin sales — is not taxed at the state level, regardless of where the beneficiaries reside (in most configurations).

Wyoming bottom line: Best for Bitcoin-native families who want maximum digital asset statutory protection, the Wyoming Trust LLC structure, or a directed trust with a Bitcoin-specialist investment adviser. The dedicated digital asset legislation is the clearest statutory foundation available for Bitcoin trusts.

South Dakota: The Dynasty Trust Pioneer

South Dakota pioneered the modern trust-haven movement starting in 1983 when it eliminated the rule against perpetuities — the first state to do so. It has the deepest trust ecosystem of any of the four states, with the largest concentration of specialized trust companies, the most refined confidentiality statutes, and the most permissive self-settled trust (Domestic Asset Protection Trust, or DAPT) rules.

DAPT Strength

South Dakota's Domestic Asset Protection Trust has no exception creditors — meaning domestic violence, pre-existing tort claimants, or child support claims that could pierce DAPTs in other states cannot do so in South Dakota. The 2-year statute of limitations (1 year from discovery) for fraudulent transfer claims is among the shortest available.

Digital Asset Statute (SDCL 55-1B)

South Dakota enacted specific statutory language allowing trustees to hold digital assets as trust property, providing guidance on custody, voting rights for digital assets, and the trustee's duty of care for digital asset holdings. While not as comprehensive as Wyoming's framework, it provides explicit statutory authority that Nevada and Delaware lack.

Corporate Trustee Ecosystem

South Dakota has the most developed concentration of specialized trust companies in the country — including several that have built Bitcoin custody capabilities. For families requiring an institutional corporate trustee (for QDOT compliance, for trusts exceeding $2M requiring corporate trustee, or for family governance preferences), South Dakota offers the most options.

South Dakota bottom line: Best for families prioritizing maximum DAPT asset protection, the deepest institutional trustee ecosystem, and the most mature dynasty trust infrastructure. Also excellent for very large, complex multi-generational structures where institutional oversight and established case law from SD trust companies provide stability.

Nevada: Strong Protection, Western Flexibility

Nevada occupies a middle position — strong dynasty trust and asset protection laws, no state income tax, and a growing trust company ecosystem. It lacks the dedicated digital asset statutes of Wyoming and South Dakota, and its trust industry is smaller. However, Nevada's domestic asset protection trust is strong, and its self-settled trust laws provide meaningful protection.

Nevada's primary advantages: proximity (many California and Pacific Coast families prefer a neighboring state), no state income tax, a 2-year statute of limitations on fraudulent transfer claims, and strong spendthrift protections. For families who want to avoid the perception of "going all the way to Wyoming or South Dakota," Nevada offers comparable core protections with a more familiar Western presence.

Nevada bottom line: Solid choice for Pacific Coast families who want strong dynasty trust and asset protection without the full Wyoming or South Dakota ecosystem. Not the first choice for Bitcoin-specific digital asset planning — Wyoming's statutory framework is superior for that purpose.

Delaware: Institutional Depth, Legal Certainty

Delaware's primary advantage is its Court of Chancery — a specialized equity court with over 230 years of trust and corporate law precedent. For families who prioritize legal certainty and established case law over maximum asset protection or tax minimization, Delaware's deep jurisprudence provides confidence that trust disputes will be resolved predictably.

Delaware also has strong directed trust laws, excellent decanting flexibility, and the deepest concentration of major institutional trust companies (including subsidiaries of major banks). For family offices that want Bank of America, Northern Trust, or Bessemer Trust as their Delaware trustee, the options are available.

The tradeoff: Delaware does tax trust income in some configurations (particularly where beneficiaries are Delaware residents), and its digital asset statutory framework is general property law rather than purpose-built digital asset legislation.

Delaware bottom line: Best for families that want the deepest institutional trustee options, the most established case law, and Court of Chancery dispute resolution. Less optimal for Bitcoin-specific digital asset protection or maximum DAPT strength.

What About Your Home State?

Most families live in states that are not trust havens — California, New York, Texas, Florida. Can a California resident form a Wyoming trust? Yes — with important conditions.

To take advantage of Wyoming (or South Dakota, Nevada, or Delaware) law, the trust must have a genuine nexus to that state: a resident trustee or corporate trustee in the state, trust administration activities occurring in the state, and ideally trust property held in the state. Simply "filing" in Wyoming while a California resident manages everything from Los Angeles does not create Wyoming situs.

The practical approach: appoint a Wyoming corporate trust company (or South Dakota, Nevada, or Delaware trust company) as trustee — or as co-trustee alongside your family. The trust company's presence in the state creates the genuine connection required. The grantor (you) can live anywhere. Beneficiaries can live anywhere. Only the trustee needs to be resident.

6-Item Trust Situs Decision Checklist

  • Define your primary goal. Asset protection (DAPT)? Dynasty trust (perpetual duration)? Digital asset statutory protection? Institutional trustee access? Legal certainty? Each priority points to a different state. Be explicit about which matters most before comparing.
  • Identify trustee candidates first. The trustee must be resident in the chosen state. Research trust companies in Wyoming, South Dakota, Nevada, and Delaware that have Bitcoin custody capabilities or are willing to work with your preferred Bitcoin custodian before selecting the state.
  • Confirm the trust's nexus requirements. Work with trust counsel to ensure the administration genuinely connects to the chosen state — not just a nominal address. A trust company co-trustee handling real administrative functions satisfies this; a PO box does not.
  • Model state income tax exposure. All four states have no state income tax on trust income — but verify this for your specific trust configuration and beneficiary residency. Some states attempt to tax trust income if beneficiaries live there; confirm the exposure with your CPA.
  • Consider the directed trust structure. If you want a Bitcoin-specialist investment adviser directing investments separate from the corporate trustee's administrative duties, confirm the chosen state's directed trust statute. Wyoming and South Dakota both have strong statutory frameworks for this separation.
  • Plan for decanting flexibility. Even the best trust drafting can't anticipate everything over 100+ years. Confirm the chosen state allows decanting (modifying an irrevocable trust by pouring assets into a new trust) — all four states do, but with different rules. Decanting flexibility is the long-term safety valve for perpetual trusts.

The Bottom Line

For most Bitcoin families, the choice narrows to Wyoming or South Dakota. Wyoming leads on digital asset statutory protection and the unique Wyoming Trust LLC structure. South Dakota leads on DAPT strength and institutional trustee depth. Nevada is a solid third choice for Pacific Coast families. Delaware works best when institutional trustee requirements or legal certainty preferences take priority over maximum asset protection.

The state where you live does not have to be the state where your trust lives. This is a feature, not a complication — it gives Bitcoin families access to the best trust law in the country regardless of home domicile. Use it.
