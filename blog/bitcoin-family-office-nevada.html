Bitcoin Family Office in Nevada: The Asset Protection and Privacy Advantage

Nevada offers a unique combination that no other state matches: no income tax, no estate tax, a Domestic Asset Protection Trust that allows you to be your own beneficiary, and LLC charging order protection codified by statute. For Bitcoin families prioritizing asset protection alongside estate planning, Nevada is the strongest current-generation protection structure in the US.

Nevada's Tax Advantages for Bitcoin Families

Nevada's baseline is excellent: no state income tax, no capital gains tax at the state level, and no state estate or inheritance tax. Combined with federal planning, Nevada Bitcoin families face only federal obligations — no additional state layer eroding their position.

For a California resident converting to Nevada domicile, the income tax savings alone can be transformative: California's 13.3% top income tax rate becomes zero. On $1M of Bitcoin sales in a given year, that's $133,000 retained. Over a decade of managing a large Bitcoin position, the compounding effect of zero state income tax is substantial.

Nevada vs. Wyoming baseline: Both states have no income or estate tax. Nevada's advantage is asset protection (DAPT law and LLC privacy). Wyoming's advantage is dynasty trust law (stronger directed trust statute, more trust company infrastructure). The choice often comes down to your primary concern: current-generation creditor protection (Nevada) vs. perpetual multi-generational dynasty (Wyoming).

The Nevada Domestic Asset Protection Trust (DAPT)

The Nevada DAPT is the most powerful tool in Nevada's arsenal for Bitcoin families. Formally called the Nevada Self-Settled Spendthrift Trust, it allows you to:

  • Be both grantor and discretionary beneficiary — you transfer Bitcoin to the trust, and the trust can distribute assets back to you at the trustee's discretion
  • Protect trust assets from creditors — after a 2-year seasoning period, Nevada trust assets are generally beyond the reach of the grantor's creditors
  • Maintain access to income and principal — because you're a discretionary beneficiary, you're not giving up economic access, just legal control

This combination is unusual. Most states that allow asset protection trusts require you to give up beneficial interest — the protection comes at the cost of access. Nevada allows both protection AND retained access (subject to trustee discretion).

Nevada DAPT + Bitcoin: The Mechanics

A Bitcoin family using a Nevada DAPT would structure it as follows:

  1. Establish a Nevada DAPT with an independent Nevada trustee (required — you cannot be the sole trustee)
  2. Transfer Bitcoin to the trust — this is a completed transfer for asset protection purposes (after the 2-year seasoning)
  3. The trust holds Bitcoin in a multi-sig arrangement — the Nevada trustee holds one key, you or a trusted co-holder holds another
  4. Distribution requests go to the trustee — who has discretion to approve. For operational needs, this is rarely an issue in practice
  5. Your creditors cannot reach the trust assets once seasoning is complete

Nevada LLC: Privacy, Charging Order, and Series Structure

Charging Order Protection

Nevada's LLC charging order protection is one of the strongest in the country — and it's codified by statute rather than case law. A "charging order" is the only remedy creditors have against an LLC member's interest: they can receive distributions if the LLC makes them, but they cannot force distributions, take management control, or reach the underlying LLC assets (including Bitcoin).

In practice, this creates a powerful standoff: a creditor who obtains a charging order gets the right to receive any distributions your LLC makes. Since you control the LLC and can simply not make distributions, the charging order can be economically worthless to the creditor. Many creditors choose to settle rather than hold a charging order against a well-structured Nevada LLC.

Nevada LLC Privacy

Nevada does not require disclosure of beneficial ownership in public filings. Your Nevada LLC can list a registered agent and a manager — without publicly identifying the member/owner. This provides a layer of privacy that states like California (which require member disclosure) do not offer.

For Bitcoin families who prioritize privacy about their holdings, Nevada LLC formation through a nominee manager arrangement can keep your name entirely out of public records. This doesn't eliminate your tax obligations, but it prevents casual discovery of your Bitcoin holdings through public business filings.

Nevada Series LLC

Nevada's Series LLC allows a single LLC to have multiple "cells" or "series" with segregated assets and liabilities. Each series can hold different Bitcoin UTXOs or asset types, with liability isolation between them. A creditor of one series cannot reach assets in another series.

For Bitcoin families with: multiple Bitcoin positions purchased at different times (and different basis), some BTC in self-custody and some in custody with an institutional custodian, or Bitcoin alongside real estate or other assets — the Series LLC allows all to be held within a single legal entity while maintaining liability isolation between the cells.

Setting Up a Nevada Bitcoin Family Office Structure

Step 1: Nevada LLC Formation

  • File Articles of Organization with Nevada Secretary of State: $75
  • Annual fees: $200/year (lowest of the major asset protection states)
  • Registered agent in Nevada: $50–$200/year
  • Operating Agreement: should include specific provisions for digital asset custody, multi-sig key management, and succession of membership interest

Step 2: Nevada DAPT (if asset protection is the primary goal)

  • Engage Nevada trust attorney for DAPT drafting: $8,000–$20,000
  • Select independent Nevada trustee (individual or licensed trust company)
  • Transfer Bitcoin to DAPT — establishes the 2-year seasoning clock
  • DAPT owns the Nevada LLC (the LLC is the operational holding vehicle; the DAPT provides the protective wrapper)

Step 3: Wyoming Dynasty Trust (for multi-generational transfer)

For families who want both current-generation protection AND perpetual dynasty planning, the optimal structure is:

  • Nevada LLC (operational layer) — privacy, charging order protection
  • Wyoming Dynasty Trust (ownership layer) — owns the Nevada LLC; perpetual; estate tax removal
  • Nevada DAPT (optional) — if you want self-settled protection during your lifetime, a separate DAPT can hold a portion of the position

Who Should Choose Nevada Over Wyoming?

The Nevada structure makes the most sense for Bitcoin families in these situations:

  • High creditor risk: Business owners, physicians, attorneys, real estate developers, or anyone with significant lawsuit exposure who wants to protect Bitcoin from professional liability
  • Active litigation or near-term creditor risk: If a lawsuit is pending or threatened, the 2-year seasoning clock needs to start as soon as possible
  • Privacy as a priority: Nevada's LLC privacy provisions are the strongest of any major asset protection state
  • Current-generation access needed: The DAPT structure allows you to remain a beneficiary — Wyoming's primary structure removes you as a beneficiary more definitively
  • Nevada residents: If you live in Nevada, using Nevada structures avoids the complexity of out-of-state trust administration

Nevada Bitcoin Estate Planning Considerations

While Nevada excels at asset protection, there are specific estate planning considerations to address:

Nevada Uniform Trust Code

Nevada adopted the UTC with significant modifications — notably the ability to create silent trusts (no duty to inform beneficiaries), perpetual trusts, and directed trusts. The "quiet trust" provision is particularly useful for Bitcoin families who want to establish trusts for children without triggering early distributions or creating management challenges before heirs are ready.

Bitcoin Succession in Nevada Trusts

Nevada trust law has no specific provisions for digital assets beyond the general RUFADAA adoption (Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act). Your trust document should explicitly address: who holds multi-sig keys, what constitutes "access" to Bitcoin under the directed trust, and the succession protocol when a key holder is incapacitated or dies.

Nevada Community Property

Nevada is a community property state — Bitcoin acquired during marriage is generally community property. This provides the double step-up in basis advantage at death (both spouses' share gets a new basis), but also means pre-nuptial agreements are important for Bitcoin holders who want to maintain separate property status for pre-marriage holdings. FAQ: Bitcoin Family Office in Nevada

What makes Nevada good for a Bitcoin family office?
Nevada offers: no state income tax, no state estate or inheritance tax, one of the strongest Domestic Asset Protection Trust laws in the US, strong LLC charging order protection codified by statute, Series LLC for segmented holdings, and significant privacy advantages. For Bitcoin families focused on asset protection alongside estate planning, Nevada is often the strongest jurisdiction for current-generation protection.
What is a Nevada Domestic Asset Protection Trust?
A Nevada DAPT (also called a Self-Settled Spendthrift Trust) allows you to be both the grantor and a discretionary beneficiary of the trust, while protecting trust assets from your creditors after a 2-year seasoning period. This is powerful because you transfer Bitcoin to the trust for asset protection but retain potential economic access as a beneficiary. Most states don't allow the grantor to be a beneficiary of an asset protection trust.
Nevada vs. Wyoming — which is better for Bitcoin?
It depends on your priority. Wyoming is better for perpetual dynasty trusts (stronger directed trust statute, more Bitcoin trust company infrastructure). Nevada is better for current-generation asset protection (DAPT law is excellent, LLC charging order protection is codified, privacy is stronger). The optimal structure for many large Bitcoin family offices: Nevada LLC (operational layer) + Wyoming dynasty trust (long-term holding). The two structures complement each other rather than compete.
Do I need to live in Nevada to use a Nevada LLC or trust?
No. Nevada LLCs and trusts are available to residents of any state. You need a Nevada registered agent ($50–$200/year). For a Nevada trust, you need either an individual Nevada resident trustee or a licensed Nevada trust company to administer the trust under Nevada law. You can be a beneficiary regardless of where you live.
How does Nevada LLC charging order protection work for Bitcoin?
A creditor who obtains a charging order against your Nevada LLC membership interest can receive distributions IF the LLC makes them — but cannot force distributions, take management control, or reach the underlying Bitcoin assets. Since you control the LLC as manager and can choose not to make distributions, the charging order is often economically worthless to the creditor. Many creditors settle rather than hold a charging order against a properly structured Nevada LLC.
What is the cost of setting up a Nevada Bitcoin family office structure?
Nevada LLC: $75 formation + $200/year + registered agent ($50–$200). Nevada DAPT: $8,000–$20,000 for trust drafting + trustee fees (0.25–0.75% AUM/year). Nevada attorney fees for Bitcoin-specific provisions: $5,000–$15,000 additional. Total first-year setup for LLC + DAPT: $15,000–$40,000. Annual ongoing: $5,000–$20,000 depending on trustee fee structure and complexity.
