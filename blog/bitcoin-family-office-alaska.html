Bitcoin Family Office in Alaska: Zero Income Tax, No Estate Tax, and the Only State That Pays You to Live There

Alaska is the only state in America that has no income tax, no estate tax, no inheritance tax, no sales tax at the state level — and pays every resident an annual dividend from its sovereign wealth fund. The Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend: $1,312 per person in 2023, $1,702 in 2022, historically ranging from $330 to $2,072. For a Bitcoin family of four, Alaska sends you a check every fall while charging you nothing on Bitcoin gains. The tax profile is among the cleanest in the country. The planning challenge is practical: Alaska is remote, the advisory ecosystem is thin, and establishing genuine Alaska domicile requires real commitment. For those willing to make that commitment — or who already live there — Alaska is an underappreciated A-tier Bitcoin family planning state.

Alaska's Tax Profile: The Cleanest Large State in America

No State Income Tax — Ever

Alaska has never imposed a state income tax on individuals. The state abolished its personal income tax in 1980, when the Trans-Alaska Pipeline System (TAPS) began generating oil revenue sufficient to fund state government without income taxation. Alaska has operated without a personal income tax for over 45 years — longer than most Bitcoin holders have been alive.

For a Bitcoin holder realizing a gain in Alaska, the combined long-term capital gains rate is exactly the federal rate: 20% + 3.8% NIIT = 23.8%. No state layer. No city layer. No surcharge. The same rate as Wyoming, Nevada, Tennessee, and Florida — the states usually cited as Bitcoin tax havens. Alaska belongs in that same tier and almost never appears in those conversations.

The Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend: The World's Only Universal Basic Income from a Sovereign Wealth Fund

The Alaska Permanent Fund was established by constitutional amendment in 1976. It receives at least 25% of all oil and mineral revenues received by the state and invests them in a diversified portfolio of global assets. As of 2025, the Fund held over $80 billion in assets — making it one of the largest sovereign wealth funds in the world on a per-capita basis.

Each year, a dividend is paid to every Alaska resident who has lived in the state for a full calendar year and intends to remain indefinitely. Recent dividend history:

Bitcoin's 21-million-coin fixed supply resonates with this framework in a way it doesn't in states with pure fiat finance cultures.

No Estate Tax, No Inheritance Tax, No City Income Tax

Alaska has no estate tax (the state pickup tax expired in 2005 and was never replaced), no inheritance tax, and no municipal income taxes. Anchorage, Fairbanks, Juneau, and Sitka do not levy income taxes. The death tax picture is completely clean — Bitcoin passes to heirs with no state-level death tax friction, regardless of estate size.

Alaska Wealth Hubs

Anchorage: The Commercial and Professional Hub

Anchorage is home to roughly 40% of Alaska's ~730,000 residents and nearly all of its corporate headquarters, financial services, legal infrastructure, and professional wealth. The Anchorage wealth profile is concentrated in:

  • Oil and gas: BP, ConocoPhillips, and Hilcorp have major Alaska operations managed from Anchorage. Oil industry executives, engineers, and contractors who have spent careers in Alaska's oil sector have accumulated significant wealth — often concentrated in equity compensation from oil companies, real estate, and increasingly Bitcoin as a hedge against the oil cycle and monetary debasement.
  • Native Alaskan corporations: The Alaska Native Claims Settlement Act (1971) created 12 regional and over 200 village corporations, giving Alaska Natives ownership of 44 million acres and $962.5 million in initial funding. The largest Native corporations — CIRI (Cook Inlet Region), NANA Regional Corporation, Doyon Limited, Ahtna — have grown into diversified businesses with revenues in the billions. Native corporation executives managing billion-dollar portfolios have real family office planning needs that almost no advisory firm in the Lower 48 is addressing.
  • Healthcare and government: Providence Health & Services, Alaska Native Tribal Health Consortium, and the state government create a large professional class of physicians, executives, and administrators with meaningful W-2 income and growing Bitcoin positions.
  • Real estate and construction: Alaska's construction boom — driven by military bases (Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson, Fort Wainwright), infrastructure projects, and remote access needs — has created significant business owner wealth in the construction, logistics, and real estate sectors.

Kenai Peninsula and Matanuska-Susitna Valley: Resource Wealth and Self-Reliance

Outside Anchorage, Alaska's wealth is resource-based and profoundly self-reliant in character. The Kenai Peninsula — home to commercial fishing (salmon, halibut, crab), oil production (Kenai fields), and tourism — creates a wealth class of fishing business owners, oil workers, and lodge operators whose economic philosophy closely mirrors Bitcoin's self-sovereignty narrative. People who survive Alaskan winters, operate commercial fishing vessels, and build businesses in remote locations without municipal support develop a deeply practical, self-reliant worldview that aligns naturally with Bitcoin's trustless, self-custody model.

Military Wealth: The Permanent Population Anchor

Alaska has a larger active-duty military presence per capita than almost any other state. Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson (Anchorage) and Fort Wainwright (Fairbanks) create a substantial, stable population of military personnel and their families who often remain in Alaska after service. Military retirees who established Alaska residency during service and accumulated TSP (Thrift Savings Plan) wealth alongside Bitcoin have genuine planning needs — particularly around the Servicemembers' Civil Relief Act (SCRA) and military-specific tax rules — that Alaska-knowledgeable advisors serve well.

Alaska Trust Law: The DAPT Pioneer

Alaska was the first state in the United States to enact a Domestic Asset Protection Trust statute — in 1997, the same year as Delaware. Alaska's DAPT law allows a self-settled trust (where the grantor is also a beneficiary) to protect assets from future creditors after a relatively brief seasoning period.

Alaska trust law key features:

  • DAPT: Alaska DAPT requires a 4-year seasoning period for full protection against pre-existing creditors (2 years for transfers not fraudulent as to existing creditors). The grantor can be a discretionary beneficiary and retain certain limited powers.
  • Dynasty trust duration: Alaska allows perpetual trusts — no Rule Against Perpetuities for trusts that opt into Alaska's perpetual trust statute.
  • Trust income tax: Alaska has no state income tax, including on trust income. An Alaska-sited trust with an Alaska trustee pays $0 Alaska income tax on accumulated gains. This is a material advantage over states like California and New York that impose trust income tax based on trustee residence or beneficiary residence.
  • Directed trusts: Alaska's directed trust statute allows full separation of investment and distribution functions, enabling a Bitcoin holder to retain investment control as Investment Trust Director while an Alaska corporate trustee holds legal title.
Alaska vs South Dakota for Trust Situs: Alaska and South Dakota are the two original DAPT jurisdictions (both enacted in 1997). South Dakota has generally become the preferred institutional choice due to its deeper corporate trustee infrastructure, lower trust tax (SD also has 0% trust income tax), and more active legislative refinement of trust law. Alaska remains a strong alternative — particularly for Alaskan residents who prefer in-state trustee oversight or who want to leverage Alaska's 0% income tax at both the personal and trust level simultaneously.

The Alaska Domicile Decision: Who Should Seriously Consider It

Category 1: Already Living in Alaska

Alaska residents with significant Bitcoin positions are already in one of the best possible tax jurisdictions. The planning priority is not domicile change but structure: WY LLC (or Alaska LLC) + Alaska perpetual trust (or South Dakota trust) + annual gifting. Many Alaska residents are underplanning because they assume their tax situation requires no action — the 0% income tax is already priced in. What they miss: federal estate tax still applies, the trust structure is still necessary, and the planning urgency is real even without a state income tax layer.

Category 2: Remote Workers With Location Flexibility

The post-2020 remote work economy has made Alaska domicile genuinely viable for knowledge workers who previously could not have lived there. A software engineer earning $200,000/year from a Seattle or San Francisco company who relocates to Anchorage or the Kenai Peninsula:

  • Pays $0 Alaska income tax vs. $18,000–$26,600 in Washington state or California
  • Receives $1,300–$1,700/year PFD
  • Lives at significantly lower cost than any West Coast metro
  • Accumulates Bitcoin at a faster after-tax rate than in virtually any other state

The lifestyle tradeoff is real — Alaska winters, remoteness, limited cultural infrastructure — but for a specific type of Bitcoin-oriented, self-reliant, outdoors-focused professional, Alaska is the best domicile in America by the numbers.

Category 3: Domicile Migration for Pre-Liquidity Planning

For high-income professionals in California, New York, or Oregon who are planning a large Bitcoin realization event and are willing to genuinely establish Alaska domicile before that event, Alaska's 0% income tax produces the same savings as Wyoming or Nevada. On a $10M gain: $1,330,000 in California tax savings; $990,000 in New York savings; $990,000 in Oregon savings. The domicile change to Alaska rather than Nevada or Wyoming requires the same genuine residency establishment — primary home, driver's license, voter registration, physical presence — but Alaska's lifestyle is dramatically different from Nevada's. The savings are identical; the motivation must be lifestyle-genuine.

10-Item Alaska Bitcoin Planning Checklist

  • If already Alaska resident: confirm domicile documentation is current (AK driver's license, AK voter registration, primary home in AK) — particularly important if you spend significant time outside Alaska for work or family
  • Apply for Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend every year — requires annual application; missing a year means losing that year's dividend (no retroactive claims)
  • Form Wyoming LLC (or Alaska LLC) to hold Bitcoin — WY charging order protection slightly stronger than AK; both work; use whichever state your attorney recommends given your other planning considerations
  • Consider Alaska perpetual dynasty trust with Alaska corporate trustee — Alaska 0% trust income tax + perpetual duration + DAPT; particularly attractive for Alaska residents who want in-state trust administration and prefer not to use South Dakota trustees
  • Alternatively: South Dakota dynasty trust with SD trustee — SD's deeper institutional trust infrastructure and shorter DAPT seasoning (2yr vs 4yr) make SD attractive even for Alaska residents; the 0% trust income tax result is identical
  • Launch annual exclusion gifting — $19K/recipient, no Alaska gift tax; federal estate tax still applies to Alaska residents; gifting is still the primary tool for reducing federal taxable estate
  • Model GRAT for next Bitcoin correction — Alaska's 0% income tax means GRATs are purely federal estate tax tools; valuable for estates approaching the $13.61M federal threshold
  • If Native Alaska corporation executive or board member: seek counsel familiar with the intersection of ANCSA corporation equity, tribal corporation governance, and Bitcoin estate planning — this is a niche with almost no existing advisory resources
  • Update estate plan for digital asset provisions — Alaska has enacted digital asset trust authority; confirm your estate plan includes hardware wallet successor protocols and multi-sig key distribution
  • If considering Alaska domicile from a high-tax state: establish AK primary residence, AK driver's license, AK voter registration, and at least 183 days AK presence in the migration year before realizing any Bitcoin gains — California FTB and NY DTA are the most aggressive states for challenging domicile changes; Alaska domicile must be genuine
