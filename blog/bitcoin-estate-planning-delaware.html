Bitcoin Estate Planning in Delaware

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in Delaware Est. 9 min read

Delaware is the most famous business-formation jurisdiction in the United States. More than two-thirds of Fortune 500 companies are incorporated in Delaware, and the Delaware LLC is one of the most widely used business structures in America. When Bitcoin families hear "Delaware," they often assume it must be the optimal jurisdiction for all planning structures — including trusts. This assumption is wrong, and it can lead to costly misdirection.

Delaware has no state estate tax and no inheritance tax — an excellent baseline for Bitcoin families domiciled in the First State. Delaware's corporation and LLC law is world-class. Its Court of Chancery — a specialized equity court with sophisticated judges experienced in business law — is among the most respected courts in the country. But for multigenerational Bitcoin trusts, Wyoming and South Dakota are materially superior jurisdictions. This guide explains why, covers what Delaware does well, and outlines the estate planning framework appropriate for Delaware Bitcoin families.

Delaware's Tax Baseline for Bitcoin Families

Delaware imposes no state estate tax and no inheritance tax. Delaware-domiciled Bitcoin holders who die leaving Bitcoin to heirs face no state-level death tax — only the federal estate tax for estates above the federal exemption ($13.99 million per individual in 2025). This places Delaware in the favorable tier of no-estate-tax states alongside Florida, Texas, Nevada, and Wyoming.

Delaware does impose a state income tax, with rates up to 6.6% on taxable income, including capital gains. Bitcoin sales and exchanges are taxable events subject to Delaware income tax in addition to federal capital gains tax. The stepped-up cost basis at death remains a valuable planning tool for Delaware Bitcoin holders holding positions with significant embedded appreciation.

Delaware LLCs and Corporations: What They Do Well

Delaware's LLC Act (6 Del. C. § 18-101 et seq.) and corporation law (Delaware General Corporation Law, 8 Del. C.) are extensively litigated, deeply interpreted by sophisticated courts, and widely understood by legal and financial professionals nationwide. For a Bitcoin holding company that may eventually seek institutional investment, issue membership interests to employees, or be involved in commercial transactions with parties who require a familiar jurisdiction, Delaware is a sound choice. Specific Delaware LLC advantages include:

  • Series LLCs: Delaware allows the creation of separate "series" within a single LLC — each with its own assets, liabilities, and members — without forming separate entities. For a family holding multiple Bitcoin wallets or multiple investment accounts, a Delaware Series LLC can create legal segregation between different pools of assets within one entity structure.
  • Charging order protection: Delaware LLC interests are protected by charging order, limiting creditors to distributions rather than allowing seizure of the underlying Bitcoin.
  • Court of Chancery: Disputes involving Delaware LLCs are resolved in the Court of Chancery — a court with no jury, run by chancellors (judges) who specialize in business and equity law. This is materially superior to general civil courts for complex governance disputes involving Bitcoin custody or succession.
  • Delaware Statutory Trust (DST): The Delaware Statutory Trust is a separate structure from a traditional trust — used primarily for real estate syndication and 1031 exchange purposes. Bitcoin families should not conflate a DST with a traditional revocable or irrevocable trust for estate planning; they are fundamentally different instruments designed for different purposes.
Delaware is the gold standard for LLCs and corporations. But for multigenerational Bitcoin trusts — dynasty trusts, directed trusts, asset protection trusts — Wyoming and South Dakota are materially superior. Know which instrument you're choosing, and choose the right jurisdiction for each.

Why Wyoming and South Dakota Are Superior for Bitcoin Trusts

For multigenerational irrevocable trusts holding Bitcoin, three factors determine which state offers the best situs: directed trust legislation, asset protection trust provisions, and perpetual trust authority. Delaware trails Wyoming and South Dakota on all three:

Directed trusts: Wyoming's Uniform Trust Code and directed trust statute allow extensive separation of investment direction from administrative trustee functions, with clear statutory exculpation for each role. Delaware allows some directed trust arrangements, but Wyoming's statutory framework is more explicit, more complete, and more extensively developed for Bitcoin custody structures where a specialized investment advisor or custodian directs all custody decisions independently of the administrative trustee.

Domestic asset protection trusts (DAPTs): Wyoming permits self-settled asset protection trusts — where the grantor is also a discretionary beneficiary — after a relatively short seasoning period, with strong creditor protection. Delaware enacted a DAPT statute but with a longer seasoning period (four years) and less-tested creditor protection than Wyoming's structure. South Dakota's DAPT statute is also competitive with Wyoming.

Perpetual trusts and dynasty trust authority: Wyoming abolished the rule against perpetuities for trusts governed by Wyoming law, permitting perpetual dynasty trusts. Delaware allows trusts lasting up to 110 years for personal property (360 years for real property) under its "wait and see" rule against perpetuities reform — but this is not perpetual. For families building a Bitcoin dynasty trust intended to hold for three or more generations across 100+ years, Wyoming's perpetual trust authority is materially superior.

Trust income taxation: Delaware taxes trust income where Delaware-resident beneficiaries receive distributions. Non-Delaware trust income accumulation may be exempt from Delaware income tax in many structures. Wyoming has no state income tax at all — trusts sited in Wyoming with no Wyoming-resident beneficiaries pay no state income tax on accumulated trust income.

The Right Use of Delaware: LLCs Holding Bitcoin, Governed by Sophisticated Courts

Delaware's strength for Bitcoin families lies in the holding company layer, not the trust layer. A Delaware LLC holding Bitcoin — whether owned by the grantor directly, held in a Wyoming trust, or used as a joint venture vehicle between family members — benefits from Delaware's well-understood LLC statute and Court of Chancery dispute resolution. Specific appropriate uses include:

  • A Delaware LLC owned by a Wyoming trust — using Delaware's LLC infrastructure for the operating/holding company and Wyoming's trust infrastructure for the ownership and succession layer above it.
  • A Delaware LLC used as a venture or operating entity for Bitcoin-related business operations, where investors or counterparties require a Delaware entity.
  • A Delaware Series LLC where a family segregates distinct Bitcoin pools or investment mandates within one legal entity.

Estate Planning Framework for Delaware-Domiciled Bitcoin Families

For Bitcoin families domiciled in Delaware — not just incorporating there — the estate planning framework follows the no-estate-tax playbook with Wyoming trust situs as the preferred vehicle for multigenerational holding:

  1. Establish a Wyoming-sited dynasty trust administered by a Wyoming corporate trustee, for the benefit of Delaware-resident beneficiaries. Transfer Bitcoin or LLC interests holding Bitcoin to the trust during the grantor's lifetime using the federal gift tax exemption. Wyoming's perpetual trust authority, directed trust provisions, and no-income-tax environment are superior to Delaware for this layer.
  2. Hold Bitcoin in a Delaware or Wyoming LLC owned by the Wyoming trust — using whichever LLC jurisdiction is optimal for the family's operational and relational needs. For purely family holding with no outside investors, Wyoming LLC is equally suitable. For LLCs with potential institutional relationships, Delaware LLC is more recognized.
  3. Use federal transfer strategies — GRATs, SLATs, annual exclusion gifting, CRTs — aggressively, since no Delaware estate tax creates an urgency asymmetry in favor of lifetime giving before potential federal exemption reduction after 2025.
  4. Document Bitcoin custody succession explicitly — Delaware adopted RUFADAA, providing fiduciary access authority, but technical key succession must be addressed in a separate succession protocol coordinated with your multi-signature architecture.
