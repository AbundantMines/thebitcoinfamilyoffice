North Dakota's 2023 Tax Reform: The Rate Nobody Noticed

In 2023, the North Dakota Legislature passed sweeping income tax reform — reducing rates, simplifying brackets, and establishing a flat rate structure that puts North Dakota among the most competitive income tax states in the country.

The result: North Dakota's top individual income tax rate is now 2.5% — a flat rate that applies to higher-income filers, with lower rates (or zero rate) for individuals below certain income thresholds. For a Bitcoin holder realizing a significant gain — which puts them well into the higher income brackets — North Dakota's 2.5% flat rate is the effective rate.

Combined long-term capital gains rate for a North Dakota resident: 20% + 3.8% + 2.5% = 26.3%.

North Dakota Wealth Hubs

The Bakken Formation: America's Domestic Oil Boom

The Bakken Shale formation — centered in western North Dakota (Williston Basin) and extending into Montana and Saskatchewan — was one of the most significant domestic oil discoveries in American history. The combination of horizontal drilling and hydraulic fracturing unlocked crude oil production that transformed North Dakota from a negligible oil producer to the second-largest oil-producing state in the US (behind Texas) at the peak of the boom in 2014.

The Bakken created enormous wealth across multiple vectors:

  • Mineral rights royalties: North Dakota landowners sitting on Bakken mineral rights received royalty checks that, in many cases, exceeded the total value of their farmland at pre-boom prices. Families that had held mineral rights for generations suddenly had liquid wealth that required sophisticated planning — often for the first time.
  • Oil field services: Williston, Watford City, Dickinson, and Minot became boomtowns. Oil field services companies, equipment operators, pipeline contractors, and support businesses created a new class of business owners with significant capital events when they sold or went public.
  • Land appreciation: Surface land values in western North Dakota appreciated dramatically as the oil boom brought infrastructure, population, and economic activity to previously remote agricultural areas.

The Bakken boom wealth is now in its second phase: the initial liquidity events have occurred, the windfall recipients have had 10+ years to compound wealth, and they are now thinking about passing that wealth to the next generation. Bitcoin has become a meaningful part of this conversation — particularly for Bakken landowners who understand scarce natural resources and have direct experience with how government policy and monetary debasement can affect commodity-denominated wealth.

Fargo: The Upper Midwest's Tech Corridor

Fargo is the economic center of the Upper Midwest — the largest city in North Dakota and one of the most economically dynamic metros in the region. Fargo's tech economy is anchored by several significant presences:

  • Microsoft: Significant Fargo operations — development, cloud services, and back-office functions. Microsoft's Fargo presence creates a professional class of software engineers and product managers earning Seattle-equivalent compensation in a North Dakota cost-of-living environment. The wealth accumulation velocity — high tech income, low housing costs, 2.5% income tax — is among the best in the country for Bitcoin accumulation on an after-tax basis.
  • Amazon: Fargo is home to Amazon fulfillment and data center operations, with the Amazon Web Services (AWS) regional infrastructure that supports northern Plains businesses.
  • NDSU Research & Technology Park: North Dakota State University's tech commercialization engine has spun out agricultural technology, biotech, and software companies that create a steady stream of liquidity events and equity compensation for Fargo's tech professional class.
  • Sanford Health / Essentia Health: The Fargo healthcare system creates a substantial professional wealth class — physicians, hospital executives, and healthcare technology entrepreneurs whose equity and practice valuations create planning needs that intersect directly with Bitcoin.
  • Farm Credit Services of America: Omaha-headquartered but with deep Fargo ties — providing financial services to agricultural businesses throughout the Great Plains and creating an Fargo-adjacent professional wealth class focused on agriculture and land finance.

Agricultural Land Wealth: The Compounding Acre

North Dakota is one of the top five agricultural producing states in the country — the largest producer of sunflowers, durum wheat, canola, dry peas, lentils, flaxseed, and honey, and a major producer of spring wheat, corn, and soybeans. The agricultural wealth in North Dakota is real, multi-generational, and increasingly liquid as farmland values have reached record levels.

North Dakota farmland averaged over $3,000 per acre as of 2024 — up from under $1,000 per acre in 2000. For families holding 2,000–5,000 acres (common for established North Dakota farm families), this represents $6M–$15M in appreciated agricultural assets, often with low cost basis from multi-generational holding. The estate planning intersection with Bitcoin: farm families who are simultaneously dealing with highly appreciated land, Bitcoin positions accumulated from oil royalties, and the need to pass both to the next generation without triggering massive capital gains or inheritance disruption.

Bismarck: State Government and Energy Regulation Wealth

Bismarck, as North Dakota's state capital, houses state government, energy regulatory agencies, and the financial services companies that support North Dakota's energy and agricultural sectors. The wealth here is more conservative and government-adjacent than Fargo's tech wealth — but it intersects with Bitcoin in a specific way: North Dakota has been one of the more Bitcoin-friendly state regulatory environments, and policymakers in Bismarck have been engaged with Bitcoin's monetary policy implications in ways that other state capitals have not.

North Dakota Trust Law

North Dakota adopted the Uniform Trust Code (the North Dakota Uniform Trust Code, Chapter 59-12 et seq.). The trust framework is modern and supports directed trusts, decanting, and trust protector provisions.

North Dakota has a modified Rule Against Perpetuities — the state has enacted a 1,000-year limit on trust duration for certain trusts, which is longer than the traditional common law RAP (21 years after lives in being) but shorter than South Dakota's and Wyoming's perpetual trust statutes. A 1,000-year trust is functionally a dynasty trust for most planning purposes, though the legal perpetual-trust designation belongs to South Dakota and a small number of other states.

North Dakota does not have a Domestic Asset Protection Trust statute. For maximum creditor protection and perpetual dynasty trust flexibility, North Dakota Bitcoin holders use South Dakota as the preferred trust situs — particularly compelling given that Sioux Falls (SD) is one of the closest major financial centers to Fargo and is the premier trust jurisdiction in the country.

North Dakota Bitcoin Planning: The WY LLC + SD Trust Architecture

The standard architecture applies directly:

  • Wyoming LLC — holds the Bitcoin position. Wyoming and North Dakota share a border; the adjacent-state simplicity is the same as the Iowa–South Dakota relationship. Wyoming charging order protection, zero Wyoming income tax at the entity level.
  • South Dakota dynasty trust — owns the Wyoming LLC. Perpetual duration (vs. ND's 1,000-year limit), 0% trust income tax on accumulated gains (SD has no trust income tax on non-South-Dakota-resident beneficiaries), DAPT creditor protection with a 2-year seasoning period. SD is 4 hours from Fargo.
  • Annual exclusion gifting — $19,000/$38,000 per recipient per year. No North Dakota gift tax. The 2.5% income tax makes every dollar of unrealized gain that can be gifted before realization more valuable than in a high-rate state, but the 2.5% rate also means the urgency is lower than for a Wisconsin or Minnesota holder.

North Dakota's 2.5% flat rate means the absolute income tax savings from a domicile change to Wyoming or South Dakota are smaller than from most other income-tax states — $125,000 on a $5M gain vs. $0 in Wyoming. For many North Dakota residents with strong ties to the state, the lifestyle disruption of a domicile change to Wyoming is not worth $125,000 in state income tax savings. The in-state structure (WY LLC + SD trust) is often the more practical primary strategy.

10-Item North Dakota Bitcoin Planning Checklist

  • Verify current North Dakota income tax rate and bracket thresholds — 2.5% is the legislated flat rate post-2023 reform; confirm the applicable rate for your income level with an ND-licensed CPA
  • If considering domicile change to Wyoming or South Dakota for income tax savings: calculate the math — 2.5% gap is smaller than most states (saves $125K on $5M, $250K on $10M); weigh against lifestyle disruption
  • Form Wyoming LLC to hold Bitcoin — adjacent state (WY borders ND), optimal LLC jurisdiction, charging order protection
  • Establish South Dakota dynasty trust — perpetual (vs ND's 1,000-year), 0% trust income tax on accumulated gains (no SD income tax on non-resident beneficiary trusts), DAPT protection; Sioux Falls is 4 hours from Fargo
  • If Bakken mineral rights holder: coordinate oil royalty income and Bitcoin gains in trust structure — both types of resource wealth compound efficiently in a SD dynasty trust with 0% trust income tax
  • Launch annual exclusion gifting — $19K/recipient, no ND gift tax; at 2.5% ND rate, the income tax savings from gifting are smaller than high-rate states, but the federal estate tax benefit is identical
  • Evaluate agricultural land + Bitcoin coordination — if you hold appreciated farmland and Bitcoin simultaneously, a SD dynasty trust can hold both asset classes under one perpetual structure with a unified succession plan
  • Model GRAT for next Bitcoin correction — useful for any estate approaching the federal $13.61M threshold; ND's no-estate-tax profile means GRATs are federal-only planning tools here
  • Update estate plan for digital asset provisions — ND Bitcoin holders often work with Bismarck or Fargo generalist attorneys who may not have Bitcoin-specific trustee authority language; ensure your plan includes hardware wallet successor protocols and multi-sig key distribution
  • Seek North Dakota–aware Bitcoin counsel — most sophisticated Bitcoin estate planning firms are in New York, Texas, and the coasts; verify that your advisor understands North Dakota's specific trust statute (1,000-year vs. perpetual), the ND–SD planning corridor, and North Dakota's favorable income tax environment
