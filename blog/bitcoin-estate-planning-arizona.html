Bitcoin Estate Planning in Arizona

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Article Est. 8 min read

Arizona has become one of the more attractive Sun Belt states for Bitcoin families — particularly those relocating from California. With a flat 2.5% income tax (one of the lowest in any state with an income tax), no estate tax, a modernized Uniform Trust Code, community property rules that provide a double step-up at first death, and a growing ecosystem of Bitcoin-aware professionals in the Phoenix and Scottsdale metro areas, Arizona offers a genuinely favorable planning environment for Bitcoin families who want a lower-cost alternative to Nevada or Wyoming without giving up western U.S. proximity.

Arizona's Flat 2.5% Income Tax

Arizona implemented a flat 2.5% personal income tax rate effective January 1, 2023, following the passage of Proposition 132 in 2022 — consolidating what had been a multi-bracket system with a top rate of 4.5%. The flat rate applies to all taxable income, including capital gains on Bitcoin. There is no Arizona capital gains preference: long-term gains are taxed the same as short-term gains at 2.5%.

The practical advantage over other states is significant. Compared to California (13.3%), New York (10.9%), Oregon (9.9%), or even Colorado (4.4%), Arizona's 2.5% rate is minimal. The combined federal and Arizona effective rate on long-term Bitcoin capital gains for a high-income taxpayer is approximately 26.3% (23.8% federal + 2.5% Arizona) — one of the lowest combined rates of any state with an income tax in the country.

Arizona's flat tax rate is established by statute rather than constitutional provision, which means future legislatures could theoretically modify it. However, Proposition 132 requires future income tax rate increases to receive 60% voter approval — a meaningful structural constraint on upward rate movement.

No Arizona Estate Tax

Arizona does not impose a state estate or inheritance tax. Like Texas, Florida, and Colorado, Arizona's estate tax was tied to the federal state death tax credit (IRC §2011), which was phased out after 2001. Arizona has not enacted a standalone estate tax since, and there is no current political movement to do so. Arizona Bitcoin families face only the federal estate tax at the $13.99 million exemption threshold — no additional state layer.

The absence of a state estate tax is particularly valuable given Arizona's large retiree population. Many Bitcoin holders who acquired positions early in life and have held them into retirement accumulate significant appreciated positions that become estate tax issues late in life. In Arizona, the entire estate tax burden is federal — and federal tax planning tools (GRATs, SLATs, dynasty trusts, annual exclusion gifting) are fully available without a separate state overlay to navigate.

Arizona Uniform Trust Code

Arizona adopted the Uniform Trust Code in 2009 (Arizona Revised Statutes §14-10101 et seq.), providing a modernized statutory framework for trust creation, administration, modification, and dispute resolution. Key provisions relevant to Bitcoin families include:

  • Directed trust provisions (ARS §14-10807): Arizona's UTC permits trust instruments to grant investment direction authority to parties other than the trustee. A Bitcoin trust can designate a family investment advisor or custody specialist to direct all Bitcoin investment and custody decisions, while the trustee handles administrative functions. The directed trustee is protected from liability for following directions of the investment advisor absent clear abuse.
  • Spendthrift protection (ARS §14-10502): Arizona permits robust spendthrift provisions that prevent beneficiaries from assigning their interests and restrict creditor access to trust assets before distribution. For Bitcoin trusts designed to protect multi-generational wealth, Arizona's spendthrift provisions provide solid statutory foundation.
  • Trust decanting (ARS §14-10819): Arizona permits trust decanting — moving assets from an existing trust to a new trust with updated terms — under defined conditions. For Bitcoin trusts with outdated custody provisions, decanting provides a pathway to modernize without triggering tax consequences.
  • Trust modification (ARS §14-10410–14-10416): Arizona allows trusts to be modified by court approval, non-judicial settlement agreements, or trustee consent in defined circumstances — providing flexibility to update Bitcoin trust structures as law and technology evolve.
Arizona's 2.5% flat income tax makes it one of the most cost-effective states for Bitcoin families who want Sun Belt proximity without Nevada or Wyoming's zero-tax jurisdiction status.

Community Property: The Double Step-Up Advantage

Arizona is a community property state (Arizona Revised Statutes §25-211). Bitcoin purchased with marital funds during marriage is community property, owned equally by both spouses regardless of whose name appears on the account or hardware wallet. This creates the same double step-up opportunity as Texas, California, and Washington: at the first spouse's death, both halves of the community estate receive a stepped-up basis to fair market value under IRC §1014(b)(6).

For Arizona couples with large, long-held Bitcoin positions, the community property step-up can be enormously valuable — eliminating decades of unrealized capital gains at the first death and giving the surviving spouse a full stepped-up basis in the entire position. This benefit must be weighed against the estate planning advantages of irrevocable trusts (which typically forfeit the step-up for assets transferred before death).

Arizona couples should document the community property character of Bitcoin holdings and ensure their custody arrangements and estate planning documents reflect the equal ownership of both spouses in community Bitcoin. A Bitcoin wallet held only in one spouse's name does not make the Bitcoin that spouse's separate property if it was purchased with community funds — but ambiguity in the records can create probate complications. A properly drafted community property agreement or marital settlement agreement can clarify ownership characterization.

Arizona LLCs for Bitcoin Holding

Arizona LLCs (Arizona Revised Statutes Chapter 29) provide standard charging order protection for multi-member entities. For Bitcoin families seeking strong single-member LLC protection, Arizona law is less clear than Wyoming or Nevada — the Arizona Supreme Court has not definitively ruled on whether charging order protection extends to single-member LLCs, and practitioners generally recommend multi-member structures for maximum protection.

A practical approach: form the Bitcoin holding LLC in Wyoming or Nevada for superior creditor protection and structural flexibility, then register it as a foreign LLC in Arizona if the family operates primarily in Arizona. The Wyoming formation provides strong charging order protection (even for single-member LLCs), and Arizona will recognize the entity through foreign registration without requiring re-formation under Arizona law.

Arizona as a Destination for California Relocators

The Phoenix-Scottsdale-Tempe metropolitan area has experienced significant in-migration from California, driven by housing costs, business environment, and tax considerations. For California Bitcoin families evaluating a domicile change, Arizona offers a meaningful improvement: income tax drops from 13.3% to 2.5%, estate tax remains at zero (Arizona already had none), and community property rules are similar (both are community property states), preserving the double step-up benefit.

Establishing genuine Arizona domicile — as opposed to maintaining ties to California that allow the Franchise Tax Board to assert continuing jurisdiction — requires the same careful documentation as any California exit: registering to vote in Arizona, obtaining an Arizona driver's license, filing a change of address with financial institutions and the IRS, and spending more than 183 days per year in Arizona. California's 10-year lookback posture means that documentation must be meticulous from the day of departure.

Trust Situs for Arizona Families

Arizona's Uniform Trust Code provides a solid foundation for basic trust administration, but Wyoming and Nevada remain superior trust situs choices for families seeking the most advanced Bitcoin trust structures — directed trust frameworks with explicit digital asset provisions, SPDI custodians, dynasty trust perpetuities, and self-settled asset protection trust statutes.

Arizona does not currently have a self-settled asset protection trust statute comparable to Nevada's NAPT framework. For Bitcoin families seeking to be discretionary beneficiaries of their own irrevocable trusts, Nevada or South Dakota is a better choice of trust situs. Arizona families seeking these protections can establish a Nevada-sited directed trust administered by a Nevada trustee while domiciled in Arizona — the Nevada trust law governs the trust, and Arizona's relatively favorable income tax environment (2.5%) applies to the family's domicile income.

Bitcoin Estate Planning Across States

State law matters significantly in Bitcoin estate planning. Wyoming and South Dakota stand out for trust-friendly statutes, no state estate tax, and perpetuity-exempt dynasty trust structures. Your state of domicile determines probate exposure, trust taxation, and available legal tools.
