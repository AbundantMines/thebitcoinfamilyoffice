Bitcoin 0% Capital Gains Rate: How to Harvest Bitcoin Gains Tax-Free

Most Bitcoin holders focus relentlessly on avoiding taxes. Fewer think about the years when they can pay zero. The federal 0% long-term capital gains rate is real, it applies to Bitcoin, and for taxpayers with income below certain thresholds — retirees, people between jobs, families with strategic income — it means selling Bitcoin and paying no federal capital gains tax at all. Here's how to use it.

The 0% Rate: What It Is and Who Qualifies

The United States taxes long-term capital gains — gains on assets held more than one year — at preferential rates: 0%, 15%, or 20%, depending on total taxable income. For Bitcoin held more than one year, these are the applicable rates. The 0% rate is not a loophole or an obscure exception — it is explicit statutory law, applied by the IRS on Schedule D, available to any taxpayer whose income falls below the threshold.

The key requirement: the gain must be long-term. Bitcoin held for one year and one day (or longer) qualifies. Bitcoin held for less than one year is short-term, taxed as ordinary income at rates up to 37%. The one-year holding period is binary — there is no partial preference for gains on Bitcoin held, say, six months. Twelve months and one day: 0% possible. Eleven months and thirty days: up to 37%.

The 0% rate is not unlimited. It applies only to the portion of taxable income that falls below the 15% bracket threshold. Gains that push total taxable income above the threshold are taxed at 15% (or 20% for very high earners). The planning task is to manage your income in a given tax year so that the gain stays within the 0% bracket.

The Stacking Rule: How Gains Sit on Top of Ordinary Income

This is the most commonly misunderstood aspect of the 0% capital gains strategy. Capital gains are stacked on top of ordinary income — they occupy the top of your income stack, not a separate column alongside ordinary income.

Here's why this matters: suppose you are married filing jointly with $70,000 of ordinary income (wages, Social Security, IRA distributions) and you hold Bitcoin with $60,000 of long-term unrealized gain. The 0% bracket threshold for 2026 is $94,050. You might think: "My ordinary income is $70,000, so I have $24,050 of 0% bracket room." That is correct — but only for the first $24,050 of the gain.

If you realize the full $60,000 gain:

  • Total taxable income = $70,000 (ordinary) + $60,000 (gain) = $130,000
  • The gain sits on top of ordinary income: $70,000 to $94,050 = $24,050 of gain at 0%
  • $94,050 to $130,000 = $35,950 of gain at 15%
  • Tax on gain = $0 + ($35,950 × 15%) = $5,393

If instead you only realize $24,050 of gain (stopping at the 0% threshold):

  • Total taxable income = $70,000 + $24,050 = $94,050
  • Tax on gain = $0

The practical discipline: before realizing any Bitcoin gains, project your full-year ordinary income — including wages, retirement account distributions, Social Security, rental income, interest — and calculate exactly how much bracket room remains before the 0% threshold is crossed. Harvest only up to that amount.

These thresholds are for taxable income — after subtracting the standard deduction ($30,000 married / $15,000 single in 2026, approximately) or itemized deductions, above-the-line deductions, and any other adjustments. A married couple with $115,000 of gross income and $30,000 standard deduction has $85,000 of taxable income — below the $94,050 threshold, with $9,050 of 0% bracket room for Bitcoin gains.

The trust bracket is brutal. Trusts reach the top 20% capital gains rate at just $15,650 of income in 2026. If your Bitcoin is held in a non-grantor trust and you are planning on using the 0% rate at the trust level, that plan does not work — the trust has essentially no 0% bracket. The 0% strategy works at the individual level, not inside most trust structures.

Gain Harvesting: Sell, Rebuy, Step Up Your Basis for Free

The most powerful application of the 0% rate is gain harvesting with immediate repurchase — a strategy that permanently eliminates embedded capital gains without any holding period interruption or tax cost.

The mechanics:

  1. Identify Bitcoin lots with long-term gains that fit within your 0% bracket room
  2. Sell those lots — realizing the gain (at 0% federal tax if within the threshold)
  3. Immediately repurchase the same amount of Bitcoin at the current market price
  4. Your new cost basis = the purchase price (the gain has been permanently eliminated)

Because Bitcoin is not a security, the wash sale rule does not apply. You can sell and repurchase immediately — within the same second on the same exchange — with no 30-day waiting period, no disallowed loss risk, and no suspension of the holding period. The only requirement is that the repurchased Bitcoin starts a new holding period (held from the date of repurchase, not the original date of purchase).

The step-up effect: A married couple who bought 1 BTC in 2020 at $10,000 now holds it at $72,000. Embedded gain: $62,000. In a year with $60,000 of ordinary income, they have $34,050 of 0% bracket room. They sell and immediately rebuy 0.548 BTC (the amount with $34,050 of gain). Result: they owe $0 in federal capital gains tax. Their new basis on those 0.548 BTC is $39,513 (current market price). The $34,050 of embedded gain has been permanently eliminated. Repeat this every year with available bracket room and the embedded gain shrinks year by year — without triggering the estate tax exposure of holding until death.

Who This Strategy Applies To

The 0% bracket harvesting strategy is most valuable in years when taxable income is structurally low. Key scenarios:

Early Retirees and Bitcoin HODLers

Early retirees living on Bitcoin or spending down a cash reserve while Bitcoin appreciates often have low ordinary income — no wages, modest IRA distributions before RMD age, perhaps some rental or dividend income. This creates natural 0% bracket room that can be used to harvest gains each year. For a couple in early retirement with $40,000–$60,000 of ordinary income, $34,000–$54,000 of annual tax-free gain harvesting is available — eliminating the embedded gain progressively without triggering estate tax strategy.

Career Transitions and Sabbaticals

A year between jobs — by choice or circumstance — often produces below-threshold income. Rather than treating that year purely as a financial stress, it is also a 0% harvesting window. Bitcoin holders who take career breaks, start businesses, or work part-year have an opportunity to eliminate significant embedded gains at zero federal cost during the transition.

Young Families with a Stay-at-Home Parent

Households with one working parent and one parent staying home with children may have $40,000–$70,000 of ordinary income, leaving substantial 0% bracket room. Bitcoin accumulated during dual-income years can be harvested during the single-income years — permanently lowering the eventual capital gains exposure when the couple's income rises again.

High Earners in Unusually Low-Income Years

Even high-income earners occasionally have below-threshold years: a year with large business losses, a year of leave, a year with significant deductions from medical expenses or casualty losses. These are one-time windows that should be used for maximum Bitcoin gain harvesting.

Retirees with Controllable Income

Retirees who can control their taxable income — managing the timing of IRA distributions, Roth conversions, and Social Security benefits — have the most flexibility. A retiree taking $50,000 in ordinary income from Social Security and a small pension has $44,050 of 0% bracket room and can harvest significant Bitcoin gains each year.

Roth Conversion Competition: The Bracket Tradeoff

The 0% capital gains bracket and Roth conversion planning compete for the same income space. This is the most important planning tradeoff in the strategy.

Roth conversions involve converting traditional IRA (pre-tax) assets to Roth IRA (after-tax) — paying income tax now in exchange for tax-free growth forever. The optimal time to do Roth conversions is in years when marginal ordinary income tax rates are low — the same years that have 0% bracket room for Bitcoin gains.

The conflict: filling the 12% or 22% ordinary income bracket with Roth conversions uses the same space that Bitcoin gain harvesting needs. You cannot do both optimally in the same year if your total opportunity exceeds the available bracket room.

How to Prioritize: Roth vs Bitcoin Gain Harvest

Factor Favor Roth Conversion Favor Bitcoin Gain Harvest

The hybrid approach: For many Bitcoin families, the optimal answer is to use the bracket in layers. Fill the 0% ordinary income bracket with Roth conversions (converting pre-tax IRA at zero income tax), then use the remaining 0% capital gains bracket room for Bitcoin gain harvesting. Both strategies can coexist — they just need to be sequenced carefully so neither crowds the other out.

The NIIT Trap: 3.8% That Kicks In Before the 15% Rate

The Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) under IRC §1411 imposes a 3.8% surtax on net investment income for taxpayers with Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) above $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (married filing jointly). Bitcoin capital gains are net investment income.

Here is the trap: the NIIT threshold is based on MAGI, not taxable income. The 0% capital gains bracket threshold ($94,050 married) is based on taxable income (after deductions). These are different numbers, and the NIIT can apply even when the capital gains rate is technically 0%.

Scenario: A married couple has $180,000 of gross ordinary income, takes the $30,000 standard deduction, and has $150,000 of taxable income. The capital gains bracket: $94,050 threshold — so their gains are taxed at 15%, not 0%. Their MAGI is $180,000 + any capital gains — below the $250,000 NIIT threshold if gains are modest.

But for a couple at exactly $230,000 MAGI who realizes $30,000 of Bitcoin gain: MAGI becomes $260,000 — $10,000 above the NIIT threshold. The NIIT applies to $10,000 of gain at 3.8% = $380 of NIIT. The remaining $20,000 of gain is below the threshold.

The practical implication: if your MAGI is in the $200,000–$250,000 range, model the NIIT impact before harvesting. The 0% federal capital gains rate may still apply, but NIIT can add 3.8% to gains above the $250,000 threshold. For most gain harvesting situations (income well below $200,000), NIIT is not a factor.

Lot Selection: Which Bitcoin to Harvest for Maximum 0% Benefit

Most Bitcoin holders have accumulated across multiple purchases at different prices. Effective 0% gain harvesting requires identifying the right lots to sell.

Target Long-Term Lots With Gains Within Your Bracket Room

You want lots that:

  • Were purchased more than one year ago (long-term holding period — 0% eligible)
  • Have gains that fit within your remaining 0% bracket room
  • Have the highest gain per coin (most gain eliminated per dollar of bracket room used)

You do NOT want to harvest:

  • Short-term lots (held less than one year) — these produce ordinary income, not capital gains, and don't benefit from the 0% rate
  • Lots with losses — these are better harvested via tax-loss harvesting to generate deductible losses, not sold as gain harvests
  • Lots that would push total gains above your 0% threshold — partial harvesting is fine; stop when you hit the threshold

HIFO for Tax-Loss Harvesting; LIFO or Specific ID for Gain Harvesting

Your crypto tax software's lot assignment method matters. For 0% gain harvesting, you want to use specific identification (or LIFO — last in, first out) to select the particular long-term lots you want to harvest. HIFO (highest in, first out) is optimal for minimizing current-year taxes generally, but for gain harvesting you specifically want the higher-gain long-term lots — which may not be the ones HIFO selects.

Confirm that your exchange or crypto tax software supports specific lot identification, and document your election at the time of sale. The IRS requires specific ID elections to be made at the time of sale — you cannot retroactively assign lots after the fact.

Gifting Bitcoin to Lower-Bracket Family Members

If you are in a high-income bracket where no 0% room exists, but you have family members in lower brackets — adult children, siblings, parents — you can gift appreciated Bitcoin to those individuals and allow them to harvest the gains at 0%.

The mechanics: gifted Bitcoin carries your cost basis (carryover basis under IRC §1015). The recipient is treated as having held it from the date you acquired it for holding period purposes (tacking under §1223). If you gifted Bitcoin purchased five years ago, the recipient can sell it immediately with a long-term holding period and recognize your embedded gain at their applicable rate — potentially 0%.

The kiddie tax limitation: This strategy has limits for children under age 19 (or full-time students under 24). The "kiddie tax" under IRC §1(g) taxes a child's unearned income above a threshold ($2,600 in 2026) at the parent's marginal rate — not the child's rate. Gifting Bitcoin gains to a minor child will trigger the kiddie tax on gains above $2,600, eliminating the 0% benefit for that portion. This restriction does not apply to adult children (19+, not full-time students, or 24+ regardless).

The annual gift tax exclusion ($19,000 per recipient in 2026) limits how much can be transferred without gift tax implications. For larger transfers to adult family members, see the full Bitcoin Gift Tax guide.

8-Item 0% Gain Harvesting Execution Checklist

  • Project full-year ordinary income before year-end. Estimate wages, Social Security benefits (85% taxable for most), IRA distributions, rental income, interest, and dividends for the full calendar year. This is your ordinary income base — the 0% bracket room is the threshold minus this number.
  • Calculate remaining 0% bracket room. Subtract projected ordinary income from the applicable threshold ($94,050 married / $47,025 single). The result is your maximum gain that can be harvested at 0%. Do this in Q3, not December — earlier is better to allow time for execution.
  • Identify eligible long-term Bitcoin lots. Pull your cost basis report from your crypto tax software. Flag all lots held more than one year with unrealized gains. Sort by gain per coin to prioritize which lots deliver the most basis step-up per dollar of bracket room consumed.
  • Model Roth conversion vs. Bitcoin gain harvest tradeoff. If you also plan Roth conversions this year, model both scenarios. In most cases, use 0% ordinary income tax bracket space for Roth conversions first, then use 0% capital gains bracket space for Bitcoin gain harvesting — they don't directly compete, but get the ordering right.
  • Check NIIT exposure. If your MAGI including the harvested gains could approach $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (married), model the NIIT impact. The 3.8% NIIT on gains above the threshold adds a real cost that may partially offset the 0% income tax benefit.
  • Execute the sale and immediate repurchase. Sell the identified lots and immediately repurchase the same amount of Bitcoin. Document the specific lot IDs sold, the sale price and date, and the repurchase price and date. The repurchase starts a new holding period — the newly purchased Bitcoin will need to be held for one year to qualify for long-term treatment on the next harvest cycle.
  • Record the election in your tax records. Specific identification of lots must be elected and documented at the time of sale. Keep a written record (email to your broker/exchange, notation in your tax software) identifying exactly which lots were sold. This protects you in audit.
  • Repeat annually in low-income years. The 0% bracket is available every year you qualify. Build a systematic annual review — project October income, calculate bracket room, harvest before December 31st. Over five to ten years of systematic harvesting, significant embedded gains can be permanently eliminated at zero federal tax cost.

The Bottom Line

The 0% capital gains rate is one of the most underutilized tools in Bitcoin tax planning. Most Bitcoin holders think about taxes only when they sell — and most of those sales happen in high-income years, at the 15% or 23.8% rate. The discipline of systematically harvesting gains during low-income years, at zero federal tax cost, is available to almost everyone who holds Bitcoin long-term. It simply requires attention and execution before December 31st.

For Bitcoin families building intergenerational wealth, the 0% harvesting strategy integrates naturally with the broader estate planning picture. Harvest gains at 0% during low-income years. Hold the remainder until death for the §1014 step-up. Donate the highest-gain lots to charity (eliminating the gain entirely). The combination of these three approaches — active 0% harvesting, hold-until-death step-up, and charitable giving — can reduce the lifetime federal capital gains tax rate on Bitcoin to a number well below the statutory 20% maximum.
