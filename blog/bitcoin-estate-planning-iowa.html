Bitcoin Estate Planning in Iowa

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  8 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in Iowa Est. 8 min read

Iowa has crossed an important threshold for Bitcoin estate planning. For years, Iowa was one of a small group of states imposing an inheritance tax on beneficiaries receiving assets from Iowa decedents. That changed with the enactment of House File 2641 in 2021, which phased out Iowa's inheritance tax over several years, with full repeal effective January 1, 2025. Bitcoin estate planning Iowa now operates in an environment with no state estate tax and no inheritance tax — a clean starting point that focuses planning on federal estate tax strategy, trust structure, and Bitcoin-specific succession mechanics.

This is a meaningful change for Iowa Bitcoin families. Prior to repeal, beneficiaries in certain classes paid Iowa inheritance tax at rates ranging from 2% to 15% on inherited assets above applicable exemption amounts — a cost that could reach six figures on a significant Bitcoin inheritance. Now, Iowa families can focus that planning energy on structuring proper trusts, implementing multi-signature custody, and coordinating with federal estate tax strategy.

Iowa's Post-2025 Tax Environment: Federal Only

With the Iowa inheritance tax fully repealed as of January 1, 2025, and no Iowa estate tax, the only remaining tax constraint for Iowa Bitcoin families is federal. The federal estate tax applies to estates exceeding the federal exemption — approximately $13.99 million per individual in 2025 — with rates up to 40%. Importantly, the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act provisions that doubled the federal exemption in 2018 are scheduled to sunset after December 31, 2025, unless Congress acts to extend them. After the sunset, the exemption is projected to revert to approximately $7 million per individual (inflation-adjusted).

Iowa Bitcoin families with estates approaching the post-2025 exemption threshold should treat the remainder of 2025 as an urgent planning window. Irrevocable trust transfers, family LLC recapitalizations, and gifting strategies implemented before the sunset can lock in today's higher exemption and remove appreciated Bitcoin from the taxable estate — permanently, if properly structured.

Iowa's inheritance tax repeal is complete as of 2025. Iowa Bitcoin families now face a clean federal-only planning environment — with a 2025 year-end deadline that every family near the exemption threshold should take seriously.

Iowa Trust Law: Uniform Trust Code Framework

Iowa adopted the Uniform Trust Code (Iowa Code Chapter 633A, the Iowa Trust Code). The Iowa Trust Code provides modern trust formation and administration infrastructure, including directed trust arrangements that allow investment direction and administrative trustee functions to be separated within a single trust. For Bitcoin families, this separation is essential: it allows a technically sophisticated Bitcoin custody advisor or family member to direct custody decisions — hardware wallet selection, multi-signature configurations, key rotation — while a professional trust company handles tax compliance, distributions, and general administration.

Iowa's rule against perpetuities currently limits trusts to 21 years after the death of lives in being at the trust's creation, with a 90-year alternative wait-and-see period. This means Iowa is not a dynasty trust jurisdiction in the same sense as neighboring South Dakota, which permits perpetual trusts. Iowa families wanting multigenerational Bitcoin trusts with no defined end date should evaluate South Dakota-sited trust structures administered by South Dakota corporate trustees — a strategy available to Iowa residents regardless of domicile.

Digital Asset Succession in Iowa: RUFADAA Framework

Iowa adopted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), codified at Iowa Code §§ 633E.1 through 633E.19. RUFADAA provides the statutory foundation for fiduciaries — trustees, personal representatives, and agents under durable powers of attorney — to access digital assets of decedents and principals. The statute creates a priority hierarchy: any online tool designation by the account holder controls first, then any express direction in a governing document, then any direction in a will or trust instrument, and finally the platform's default terms of service.

For Bitcoin held in self-custody on hardware wallets, RUFADAA's provisions are relevant to any associated online accounts (exchange accounts, wallet management software, hardware wallet manufacturer accounts) but do not address the core access problem: who holds the private keys. A trustee with full RUFADAA authority and a signed letter of administration cannot sign a Bitcoin transaction without the private keys. Every Iowa Bitcoin estate plan must address key succession explicitly through a technical protocol maintained separately from the trust instrument.

Trust and LLC Structures for Iowa Bitcoin Families

Iowa Bitcoin families should consider the following planning structures, adapted to Iowa's legal environment:

  • Iowa Revocable Living Trust: The foundation of any Iowa Bitcoin estate plan — avoids probate, provides successor trustee continuity at death or incapacity, and serves as the container for the Bitcoin succession framework. Include explicit digital asset access authority and RUFADAA conforming language.
  • Iowa LLC as Bitcoin holding vehicle: An Iowa LLC holds Bitcoin and is owned by the trust. The operating agreement governs multi-signature custody, transaction authorization thresholds, and succession of member authority. Minority interests can be gifted to heirs at discounted values to reduce the federal taxable estate.
  • Irrevocable trust for federal estate tax planning: For families approaching the federal exemption, an irrevocable trust — SLAT, GRAT, or IDGT — can remove Bitcoin from the taxable estate while allowing continued family economic benefit. These structures should be implemented before the 2025 TCJA sunset.
  • South Dakota-sited dynasty trust: For Iowa families wanting a perpetual Bitcoin trust with no end date, a South Dakota-sited trust administered by a South Dakota corporate trustee provides perpetuity and South Dakota's favorable trust tax rules — accessible to Iowa residents through appropriate trust situs elections.

Practical Priorities for Iowa Bitcoin Families

  1. Recognize that Iowa inheritance tax is fully repealed: Any planning done pre-2025 with inheritance tax avoidance as a primary driver should be reviewed — it may be appropriate to simplify or adjust structures now that the tax is gone.
  2. Act on the 2025 federal exemption sunset: If your total estate approaches the projected post-2025 federal exemption, implement irrevocable trust and LLC strategies before December 31, 2025.
  3. Establish a Bitcoin-specific custody governance framework: Multi-signature arrangements, technical succession protocols, and directed trust authority should be in place regardless of the tax environment.
  4. Consider South Dakota for dynasty trust siting: For long-duration Bitcoin holding, a South Dakota perpetual trust accessed by Iowa residents provides planning horizons unavailable under Iowa law.
  5. Update existing estate plans to reflect Iowa's new tax landscape: Plans drafted pre-2025 may include provisions designed around the inheritance tax that are now unnecessary or suboptimal.
