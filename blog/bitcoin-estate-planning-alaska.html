Bitcoin Estate Planning in Alaska

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in Alaska Est. 9 min read

Alaska presents one of the most uniquely favorable environments for Bitcoin families in the country — and in ways that go beyond the typical no-estate-tax, no-income-tax playbook. The Last Frontier imposes no state estate tax, no inheritance tax, no state income tax, and no state sales tax. Alaska is also one of only nine community property states in the country — an unusual feature that creates distinctive planning opportunities for married Bitcoin holders. Alaska has enacted some of the most Bitcoin-friendly statutes in any U.S. state. And Alaska's Permanent Fund dividend — an annual distribution to every Alaska resident from the state's sovereign wealth fund — represents a philosophical precedent for universal basic income that resonates with certain Bitcoin community values.

For Bitcoin families domiciled in Alaska, the planning environment is genuinely exceptional. The primary focus is federal estate tax planning for larger estates, the distinctive community property basis planning opportunity, and the specific trust law infrastructure that makes Alaska attractive for multigenerational wealth holding.

Alaska's Tax Baseline: Four Zeros

Alaska's state tax profile for Bitcoin families:

  • No estate tax. Alaska has no state-level estate tax on decedents' estates, regardless of size.
  • No inheritance tax. Transfers to heirs at death are not subject to Alaska inheritance tax.
  • No state income tax. Alaska does not tax wages, investment income, or capital gains at the state level. Bitcoin sales, exchanges, and income events generate no Alaska income tax — only federal tax.
  • No state sales tax. Alaska is one of five states with no general state sales tax (though some municipalities impose local sales taxes).

This combination — no estate tax, no income tax, no sales tax — places Alaska in an extremely small group of states (alongside Wyoming and Nevada) that offer a comprehensive no-tax baseline for Bitcoin families at every stage of wealth accumulation, realization, and transfer. Federal taxes apply, but the state layer is entirely absent.

Alaska's four zeros — no estate tax, no inheritance tax, no income tax, no sales tax — combined with Bitcoin-friendly statutes and community property rules make it one of the most favorable states in the country for Bitcoin wealth planning.

Alaska as a Community Property State: The Stepped-Up Basis Advantage

Alaska is unusual among low-tax states in being a community property jurisdiction — one of only nine states where married couples can hold property as community property. Community property has a significant federal income tax advantage at death that most estate planners and Bitcoin families overlook:

Under IRC § 1014, when a spouse dies, community property assets receive a full stepped-up basis on both halves of the community property at the surviving spouse's fair market value at date of death. In contrast, in common law (separate property) states, only the decedent's half of jointly held property receives a stepped-up basis — the surviving spouse's half retains its original cost basis.

For Bitcoin families, this distinction is enormous. Consider a married couple who jointly purchased Bitcoin years ago at a low cost basis. In a common law state, only 50% of the position receives a step-up in basis at the first death. In Alaska as a community property state, 100% of the community property Bitcoin position receives a stepped-up basis at the first death — completely eliminating the federal income tax on the entire lifetime appreciation. If the Bitcoin position is held until the surviving spouse also dies, a second full step-up occurs, eliminating any remaining embedded gain.

Alaska is also one of only two states (the other being Tennessee) that allows married couples domiciled in common law states to elect into Alaska community property by creating an Alaska community property trust — making this stepped-up basis advantage available to non-Alaska residents through a trust established under Alaska law. For married Bitcoin holders domiciled outside Alaska with large embedded gains, the Alaska community property trust strategy may deliver more income tax savings than any other single planning tool.

Alaska's Bitcoin-Friendly Legal Infrastructure

Alaska has enacted legislation that explicitly recognizes Bitcoin and other digital assets in its commercial law framework. Alaska has adopted provisions aligning with the Uniform Commercial Code Article 12 amendments that recognize "controllable electronic records" — including Bitcoin — as a distinct category of property with a specific, Bitcoin-compatible legal framework for transfer, perfection of security interests, and ownership determination. This matters for Bitcoin estate planning because it establishes clear legal rules for:

  • How Bitcoin ownership is transferred as a matter of Alaska law
  • How creditors can perfect security interests in Bitcoin holdings (relevant for businesses using Bitcoin as collateral)
  • How fiduciaries — executors, trustees — can access and transfer Bitcoin on behalf of estates and trusts

Alaska has also adopted a version of the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), providing statutory authority for fiduciaries to access digital asset accounts. Combined with Alaska's UCC Article 12 framework, Alaska provides one of the clearest legal environments for Bitcoin custody and succession in the country.

Alaska Trust Law: Directed Trusts and Asset Protection

Alaska enacted one of the first domestic asset protection trust (DAPT) statutes in the country and has remained competitive with Wyoming and South Dakota in the trust situs competition. Key Alaska trust law features for Bitcoin families include:

Alaska Domestic Asset Protection Trust (DAPT): Alaska allows self-settled asset protection trusts where the grantor is also a discretionary beneficiary, after a four-year seasoning period. Assets transferred to a properly structured Alaska DAPT are protected from the grantor's future creditors after the seasoning period. For Bitcoin families facing potential business liability, litigation risk, or uncertain future financial circumstances, an Alaska DAPT combines asset protection with estate tax reduction.

Directed trusts: Alaska allows extensive separation of investment direction from administrative trustee functions. A Bitcoin-specialized custodian can direct all custody decisions while a corporate trustee handles compliance, reporting, and distributions. This structure is essential for Bitcoin trusts where the family insists on maintaining control over custody decisions while engaging a professional trustee for administrative functions.

Perpetual trusts: Alaska allows perpetual trusts for personal property, enabling Bitcoin to remain in trust indefinitely for multiple generations with no rule-against-perpetuities limitation. A dynasty trust established under Alaska law can hold Bitcoin for descendants across centuries — entirely outside the federal estate tax system after the initial transfer and GST exemption allocation.

Alaska Permanent Fund and the Bitcoin Philosophy

The Alaska Permanent Fund — established in 1976 from oil revenue and now managed as a sovereign wealth fund with assets exceeding $80 billion — distributes an annual dividend to every Alaska resident who has lived in the state for a full calendar year. The Permanent Fund Dividend (PFD) typically ranges from $1,000 to $2,000 per person annually, funded by the fund's investment returns. In years of high fund performance or special legislative appropriations, the PFD has exceeded $3,000.

The Permanent Fund represents a model of resource wealth being distributed broadly rather than concentrated — a philosophy that resonates with certain Bitcoin community values around sovereignty, universal access, and resistance to inflationary capture by incumbents. For Alaska Bitcoin families, the PFD is a minor income item but a major cultural touchstone: it demonstrates that Alaska takes the long-term preservation and distribution of its sovereign wealth seriously, in ways that align with the multi-generational mindset of serious Bitcoin holders.

Practical Priorities for Alaska Bitcoin Families

  1. Evaluate Alaska community property trust strategy for married couples with large embedded Bitcoin gains — the full step-up in basis at the first death can save millions in income tax compared to common law property treatment.
  2. Establish an Alaska DAPT or dynasty trust for the core Bitcoin position — Alaska's asset protection trust and perpetual trust authority are among the strongest in the country.
  3. Use federal transfer strategies aggressively — with no state estate tax, all planning energy focuses on federal strategies: GRATs, SLATs, dynasty trusts, annual exclusion gifting.
  4. Leverage Alaska's Bitcoin-friendly legal framework — structure the estate plan explicitly under Alaska law where possible, using Alaska's clear UCC Article 12 and RUFADAA framework for Bitcoin custody and succession.
  5. Document Bitcoin custody succession explicitly — legal authority is clear under Alaska law, but technical key succession must be addressed in a separate protocol coordinated with your multi-signature architecture.
