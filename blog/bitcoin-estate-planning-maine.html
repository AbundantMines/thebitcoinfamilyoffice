Bitcoin Estate Planning in Maine

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in Maine Est. 9 min read

Maine occupies an unusual position among northeastern states: its estate tax exemption of $6.8 million is relatively high compared to Massachusetts ($2M) and Rhode Island ($1.79M), but its graduated rate structure — reaching 12% at the top — means that Maine imposes one of the highest effective estate tax burdens on the East Coast for estates meaningfully above the exemption. For Bitcoin families who have accumulated positions in the $7–20 million range, Maine's estate tax is a material planning constraint that must be addressed explicitly.

Maine has no inheritance tax, and there is no Maine gift tax separate from the federal gift tax regime. This creates the same planning asymmetry found in Vermont: lifetime gifts reduce the Maine taxable estate at no Maine-level gift tax cost, making lifetime transfer strategies particularly valuable for Maine Bitcoin holders.

Maine's Estate Tax Structure

Maine imposes its estate tax under 36 M.R.S. § 4061 et seq. The current Maine estate tax exemption is $6.8 million — indexed for inflation in some years but not automatically matched to the federal exemption. The rate structure is graduated:

  • 8% on the first taxable amount above the exemption
  • Graduated up to 12% on larger taxable estates

The specific brackets apply to the estate value above the exemption threshold. An estate of $10 million — approximately $3.2 million above the Maine exemption — faces Maine estate tax that can approach $300,000–$400,000 depending on the exact bracket structure applicable. An estate of $20 million faces a Maine estate tax bill in the $1.5–1.8 million range, on top of any federal estate tax.

Maine applies its estate tax to the worldwide estate of Maine-domiciled decedents, and to Maine-sited real property and tangible personal property of non-Maine decedents. Bitcoin — which has no physical situs — is generally treated as a domicile-based intangible asset: a Maine-domiciled decedent's Bitcoin is subject to Maine estate tax regardless of where it is stored physically. A non-Maine decedent holding only Bitcoin has no Maine estate tax exposure, even if the Bitcoin was acquired while the decedent lived in Maine.

Maine's estate tax, with graduated rates reaching 12% above a $6.8 million exemption, creates meaningful planning urgency for Bitcoin families whose holdings may push several million above the threshold.

Lifetime Transfer Strategies for Maine Bitcoin Families

Because Maine has no state gift tax, lifetime transfers — including gifts to irrevocable trusts — are the primary mechanism for reducing Maine estate tax exposure. The most effective strategies:

Annual exclusion gifting programs: The federal annual exclusion ($18,000 per recipient per donor in 2025) allows unlimited recipients to receive gifts without any gift tax cost. Maine does not piggyback on the federal gift tax, so annual exclusion gifts reduce the Maine taxable estate without any Maine-level tax cost. A systematic program of gifting LLC interests holding Bitcoin to children and trusts can remove substantial value from the Maine taxable estate over a 10–15 year horizon.

Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs): A GRAT transfers Bitcoin to an irrevocable trust while retaining annuity payments for a fixed term. Appreciation above the IRS Section 7520 rate passes to beneficiaries with no gift or estate tax at the federal or Maine level. For Bitcoin's historically high volatility and growth potential, GRATs offer a powerful mechanism to shift appreciation out of the Maine taxable estate. Short-term GRATs that are rolled if Bitcoin declines during the term are typically preferable to single long-term structures.

Irrevocable Life Insurance Trusts (ILITs): Maine estate tax creates a liquidity problem at death: executors may need to pay Maine estate tax within nine months, potentially forcing a Bitcoin sale at an inopportune time. An ILIT funded with a life insurance policy provides estate liquidity — proceeds pass outside the Maine taxable estate if the trust was established and funded correctly — without requiring forced asset sales. For Maine Bitcoin families with significant estate tax exposure, an ILIT is often a necessary complement to the core planning structure, not merely an add-on.

Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs): Maine Bitcoin holders with low-cost-basis positions can contribute appreciated Bitcoin to a CRT, avoid immediate capital gains recognition on the inside sale, receive an annuity, and pass the charitable remainder to a qualified organization. The CRT reduces the Maine taxable estate by the value of the contributed Bitcoin and provides a Maine income tax charitable deduction in the year of contribution.

Maine Trust Law and Out-of-State Situs

Maine adopted the Uniform Trust Code in 2005, codified at 18-B M.R.S. § 101 et seq. Maine's UTC provides directed trust authority, decanting powers, and the basic modern trust infrastructure needed for Bitcoin trust planning. However, Maine has not enacted the premium directed trust provisions, perpetual trust authority, or asset protection trust statutes that make Wyoming and South Dakota preferred situs jurisdictions for multigenerational Bitcoin trusts.

A Maine-domiciled grantor can establish a dynasty trust sited in Wyoming, administered by a Wyoming corporate trustee, for the benefit of Maine-resident and future out-of-state beneficiaries. Maine's taxation of out-of-state trust income focuses on whether Maine-resident beneficiaries receive distributions; accumulation trusts with long time horizons before meaningful distributions can achieve significant deferral of Maine income tax. For families building multigenerational Bitcoin trusts — where the trust accumulates for decades before distributions to grandchildren or later descendants — Wyoming situs from a Maine grantor is a powerful combination. Maine LLC for Bitcoin Holding

Maine LLCs (governed by 31 M.R.S. § 1501 et seq.) provide a sound vehicle for Bitcoin holding with charging order protection and flexible operating agreement governance. A Maine family LLC holding Bitcoin allows systematic gifting of minority interests at valuation discounts — typically 20–35% for minority interest and lack of marketability — reducing the Maine estate tax value of transferred interests.

The LLC operating agreement should explicitly address Bitcoin custody governance: multi-signature custody requirements, transaction authorization thresholds, key-person succession protocols specifying what happens if the primary manager is incapacitated or deceased, and technical documentation standards. Maine has adopted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), providing statutory authority for fiduciaries to access digital assets — but technical access to Bitcoin private keys must be addressed separately in the succession planning documents, not merely in the legal authority granted by statute.

Federal Estate Planning for Maine Bitcoin Holders

Maine Bitcoin families face both state estate tax (above $6.8M) and potential federal estate tax (above approximately $14M per individual in 2025, potentially reduced after 2025 TCJA sunset). Planning must address both layers simultaneously:

  1. Quantify Maine estate tax exposure — identify the gap between your current estate value and the $6.8M Maine exemption; Bitcoin price volatility means this analysis should be updated regularly.
  2. Begin a systematic gifting program of Bitcoin or LLC interests to reduce the Maine taxable estate; use annual exclusions aggressively.
  3. Establish GRATs to transfer Bitcoin appreciation to heirs without gift or estate tax cost; consider rolling short-term GRATs as a systematic strategy.
  4. Fund an ILIT with life insurance to provide estate liquidity for Maine estate tax payment without forcing Bitcoin sales at death.
  5. Evaluate Wyoming trust situs for a multigenerational trust holding Bitcoin LLC interests — capturing Wyoming's perpetual trust and directed trust advantages while managing Maine's trust income tax rules.
  6. Document Bitcoin succession explicitly — RUFADAA provides legal authority, but technical key succession must be addressed in a separate protocol coordinated with your custody architecture.
