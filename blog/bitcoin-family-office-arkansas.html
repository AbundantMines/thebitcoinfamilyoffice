Arkansas's Tax Transformation Under Governor Sanders

Arkansas has been one of the most aggressive income tax reform states under Governor Sarah Huckabee Sanders. Starting from a top rate that exceeded 5.9%, a series of special sessions and regular legislative sessions have cut the top rate to approximately 3.9% — with Sanders expressing intent to continue reducing toward Tennessee-style 0% income tax territory over time. Verify the current rate with an Arkansas-licensed CPA, as the rate has been changing rapidly.

At ~3.9%, Arkansas's combined LTCG rate is approximately 27.7% — competitive with Iowa (27.6%) and North Carolina (27.79%). Arkansas now sits in the B+ tier alongside Indiana, Ohio, Iowa, and Missouri — a peer group that would have been unthinkable five years ago when Arkansas's rate exceeded 6%.

The Reform Trajectory: Unlike states that have legislated a specific glide path (Nebraska's LB 754, Iowa's HF 2317), Arkansas's reform has proceeded through a series of successive cuts, each requiring new legislative action. The trajectory is clear and the political will exists under Governor Sanders, but the exact final rate and timeline are not legislatively locked in the way Iowa's 3.8% and Nebraska's 3.99% are. Verify current and projected rates with Arkansas counsel.

Arkansas Wealth Profile: The Walmart Ecosystem

Bentonville: The Walmart Universe

Bentonville is the most economically disproportionate small city in America. A city of approximately 55,000 people is home to the global headquarters of the largest company on earth by revenue — and the extended Walton family wealth ecosystem has turned Northwest Arkansas into a genuine global destination for art, cycling, outdoor recreation, and now technology:

  • Walmart Inc. — Fortune #1 company globally. Bentonville HQ. Walmart's executive compensation — equity awards, stock options, performance bonuses — creates a regular cadence of large liquidity events for Walmart's C-suite and senior vice presidents. These are executives earning Bay Area compensation while living in Arkansas with low cost of living and (now) competitive income tax rates.
  • Walmart supplier ecosystem: Major consumer goods companies — Procter & Gamble, Nestlé, Unilever, Johnson & Johnson, Tyson Foods, J.M. Smucker, and hundreds of others — maintain permanent "supplier offices" in Bentonville to manage their Walmart relationships. These offices are staffed by senior executives earning global compensation who choose to live in Northwest Arkansas for the Walmart relationship. This supplier community creates a distinct wealth class of corporate executives with major company equity and Walmart relationship bonuses, living in an environment with Arkansas's now-competitive income tax.
  • The Walton Family Foundation: The Foundation has invested approximately $1.5 billion in Northwest Arkansas arts and cultural infrastructure — the Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art, the Momentary contemporary art center, the Razorback Regional Greenway cycling trail, the Ledger hotel, and dozens of supporting institutions. This investment has transformed Bentonville from a small Arkansas town into a genuinely world-class cultural destination, attracting wealth from outside the Walmart ecosystem who choose Bentonville for lifestyle reasons.
  • Tyson Foods — Springdale, Arkansas (adjacent to Bentonville in the Northwest Arkansas metro). Tyson is the world's second-largest processor and marketer of chicken, beef, and pork. Tyson executive wealth and the founding Tyson family wealth represent a distinct agribusiness wealth profile alongside the retail-oriented Walmart ecosystem.
  • Dillard's Department Stores — Little Rock headquarters. The Dillard family has built significant wealth from the regional department store chain founded in 1938.

Little Rock: State Government and Stephens Inc.

Little Rock's most important wealth institution is Stephens Inc. — the largest investment bank headquartered in the United States between the coasts. Founded by Jackson Stephens and Witt Stephens, Stephens Inc. has financed Arkansas businesses for decades and managed billions in wealth for the state's business community. The Stephens family's own wealth and the professional community around Stephens Inc. represent Little Rock's primary wealth planning market.

The Tennessee Border Comparison

Arkansas borders Tennessee along the Mississippi River, with Memphis directly across from the Arkansas border communities of Marion, West Memphis, and Blytheville. Tennessee's 0% income tax creates a planning consideration for high-income Arkansas residents who live near the state border — but unlike the Kansas/Missouri or Indiana/Illinois situation, the Tennessee border communities are not where Arkansas's wealth is concentrated. Bentonville is 5+ hours from Memphis. The Tennessee domicile option for Arkansas Bitcoin holders requires a genuine lifestyle migration, not just a short move.

Arkansas Trust Law

Arkansas adopted the Uniform Trust Code (the Arkansas Trust Code, Title 28 of the Arkansas Code). Arkansas supports directed trusts, trust protectors, and decanting. Arkansas does not have a perpetual dynasty trust statute — trusts are subject to Arkansas's Rule Against Perpetuities. Arkansas has no Domestic Asset Protection Trust statute.

Standard recommendation: Wyoming LLC + South Dakota dynasty trust for Arkansas Bitcoin holders. The SD trust siting provides perpetual duration (vs. Arkansas RAP), 0% trust income tax on accumulated gains, and DAPT creditor protection not available in Arkansas. The Bentonville-to-Sioux Falls planning corridor is served by several regional trust companies that understand the Northwest Arkansas wealth profile.
