Bitcoin Estate Planning in North Dakota

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in North Dakota Est. 9 min read

North Dakota occupies a distinctive position in the American wealth landscape: a state with no estate tax, no inheritance tax, relatively modest income tax rates, and wealth traditions rooted in farmland, mineral rights, and energy production that stretch back generations. In recent years, the Bakken Formation oil boom created a new wave of North Dakota wealth — sudden liquidity from mineral royalties that many families have struggled to preserve and deploy into durable long-term assets. Bitcoin has emerged as one vehicle for that preservation, appealing to North Dakota families who understand commodity cycles, distrust institutional intermediaries, and prefer holding real assets over fiat-denominated instruments.

North Dakota also hosts the Bank of North Dakota — the only state-owned bank in the United States — a century-old institution that provides a unique counterpoint to the conventional banking system that Bitcoin was designed to offer an alternative to. Understanding North Dakota's financial and agricultural wealth traditions is essential context for Bitcoin estate planning in the Peace Garden State.

North Dakota's Tax Baseline for Bitcoin Families

North Dakota imposes no state estate tax and no state inheritance tax. The state does impose a state income tax, with rates ranging from 1.1% to 2.9% on individual income — among the lowest state income tax rates in the country following recent legislative reductions. The substantially reduced top rate means that North Dakota Bitcoin holders who sell or exchange Bitcoin during their lifetime face a combined tax rate meaningfully lower than most states — federal capital gains tax plus approximately 2.9% state income tax, compared to 6–13% in high-tax states.

North Dakota's low income tax environment, combined with the absence of estate and inheritance taxes, creates a genuinely favorable baseline for Bitcoin wealth planning. The primary planning focus for North Dakota Bitcoin families is federal: managing the federal estate tax exemption for larger estates, optimizing the basis step-up at death, and building trust structures that protect Bitcoin for multiple generations under federal GST tax rules.

North Dakota's combination of no estate tax, low income tax rates, and agricultural wealth traditions creates a unique environment where Bitcoin fits naturally alongside farmland and mineral rights as a long-term store of value for North Dakota families.

Agricultural Wealth and Bitcoin: The North Dakota Intersection

North Dakota farm families represent some of the most complex estate planning challenges in the country — not because of high tax rates, but because of the structural challenges of transferring illiquid agricultural wealth across generations:

Farm succession challenges: North Dakota farmland has appreciated dramatically over the past 20 years, with per-acre values in prime Cass County farmland exceeding $5,000–$6,000 per acre. A family farm of 2,000 acres can represent $10–12 million in asset value — entirely illiquid, generating modest annual cash flow relative to its value, and deeply emotionally significant to the family. Traditional estate planning for farm families involves careful use of the federal estate tax exemption, installment sales, and family LLC structures to transfer the farm across generations without forcing liquidation.

Bitcoin as the liquid counterpart: For North Dakota farm families, Bitcoin serves a distinctive role: it provides the liquid, globally portable, appreciation-oriented asset that farmland cannot be. A farm family that holds $8 million in farmland and $2 million in Bitcoin has a balanced portfolio — the farmland provides generational rootedness, cash flow, and inflation protection linked to agricultural commodity prices; the Bitcoin provides liquidity, optionality, and exposure to a different kind of scarcity and appreciation dynamic. Estate planning for this family must address both asset classes simultaneously, with different tools for each.

Mineral rights and the Bakken legacy: The Bakken Formation oil boom of the 2000s and 2010s created significant mineral rights wealth for North Dakota families sitting on productive acreage. Like Bitcoin, mineral royalty interests are difficult to value precisely and can be illiquid. Families that reinvested Bakken royalties into Bitcoin during the period of abundant cash flow now hold both legacy mineral rights and digital asset positions — a portfolio that requires careful estate planning to transfer efficiently.

The Bank of North Dakota: Context for Bitcoin Adoption

The Bank of North Dakota (BND), established in 1919, is the only state-owned bank in the United States. It serves as the state's depository for all state funds, provides liquidity and correspondent services to North Dakota's community banks, and funds agricultural, student, and business loans at subsidized rates. The BND's existence reflects North Dakota's historical distrust of large out-of-state financial institutions — a tradition of financial self-reliance that predates Bitcoin by a century.

For North Dakota Bitcoin holders, the BND's existence is not directly relevant to estate planning — the BND is a state-run financial institution, not a Bitcoin custodian or trust company. However, the philosophical orientation that created the BND — distrust of concentrated financial power, preference for local control, emphasis on self-reliance — is culturally resonant with the Bitcoin ethos. North Dakota Bitcoin holders often come from agricultural and energy backgrounds where managing assets independently, without dependence on large institutional intermediaries, is a deeply held value.

This cultural context shapes how North Dakota Bitcoin families approach estate planning: they tend to prefer self-custody over third-party custodians, direct ownership over financial wrappers, and simple, understandable structures over complex institutional arrangements. Bitcoin estate planning for North Dakota families must respect these preferences while ensuring that technical succession planning is robust enough to actually transfer Bitcoin to heirs.

North Dakota Trust Law and LLC Structures

North Dakota adopted the Uniform Trust Code, providing a functional framework for trust formation, directed trusts, and digital asset fiduciary access. North Dakota has also adopted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), providing statutory authority for executors, trustees, and agents under power of attorney to access digital assets of deceased or incapacitated individuals.

For multigenerational Bitcoin trusts, North Dakota families typically look to Wyoming or South Dakota for trust situs — both neighboring states with superior perpetual trust provisions, directed trust legislation, and no state income tax on trust income. A North Dakota-domiciled grantor can establish a Wyoming dynasty trust administered by a Wyoming corporate trustee, contribute Bitcoin or LLC interests to the trust, and establish a multigenerational holding structure that benefits North Dakota-resident and future out-of-state beneficiaries.

North Dakota LLCs provide a capable holding vehicle for Bitcoin alongside farmland and mineral rights. An operating agreement that addresses multi-signature custody requirements, succession of key management authority, and technical documentation standards is essential. For families holding both farmland and Bitcoin in the same LLC, the operating agreement must be flexible enough to govern both asset classes — with different governance provisions for each.

Federal Estate Planning Priorities for ND Bitcoin Families

  1. Coordinate farm and Bitcoin planning — develop an integrated estate plan that addresses both asset classes, using family LLCs to hold Bitcoin alongside farmland and mineral rights interests.
  2. Use federal transfer strategies aggressively — GRATs, annual exclusion gifting, and dynasty trust contributions all reduce the federal taxable estate without any North Dakota estate tax cost.
  3. Evaluate Wyoming trust situs for the multigenerational Bitcoin trust, capturing Wyoming's perpetual trust and no-income-tax advantages while the grantor remains domiciled in North Dakota.
  4. Address Bitcoin succession technically — North Dakota RUFADAA provides legal authority for fiduciaries, but technical key succession must be addressed in a separate protocol. For North Dakota families who prefer self-custody, this protocol must be thoughtfully designed to work without institutional intermediaries.
  5. Plan for the TCJA sunset — if the federal estate tax exemption falls from ~$14M to ~$7M after 2025, North Dakota families with significant combined farm, mineral, and Bitcoin holdings may face federal estate tax exposure for the first time. Planning before the sunset is critical.
