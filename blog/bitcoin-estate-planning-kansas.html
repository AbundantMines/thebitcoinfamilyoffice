Bitcoin Estate Planning in Kansas

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  8 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in Kansas Est. 8 min read

Kansas offers a favorable tax environment for Bitcoin estate planning, with no state estate tax and no inheritance tax. Kansas has also modernized its trust law in recent years, most notably by permitting dynasty trusts under the Kansas Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act and related trust reforms — a meaningful upgrade from the state's former rule against perpetuities that limited trust durations. For Kansas Bitcoin families, the combination of zero state wealth transfer taxes and extended trust durations makes Bitcoin estate planning Kansas a more capable jurisdiction than many families realize.

Kansas is also home to an increasingly significant Bitcoin community, particularly in the Wichita and Kansas City metro areas, where technology-adjacent wealth and agricultural family wealth intersect in ways that mirror Bitcoin's appeal as a hard-asset monetary alternative. For Kansas families holding Bitcoin alongside farmland, grain elevators, or agribusiness interests, a unified multi-asset succession plan is the right frame for planning.

Kansas Tax Environment: No Estate or Inheritance Tax

Kansas imposes no state estate tax and no inheritance tax. The state repealed its estate tax in 2010, and it has never imposed a separate inheritance tax on beneficiaries. This leaves only the federal estate tax as a planning concern for Kansas Bitcoin families with substantial holdings. The federal exemption is approximately $13.99 million per individual in 2025, with the potential for a significant reduction after December 31, 2025, if the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act exemption provisions are not extended by Congress.

For Kansas Bitcoin families whose total estate — including Bitcoin, farmland, and business interests — approaches the post-2025 projected exemption of approximately $7 million per individual, the 2025 planning window is critical. Irrevocable trust transfers, family LLC minority interest gifting, and other estate reduction strategies implemented before the sunset lock in the current exemption permanently for transferred assets.

Kansas Trust Law and Dynasty Trusts

Kansas has adopted the Uniform Trust Code (Kansas Statutes Annotated Chapter 58a). The Kansas UTC provides modern trust formation and administration infrastructure, including directed trust provisions that allow investment direction and administrative trustee functions to be separated. For Bitcoin family trusts, this separation enables a Bitcoin-knowledgeable adviser to direct custody decisions while a professional trustee handles the administrative functions.

Kansas has also reformed its rule against perpetuities to permit dynasty trusts. Under Kansas law, trusts can be structured to last for extended periods — with the Uniform Statutory Rule Against Perpetuities providing significant flexibility. Kansas families seeking truly perpetual Bitcoin trusts should evaluate neighboring Wyoming or South Dakota for trust siting, but Kansas's extended trust duration provisions accommodate most practical multigenerational planning objectives without leaving the state.

Kansas has removed two of the most common barriers to multigenerational Bitcoin planning: state estate tax and duration limits. The legal infrastructure for long-duration family Bitcoin trusts exists in Kansas today.

Digital Asset Succession in Kansas: RUFADAA

Kansas adopted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), codified at Kansas Statutes Annotated §§ 58-4601 through 58-4621. RUFADAA provides the statutory basis for trustees, personal representatives, and agents under durable powers of attorney to access and administer digital assets belonging to decedents and principals.

Kansas's adoption of RUFADAA means fiduciaries have clear legal authority to access exchange accounts, wallet service accounts, and other digital asset accounts when authorized by the governing documents. For Bitcoin held in self-custody — hardware wallets, multi-signature arrangements, cold storage — RUFADAA establishes the legal authority to administer the assets, but technical access still depends entirely on the private key infrastructure. Every Kansas Bitcoin estate plan must address key succession through a technical protocol that exists independently of the legal documents and is maintained with the same rigor as the trust instrument itself.

Agricultural Land, Business Interests, and Bitcoin

Many Kansas Bitcoin families hold significant value in farmland, agribusiness entities, or family businesses alongside their Bitcoin positions. These are fundamentally different assets in their succession mechanics: farmland transfers by deed, business interests by operating agreement and assignment, and Bitcoin by cryptographic key access. A comprehensive Kansas estate plan addresses all three simultaneously.

A Kansas family LLC is the most effective integration vehicle. The LLC can hold farmland interests (or ownership in a farming entity), Bitcoin, and other family assets in a single governed structure. The operating agreement specifies management succession for each asset class, transaction authorization requirements, and transfer restrictions that preserve family ownership across generations. Minority LLC interests gifted to heirs at valuation discounts reduce the federal taxable estate while preserving the family's economic relationship to all underlying assets.

Practical Priorities for Kansas Bitcoin Families

  1. Establish a Kansas LLC as the unified holding vehicle for Bitcoin and other family assets, with an operating agreement that addresses succession of management authority for each asset class.
  2. Fund a directed trust with LLC interests, designating a Bitcoin investment trust adviser for custody governance and a professional Kansas trustee for administrative functions.
  3. Act on the 2025 federal exemption window: If your total estate approaches the post-2025 projected federal exemption, implement irrevocable trust structures and LLC minority interest gifting before December 31, 2025.
  4. Implement multi-signature custody and a key succession protocol: Ensure trustees and executors have both legal authority (RUFADAA-compliant trust language) and technical access capability (documented key succession procedures stored separately from trust documents).
  5. Evaluate Wyoming or South Dakota for trust siting if perpetual trust duration is a priority — both states permit truly perpetual trusts and are accessible to Kansas residents.
