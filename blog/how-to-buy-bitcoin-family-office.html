How to Buy Bitcoin for a Family Office: Institutional Acquisition, OTC Desks, and Custody Setup

Buying Bitcoin for a family office is not the same as buying it personally. The differences are not just scale — they are structural. A family office acquisition requires an institutional framework: documented authority, proper entity holding structure, qualified custody arrangements, and rigorous cost basis record-keeping from the moment of settlement. Getting these elements right at the acquisition stage is far easier than retrofitting them after the fact.

This guide covers the complete institutional acquisition process — from investment policy authorization through OTC desk selection, custody setup, and ongoing governance — for family offices making their first or subsequent significant Bitcoin purchases. It assumes a purchase size of $500,000 or more; smaller purchases follow a simplified version of the same framework.

Step 1: Establish a Bitcoin Investment Policy Statement

Step 1 of 6

Document the allocation before executing the first trade

The investment policy statement (IPS) is the governance foundation of any institutional Bitcoin position. Without it, you are exposed to internal and external challenges to the investment's appropriateness.

Before purchasing Bitcoin at an institutional scale, the family office should have a documented IPS that addresses:

  • Investment thesis: Why Bitcoin belongs in the portfolio. This should be a clear, first-principles statement — monetary properties, portfolio diversification, long-duration inflation hedge — not a reference to price performance.
  • Allocation parameters: Target allocation (expressed as a percentage of total investable assets), acceptable range (e.g., 5-15%), and rebalancing triggers.
  • Acquisition strategy: Lump sum vs. dollar-cost averaging, OTC vs. exchange, timing parameters, and position size limits.
  • Custody requirements: Minimum custody standards — self-custody multisig, qualified custodian, or hybrid. No exchange-only custody for a significant institutional position.
  • Governance authority: Who has authority to execute Bitcoin purchases? How are transactions authorized? What constitutes a quorum for major decisions?
  • Reporting requirements: How frequently is the Bitcoin position valued and reported? What events trigger immediate notification to family principals?

The comprehensive IPS framework is covered at The Bitcoin Investment Policy Statement. For families comparing self-custody against ETF exposure, the direct ownership vs. ETF tax analysis provides the analytical foundation for the IPS custody decision.

Step 2: Determine the Acquisition Channel

Step 2 of 6

Choose between OTC desk, institutional exchange, or dollar-cost averaging

The right acquisition channel depends on purchase size, timing flexibility, and market impact tolerance.

OTC (Over-the-Counter) Desks

For purchases above $250,000-500,000, OTC desks are typically superior to exchange-based execution. An OTC desk acts as a counterparty, providing a quoted price for a specific quantity of Bitcoin rather than breaking the order into smaller pieces that move the market. Benefits:

  • Price certainty: The OTC desk quotes a price before execution; you know your exact acquisition cost before committing
  • Minimal market impact: Large exchange orders are visible in the order book and can move prices against you during execution; OTC trades are not
  • White-glove service: OTC desks handle all settlement logistics and work with your custody setup
  • Compliance infrastructure: Reputable OTC desks have full KYC/AML procedures and can provide the compliance documentation institutional clients need

Well-regarded institutional OTC desks include Genesis Trading, Cumberland DRW, Wintermute, and the OTC operations of major exchanges (Coinbase Prime, Kraken OTC). For family offices, Coinbase Prime and Kraken's institutional desk are often the most accessible starting points due to their regulatory standing and institutional onboarding infrastructure.

Institutional Exchange Accounts

For purchases under $250,000, or for dollar-cost averaging programs, an institutional exchange account (Coinbase Prime, Gemini Institutional, Kraken) provides adequate liquidity with less counterparty overhead than a dedicated OTC relationship. Execute limit orders rather than market orders to control the exact acquisition price; use TWAP (time-weighted average price) algorithms if the exchange provides them for large market orders.

Dollar-Cost Averaging

Spreading a large acquisition over time — purchasing the same dollar amount weekly or monthly — reduces price timing risk at the cost of potentially higher average acquisition cost in trending markets. For a family office making its first significant Bitcoin allocation, a structured DCA program over 3-6 months is a reasonable approach that addresses the internal governance question of "what if we buy right before a major price drop?"

Step 3: Select and Onboard Institutional Counterparties

Step 3 of 6

Establish trading relationships before you need to execute

Institutional onboarding takes time. Start the process well before you intend to execute your first purchase.

Onboarding requirements for OTC desks and institutional exchanges typically include:

  • Entity documentation: Formation documents (LLC operating agreement, trust agreement, or corporation articles), good standing certificate, and EIN
  • Ownership and control: Identification of all beneficial owners above a threshold (typically 25%), plus controlling managers or trustees
  • KYC/AML: Government-issued ID and proof of address for all principals; source-of-funds documentation
  • Authorized signatory: Designation of the individuals authorized to execute trades and initiate withdrawals
  • Custody address whitelist: Many institutional platforms require pre-registration of Bitcoin withdrawal addresses before funds can be withdrawn to self-custody

Allow 2-4 weeks for full onboarding, longer for more complex entity structures. Do not expect to open an OTC account and execute a large trade on the same day.

Step 4: Design and Implement Custody Architecture

Step 4 of 6

Establish the custody destination before settlement

Never take delivery of Bitcoin to an unprepared address. Set up, test, and verify the custody destination before settling any trade.

The fundamental custody decision for a family office is the self-custody vs. qualified custodian vs. hybrid choice:

Self-custody multisigMaximumNoneLow ongoingHigh

Hybrid (multisig + custodian)HighLowModerateModerate

For family offices above $10 million in Bitcoin, the hybrid approach is most common: a multi-signature arrangement where the family holds keys and a qualified custodian (Unchained Capital, Casa, Anchorage) provides a co-signing key and institutional backup. This preserves meaningful self-sovereign control while adding professional infrastructure and clear key recovery procedures.

The detailed technical framework for custody architecture is at Bitcoin Custody Architecture. The multisig-specific framework is at Multi-Signature Bitcoin Custody for Family Offices.

Before settling any trade, verify the receiving address through at minimum two independent methods — display it on the hardware device that generated it, confirm with the custody provider, and send a small test amount before moving the full position.

Step 5: Execute the Acquisition and Document Cost Basis

Step 5 of 6

Execute carefully and document immediately

The tax basis for every Bitcoin acquired is established at the moment of acquisition. Document it immediately and completely.

Execution checklist:

  • Confirm the receiving custody address with your custody provider (never enter an address from memory)
  • Send a test transaction of nominal value to the custody address and confirm receipt before settling the full amount
  • Execute the OTC or exchange trade with an authorized signatory as specified in your IPS
  • Obtain a trade confirmation showing the exact quantity, price, and timestamp of each lot purchased
  • If DCA program: document each individual purchase as a separate cost basis lot
  • Confirm on-chain settlement — the transaction appears in the block explorer with the correct quantity at the custody address

Cost basis documentation for each lot:

  • Date of acquisition
  • Quantity of Bitcoin acquired (in BTC, to 8 decimal places)
  • USD price per BTC at acquisition
  • Total USD cost
  • Source of funds
  • On-chain transaction ID (TXID)
  • Custody address received to

This documentation becomes the basis for all future tax calculations — HIFO, FIFO, or specific identification. See the full tax reporting framework in Bitcoin Tax Reporting for High-Net-Worth Investors.

Step 6: Establish Ongoing Governance and Reporting

Step 6 of 6

Integrate Bitcoin into the family office governance framework

The first purchase is the beginning of an ongoing institutional relationship with Bitcoin. Build the systems that support it.

  • Periodic valuation: Value the Bitcoin position at month-end and quarter-end using the methodology specified in your IPS. Many family offices use the last trading day's closing price from a designated exchange.
  • Portfolio reporting: Include Bitcoin in consolidated portfolio reports. For internal reporting, report both BTC quantity and USD value. Track both.
  • Tax record maintenance: Maintain a perpetual record of all acquisition lots, any dispositions, and the running basis for each lot. This is your most important ongoing administrative task.
  • Custody review: Annually verify that custody devices are functional, firmware is updated, and key access procedures remain current. Rotate any keys that may be compromised.
  • IPS review: Review the Bitcoin IPS annually or after any significant event (major price movement, family governance change, custody technology advancement). The IPS is a living document.
