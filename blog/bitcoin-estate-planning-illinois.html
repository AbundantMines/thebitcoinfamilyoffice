Bitcoin Estate Planning in Illinois

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in Illinois Est. 9 min read

Illinois is among a minority of states that imposes its own estate tax independent of the federal system. For Bitcoin families domiciled in Illinois, this creates a distinct and compounding tax burden: the Illinois estate tax applies to estates exceeding approximately $4 million, at rates reaching 16%, layered on top of the federal estate tax that applies to estates above the federal exemption. A Bitcoin holder who has accumulated a significant position and not engaged in proactive planning may find that Illinois estate tax alone consumes a material portion of the estate — before the federal tax is even calculated.

This guide covers the Illinois estate tax framework, trust and LLC structures suited to Illinois Bitcoin families, the state's Illinois Trust Code, digital asset succession law under RUFADAA, and the strategic priorities that allow Illinois Bitcoin holders to transfer generational wealth without unnecessary erosion. Bitcoin estate planning in Illinois sits at the intersection of state tax law, digital asset custody, and multi-generational trust design — a combination that demands specialized legal and advisory expertise.

The Illinois Estate Tax: What Bitcoin Families Must Know

Illinois imposes its own estate tax under 35 ILCS 405 (the Illinois Estate and Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax Act). As of 2024, the Illinois estate tax exemption is approximately $4 million per individual — substantially lower than the federal exemption of $13.99 million. This gap means many Illinois Bitcoin families face state estate tax obligations even when they are below the federal threshold, and those above the federal threshold face a double layer of tax.

The Illinois estate tax rate structure begins at 0.8% and rises to 16% on the largest estates. Unlike the federal estate tax, Illinois does not index its exemption for inflation, and the legislature has historically resisted raising the threshold. For a Bitcoin family with a $6 million estate, Illinois estate tax alone can exceed $400,000. For a $10 million estate, the Illinois liability approaches $1 million. These are real numbers that demand real planning — not footnotes to be addressed at death.

Illinois does not have a state gift tax. This asymmetry is a critical planning opportunity: lifetime gifts reduce the Illinois taxable estate without triggering a separate Illinois transfer tax. Strategic gifting programs — using the annual exclusion, lifetime exemption, and trust structures — are the primary tool for reducing Illinois estate tax exposure over time.

Illinois's $4 million estate tax exemption captures a far broader range of Bitcoin families than the federal threshold does. Proactive planning is not optional — it is the difference between transferring wealth and surrendering it.

Illinois Trust Code and Bitcoin Trust Structures

Illinois adopted the Illinois Trust Code (ITC) effective January 1, 2020, codified at 760 ILCS 3. The ITC is based on the Uniform Trust Code and modernizes Illinois trust law to support sophisticated trust structures, including directed trusts, decanting, and trust protector arrangements. For Bitcoin families, the ITC provides the statutory foundation for several important strategies:

  • Directed trusts: The ITC expressly authorizes trust instruments to create separate roles for investment advisors and administrative trustees. For Bitcoin trusts, this allows a family member or specialized Bitcoin custodian to direct custody decisions — controlling private keys, multi-signature arrangements, and custody architecture — while a corporate trustee handles administrative, tax, and distribution functions. Neither role bears liability for the actions of the other, properly structured.
  • Trust decanting: ITC §1001 and related provisions allow a trustee with distribution discretion to transfer assets from an older trust to a new trust with updated terms — often without court approval. Bitcoin trusts drafted before digital asset succession was a recognized discipline may need decanting to add custody protocols, digital asset definitions, and successor trustee authority for hardware wallets and multi-signature keys.
  • Dynasty trusts: Illinois permits perpetual trusts, allowing Bitcoin held in trust to compound across multiple generations without triggering estate tax at each generation — a critical advantage for families with a long time horizon and a Bitcoin position they intend to hold indefinitely.

Irrevocable Trust Strategies for Illinois Bitcoin Families

The most effective strategies for reducing Illinois estate tax exposure involve transferring Bitcoin out of the taxable estate irrevocably during the grantor's lifetime. The primary vehicles are:

Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs): A GRAT allows a Bitcoin holder to transfer a position to an irrevocable trust while retaining annuity payments for a fixed term. If Bitcoin appreciates above the IRS Section 7520 hurdle rate during the GRAT term, the excess passes to heirs free of both Illinois estate tax and federal estate tax. GRATs are particularly powerful for Bitcoin given the asset's historical appreciation rate relative to the hurdle rate. Illinois's lack of a gift tax means the initial contribution is not subject to state transfer tax.

Spousal Lifetime Access Trusts (SLATs): A SLAT allows the grantor to transfer assets — including Bitcoin — to an irrevocable trust for a spouse's benefit, removing those assets from the Illinois taxable estate while preserving the spouse's indirect access through trust distributions. SLATs require careful drafting to avoid the reciprocal trust doctrine and to ensure the grantor spouse does not retain interests that pull the assets back into the estate.

Irrevocable Life Insurance Trusts (ILITs): For Illinois Bitcoin families with large positions and limited liquidity, an ILIT provides estate liquidity to pay Illinois and federal estate taxes without forcing a Bitcoin sale at an unfavorable time. Insurance proceeds pass outside the taxable estate when the ILIT is properly structured and the insured retains no incidents of ownership.

Illinois LLCs for Bitcoin Holding and Valuation Discounts

Illinois LLCs (governed by the Illinois Limited Liability Company Act, 805 ILCS 180) are effective holding vehicles for Bitcoin with significant estate planning advantages. Bitcoin held through a properly structured family LLC can qualify for minority interest and lack-of-marketability discounts when interests are transferred to heirs or trusts — typically 20–35% below the underlying Bitcoin value. These discounts reduce the Illinois estate tax value of transferred interests while preserving the economic exposure to Bitcoin appreciation.

An LLC operating agreement for a Bitcoin holding company should address: multi-signature custody requirements and key management protocols, transaction authorization thresholds (requiring manager or member consent for Bitcoin movements above specified thresholds), succession of manager roles in the event of death or incapacity, restrictions on voluntary transfers of membership interests, and integration with the family's broader estate plan documents. The LLC is not merely a tax vehicle — it is the legal governance framework for how Bitcoin decisions are made and who has authority to make them.

Digital Asset Succession Under Illinois RUFADAA

Illinois enacted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA) at 765 ILCS 1085. RUFADAA establishes a framework for fiduciary access to digital assets — including cryptocurrency — after death or incapacity. Under RUFADAA, an executor or trustee has the legal authority to access, manage, and distribute digital assets if the governing instrument or a court order grants that authority.

However, RUFADAA is not a technical solution to Bitcoin succession — it is a legal authorization framework. A fiduciary authorized by RUFADAA to access Bitcoin still needs the private keys, hardware wallet PINs, and multi-signature credentials to actually do so. Illinois Bitcoin estate plans must therefore combine the legal architecture (RUFADAA authority in the trust or will) with technical succession protocols stored securely and separately from the estate documents themselves. Wyoming Trust Situs for Illinois Families

Illinois families are not confined to Illinois trust law. A trust's situs can be established in Wyoming — capturing Wyoming's directed trust act, asset protection provisions, and perpetual trust rules — even when the grantor and beneficiaries reside in Illinois. The Illinois income tax implications of out-of-state trust situs require careful analysis: Illinois taxes trust income distributed to Illinois beneficiaries regardless of where the trust is administered. However, accumulation trusts with long time horizons and beneficiaries who may eventually relocate from Illinois can achieve meaningful deferral through Wyoming situs combined with an Illinois-optimized estate plan. Practical Priorities for Illinois Bitcoin Families

  1. Quantify the Illinois estate tax exposure based on your current Bitcoin position and total estate value — the $4 million threshold means a broader range of families are in scope for Illinois estate tax planning than for federal estate tax planning.
  2. Implement a strategic gifting program taking advantage of Illinois's lack of a state gift tax — annual exclusion gifts, trust contributions, and lifetime exemption utilization all reduce the Illinois taxable estate without state transfer tax cost.
  3. Establish an Illinois LLC as the Bitcoin holding vehicle, with an operating agreement that governs custody, succession, and governance, and gift minority interests to heirs or trusts at discounted valuations.
  4. Consider irrevocable trust strategies — GRATs, SLATs, dynasty trusts — based on your position size, age, health, and willingness to part with assets irrevocably during your lifetime.
  5. Document Bitcoin custody explicitly in a technical succession protocol stored and maintained separately from the trust instrument, coordinated with your custody architecture and updated when custody arrangements change.
