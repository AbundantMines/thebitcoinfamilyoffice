Bitcoin Estate Planning in Utah

The Bitcoin Family Office  ·  February 2026  ·  9 min read

Home › Research › Bitcoin Estate Planning in Utah Est. 9 min read

Utah imposes no state estate tax and no inheritance tax — a significant planning advantage for Bitcoin families that eliminates an entire layer of transfer tax concern present in higher-tax states. Combined with a flat 4.55% income tax rate, a modernized Uniform Trust Code, the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), and a growing technology ecosystem anchored by the Silicon Slopes corridor, Utah offers a compelling environment for Bitcoin estate planning that many families overlook in favor of better-known zero-tax jurisdictions.

This guide covers Utah's trust law framework, LLC structures for Bitcoin holding, digital asset succession under Utah's RUFADAA statute, and the federal-level strategies that allow Utah families to protect and transfer Bitcoin across generations. Bitcoin estate planning Utah rewards families who build intentional structures — Utah's favorable baseline is only fully realized when the underlying custody architecture and succession documentation are built to match.

Utah's No-Estate-Tax Advantage

Utah eliminated its state estate tax effective for deaths occurring on or after January 1, 2005, when federal estate tax reform eliminated the state death tax credit on which Utah's pick-up tax had been based. Utah imposes no inheritance tax. For Bitcoin families, this creates a clean transfer tax baseline: only the federal estate tax (applicable to estates above $13.99 million per individual in 2025) applies at death.

For Utah families with Bitcoin holdings below the federal exemption — the majority of Bitcoin holders — this means zero transfer tax at death when assets pass to heirs through a properly structured estate plan. Unlike families in Massachusetts (estate tax at $2M), Washington (estate tax at $2.19M), or New Jersey (inheritance tax on most transfers outside immediate family), Utah Bitcoin families accumulate and transfer wealth within a single-layer federal tax framework.

Utah's zero estate tax, flat income tax, and RUFADAA digital asset law make it one of the more thoughtfully positioned states for Bitcoin estate planning — combining a favorable tax baseline with the legal infrastructure Bitcoin families actually need.

Utah's Uniform Trust Code and Digital Asset Law

Utah enacted the Utah Uniform Trust Code (UUTC), codified at Utah Code Ann. §§ 75-7-101 et seq., effective July 1, 2004. The UUTC provides a modern statutory framework for trust formation and administration. Key provisions for Bitcoin families include:

  • Trust protectors: Utah trust law recognizes trust protector roles, allowing the trust instrument to vest specific powers in named individuals — including power to adapt custody arrangements, change trustees, or modify investment direction — without those individuals becoming trustees with full fiduciary duties. For Bitcoin trusts with multigenerational horizons, a technically informed trust protector can manage custody protocol evolution as the technology changes.
  • Directed trusts: Utah trust instruments can create separate investment advisors and administrative advisors, each with defined authority. This directed trust framework — while less comprehensive than Wyoming's dedicated DPTA statute — enables the functional separation that Bitcoin custody requires: a custody-specialized advisor directs Bitcoin decisions while a corporate trustee handles tax, reporting, and distributions.
  • Dynasty trusts: Utah permits trusts of personal property to continue for up to 1,000 years under Utah Code Ann. § 75-2-1203. This extraordinary perpetuity period makes Utah competitive with Wyoming and South Dakota for families seeking multigenerational Bitcoin holding structures. Bitcoin held in a properly structured Utah dynasty trust can benefit many generations without a forced taxable termination.
  • Decanting: Utah's trust decanting statute (Utah Code Ann. § 75-7-1001 et seq.) allows trustees to pour assets from an older trust into a new trust with updated terms. This allows Bitcoin trusts with outdated custody provisions to be modernized without court proceedings.

Utah's RUFADAA: Digital Asset Succession with Statutory Support

Utah enacted the Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA), codified at Utah Code Ann. §§ 75-10-101 et seq. This statute is a significant planning advantage: it creates a clear legal framework for fiduciary access to Bitcoin and other digital assets, establishing a priority hierarchy and defining the authority of personal representatives, trustees, and agents under powers of attorney.

Under Utah's RUFADAA, an authorized fiduciary can access, manage, and transfer digital assets — including Bitcoin held in self-custody — if the governing estate planning documents grant that authority. The statute establishes a three-tier priority: first, an online tool provided by the custodial service (if applicable); second, explicit directions in the estate planning documents; third, platform terms of service. For Bitcoin in self-custody, there is no online tool — the documents are the authority.

The practical implication: Utah estate planning documents must explicitly authorize digital asset access by name and category. Generic "property" language in older documents will not suffice under RUFADAA's framework. Every Utah Bitcoin estate plan should include specific RUFADAA authority language in the trust instrument, durable power of attorney, and will, accompanied by a separate technical access document maintained outside the estate plan files.

Utah LLCs for Bitcoin Holding

Utah LLCs (governed by the Utah Revised Uniform Limited Liability Company Act, Utah Code Ann. §§ 48-3a-101 et seq., effective 2014) provide effective Bitcoin holding structures with several advantages:

Valuation discounts: Bitcoin held through a properly structured Utah family LLC qualifies for minority interest and lack-of-marketability discounts when membership interests are transferred to trusts or heirs — typically 20–35%. These discounts accelerate the pace at which Bitcoin value can be shifted out of the federal taxable estate through annual exclusion gifting and lifetime exemption use.

Operating agreement governance: Utah's LLC statute grants broad operating agreement freedom, allowing families to specify multi-signature custody requirements, key-person succession protocols, transaction authorization procedures, and voting thresholds for major custody decisions. The operating agreement becomes the legal governance document for Bitcoin custody.

Charging order protection: Utah LLC law (Utah Code Ann. § 48-3a-503) provides charging order protection — creditors of a member cannot force dissolution or seize LLC assets, including Bitcoin, and are limited to charging orders against future distributions.

Federal Estate Planning Strategies for Utah Bitcoin Holders

Utah Bitcoin families can focus entirely on federal-level planning strategies, which offer substantial value without any state-level tax leakage:

Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs): GRATs allow Utah Bitcoin holders to transfer appreciation above the IRS Section 7520 hurdle rate to heirs estate-tax-free. Rolling two-year GRATs funded with Bitcoin LLC interests — with discounted valuations at entry — can systematically transfer significant value to descendants with minimal gift tax cost.

Qualified Opportunity Zone (QOZ) Strategies: Utah has designated Qualified Opportunity Zones in Salt Lake, Utah, and Weber counties. Bitcoin holders with realized gains can invest in Utah QOZ funds for capital gains deferral and potential elimination on the QOZ investment appreciation — a strategy that complements, rather than substitutes for, Bitcoin estate planning structures.

Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs): Utah Bitcoin holders with low-cost-basis positions can transfer appreciated Bitcoin to a CRT, receive an annuity or unitrust payment for a term of years, and defer capital gains recognition while passing the remainder to a qualified charity. Utah's strong philanthropy culture makes CRT structures particularly well-suited to Utah family values.

Wyoming Trust Situs for Utah Families

Utah families with long-horizon dynasty trust objectives may benefit from establishing a Wyoming-sited trust — accessing Wyoming's DPTA directed trust statute and true perpetual trust provisions — while maintaining Utah residency. Utah's own 1,000-year perpetuity rule makes this less critical than for families in states with shorter perpetuity limits, but Wyoming's directed trust statute remains more comprehensive than Utah's trust advisor framework for families requiring the full separation of investment and administrative functions. Practical Priorities for Utah Bitcoin Families

  1. Update estate planning documents to include specific RUFADAA language — Utah's RUFADAA creates a clear legal framework, but only if the documents explicitly invoke it. Generic property language is insufficient.
  2. Establish a Utah LLC as the Bitcoin holding vehicle — with a carefully drafted operating agreement governing custody architecture, key succession, and membership transfer restrictions.
  3. Fund irrevocable trusts with discounted LLC interests — through lifetime gifting or IDGT note sales — removing Bitcoin appreciation from the federal taxable estate over time.
  4. Maintain a tested technical succession protocol — separate from and updated independently of the estate plan documents — covering hardware wallet access, multi-signature key succession, and custody service credentials.
  5. Consider a Utah dynasty trust with a 1,000-year horizon for the primary family trust — capturing the long-term accumulation benefits of a perpetual holding structure without leaving Utah's legal framework.
