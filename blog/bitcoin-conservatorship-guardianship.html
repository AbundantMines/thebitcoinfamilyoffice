Bitcoin Conservatorship: What Courts Do to Your Bitcoin When Planning Fails

If you become incapacitated without a durable power of attorney or funded revocable trust, a court appoints a conservator to manage your finances — including your Bitcoin. That conservator may be a professional stranger, must get court approval before making major decisions, files public reports on your assets, and can petition to liquidate your entire Bitcoin position. This is the outcome that a few hundred dollars of estate planning documents prevents.

By The Bitcoin Family Office Editorial Team  ·  Updated March 2026  ·  11 min read

Most estate planning content focuses on death. Conservatorship is what happens before death — when you are alive but unable to manage your own affairs. It is, in many ways, a worse outcome than dying without a will, because it can last for years, costs far more, and strips decision-making authority from your family in favor of court-supervised strangers.

For Bitcoin holders specifically, conservatorship creates a cluster of problems that no other asset class generates to the same degree:

  • Courts have no established framework for Bitcoin custody during conservatorship
  • Professional conservators rarely understand Bitcoin and may treat it as an imprudent speculative asset
  • Court-required conservative investment standards may effectively require liquidation of a Bitcoin position
  • The public nature of conservatorship proceedings can expose your Bitcoin holdings to security risks
  • Self-custody Bitcoin may be inaccessible during the months-long process to establish legal authority

This article explains how conservatorship works, what it means specifically for Bitcoin, and — most importantly — the simple planning steps that make conservatorship unnecessary.

Guardianship vs. Conservatorship: The Distinction

Courts use two overlapping terms for incapacity-related appointments, and the distinction matters:

Term What It Covers Who Is Appointed Prevented By
Guardianship Personal decisions — healthcare, residence, daily care, medical treatment Guardian (of the person) Healthcare proxy / advance directive
Conservatorship Financial decisions — property management, investments, banking, Bitcoin Conservator (of the estate) Durable power of attorney or funded revocable trust
Limited guardianship/conservatorship Specific decisions only — court defines the scope Limited guardian or conservator Same documents + clear evidence of partial capacity

In some states (California, notably), both roles are combined into a single proceeding called a "conservatorship of the person and estate." In others (New York, Texas), guardianship and conservatorship are separate proceedings. Either way, the financial management role — the one that covers Bitcoin — is the conservatorship.

The guardian and conservator may be the same person or different people. Courts often prefer to appoint family members if available and appropriate, but will appoint professional fiduciaries when family members are unavailable, are in conflict, or lack the competence to manage the incapacitated person's estate.

How a Conservatorship Proceeding Works

Step 1: Petition Filed

Any "interested party" — a family member, close friend, healthcare provider, or even a government agency — can file a petition for conservatorship with the probate court. The petition must allege that the proposed conservatee lacks the capacity to manage their financial affairs and identify a proposed conservator.

For Bitcoin holders, the trigger is typically a medical event: a stroke, accident, traumatic brain injury, or onset of dementia that leaves them unable to conduct financial transactions, communicate financial instructions, or access their own accounts. Family members who discover the Bitcoin exists but cannot access it — because no one knows the seed phrase location or has legal authority — frequently initiate conservatorship proceedings as the only available remedy.

Step 2: Court Evaluation and Hearing

The court appoints a professional investigator or court visitor to interview the proposed conservatee and assess their capacity. A physician's declaration (or independent medical examination) establishes the medical basis for incapacity. The proposed conservatee has the right to contest the proceeding — in some cases, they are represented by appointed counsel.

The hearing typically occurs 4–12 weeks after the petition is filed, though emergency temporary conservatorships can be granted in days when urgent financial action is needed. Total time from petition to established conservatorship: typically 3–6 months in most states.

Step 3: Conservator Appointed and Bond Posted

The court appoints a conservator — family member if appropriate, professional fiduciary if not — and typically requires them to post a bond. The bond amount is calculated as a percentage of the estate value. For a $2 million Bitcoin position, the bond premium might be $10,000–$20,000 per year, paid from the conservatee's estate.

Step 4: Inventory Filed

Within 90 days of appointment (varies by state), the conservator must file a complete inventory of the conservatee's assets with the court. This inventory is typically a public record. For Bitcoin holders: your Bitcoin holdings, approximate values, exchange accounts, and any other assets become part of the public court file. In some jurisdictions, this information is fully searchable online.

Security Risk

A public court inventory of Bitcoin holdings is a significant security vulnerability. Anyone can search the probate court records, find the conservatorship, and learn that the conservatee holds significant Bitcoin. This has been exploited in targeted social engineering attacks against conservators and family members. Proper incapacity planning keeps your Bitcoin holdings private; conservatorship makes them a matter of public record.

Step 5: Ongoing Court Supervision

A conservator does not act autonomously. Major financial decisions require court approval — or at minimum, notice to the court and opportunity for interested parties to object. Specifically for Bitcoin:

  • Selling Bitcoin typically requires a court petition and approval
  • Transferring Bitcoin to a different custody arrangement requires court approval
  • Making investment decisions about the Bitcoin position may require court approval or a court-appointed investment advisor
  • Annual accountings must be filed with the court showing all transactions and current asset values

Courts apply a "prudent investor" standard to conservatorships — requiring the conservator to manage assets in a way that a reasonable, prudent investor would. For Bitcoin: courts in many jurisdictions have characterized Bitcoin as highly speculative, and conservators have successfully petitioned to liquidate Bitcoin positions on the grounds that holding a volatile, speculative asset is inconsistent with the prudent investor standard. A court-appointed conservator may sell your entire Bitcoin position over your family's objections.

Step 6: Fees Paid From the Estate

Conservatorship is expensive, and all costs come out of the conservatee's estate:

  • Attorney fees (petitioner's attorney): $5,000–$25,000 to establish the conservatorship
  • Court investigator fees: $300–$1,000
  • Conservator fees: Professional conservators charge 1–3% of the estate value annually. On a $2 million estate: $20,000–$60,000 per year in conservator fees.
  • Bond premiums: $5,000–$20,000 per year depending on estate size
  • Accounting fees: $3,000–$8,000 per year for annual court accountings
  • Conservatee's attorney fees: If the conservatee contests the proceeding, additional $10,000–$50,000+

Total first-year cost of a contested conservatorship on a $2 million estate: $50,000–$150,000. Total ongoing annual cost: $30,000–$90,000 per year for the duration of the incapacity. For a 5-year incapacity: $200,000–$500,000 extracted from the estate before the conservatee dies or regains capacity.

Bitcoin-Specific Conservatorship Problems

The Access Problem: Self-Custody Bitcoin Is Effectively Frozen

The moment a Bitcoin holder loses capacity, their self-custody Bitcoin faces a legal freeze. The incapacitated person cannot access it. Family members cannot access it without legal authority. And establishing legal authority — through a conservatorship proceeding — takes months.

During those months:

  • Bitcoin continues to move in price — potentially dramatically — without anyone authorized to manage it
  • Mining operations may be disrupted if the holder was the sole authorized manager of the mining LLC
  • Exchange accounts may be locked if the exchange detects unusual access patterns or receives notice of incapacity
  • Loan positions collateralized by Bitcoin (if any) may face margin calls without anyone authorized to respond

The conservatorship, once established, gives the conservator legal authority to act. But the conservator then faces their own Bitcoin problem: they need to access the Bitcoin. If it's in self-custody without a letter of instruction describing wallet locations and access protocol, the conservator must obtain court permission to hire technical forensic assistance to locate and access the assets — adding additional cost, delay, and public disclosure.

The Prudent Investor Standard vs. Bitcoin Holdings

Every conservator is bound by the prudent investor standard — a legal duty to manage the conservatee's assets as a reasonably prudent investor would. Courts applying this standard have frequently concluded that holding a highly concentrated position in a single volatile asset (Bitcoin) is imprudent — and have authorized or required conservators to diversify by selling Bitcoin.

This is not hypothetical. Court cases from California, New York, and Florida show conservators successfully petitioning to liquidate Bitcoin positions on prudent investor grounds, over the objection of family members who understood the holder's intent to hold long-term. The court doesn't care about the holder's investment philosophy — it cares about the legal standard of care the conservator owes to the estate.

A properly executed durable power of attorney — drafted before incapacity — can include an explicit "hold Bitcoin long-term" instruction to the financial agent, overriding the default prudent investor pressure. A court-appointed conservator has no such instruction to follow.

Mining Operations: The Operating Authority Gap

If the incapacitated Bitcoin holder is the manager of an LLC that operates mining equipment, the conservator faces an additional problem: managing the LLC requires authority under the LLC operating agreement, not just conservatorship authority. The conservator's authority extends to the holder's personal assets — including their LLC membership interest — but managing the LLC day-to-day requires authority under the operating agreement.

Unless the operating agreement has a successor manager provision, the conservator may need to petition the court for authority to manage the LLC — another proceeding, more cost, more delay. In the interim, the mining operation runs without authorized management: hosting agreements may lapse, revenue may not be collected, and equipment decisions stall.

When Conservatorship Cannot Be Avoided

Proper planning prevents conservatorship in most cases. But there are scenarios where conservatorship may occur regardless:

  • Sudden incapacity before planning is executed: A healthy 35-year-old who intends to get a POA "next month" and suffers a traumatic brain injury today has no planning in place. If the incapacity is permanent, conservatorship is the only path to financial management authority.
  • Disputed POA: If family members challenge the validity of an existing POA — claiming it was executed under duress, that the grantor lacked capacity, or that it was revoked — a conservatorship proceeding may be initiated to resolve the dispute. A well-executed POA witnessed and notarized correctly makes this harder to challenge.
  • Agent's failure to act: If the named POA agent fails to act — due to their own incapacity, unwillingness, or a conflict of interest — and no alternate agent was named, conservatorship fills the gap. Always name at least two alternate agents.
  • Minor with Bitcoin: A minor cannot legally control their own assets. If a minor inherits Bitcoin and no custodian is designated (UTMA account or trust), a court may appoint a guardian of the property. See our UTMA guide for the correct structure.

How to Navigate a Bitcoin Conservatorship (If It's Already Happening)

If a conservatorship is already underway for a family member with Bitcoin, here is what matters:

1. Secure the Bitcoin Immediately

Before the conservatorship is formally established, family members may have a narrow window to secure access information — not to take the Bitcoin, but to document its location and value for the court inventory. Work with the incapacitated person's estate planning attorney immediately to understand what authority exists during the gap period.

2. Petition for a Family Member as Conservator

Courts prefer family members as conservators when they are willing, able, and trustworthy. A family member who understands Bitcoin — and can explain to the court why maintaining the Bitcoin position is consistent with the conservatee's long-documented investment philosophy — is a far better conservator than a professional fiduciary who will default to liquidation as the conservative option.

3. Request Specific Authority in the Conservatorship Order

When the conservatorship is established, petition the court for an order that specifically addresses Bitcoin: authority to maintain the existing custody architecture, authority to hold Bitcoin without diversification as consistent with the conservatee's documented investment philosophy, and authority to manage mining operations (if applicable) under the LLC operating agreement. A court order that addresses Bitcoin specifically is far better than relying on the general prudent investor standard.

4. Present Evidence of the Conservatee's Investment Philosophy

Courts applying the prudent investor standard can and do consider the conservatee's documented investment history and philosophy. If the conservatee has a long history of holding Bitcoin, has written about their conviction, or has made statements to financial advisors about long-term Bitcoin holding — this evidence can support a court order allowing the conservator to maintain the Bitcoin position rather than liquidate it.

5. Consider a Substituted Judgment Petition

Many states allow a "substituted judgment" proceeding — where the court considers what the conservatee would have decided if they were able to make the decision themselves. If there is clear evidence that the conservatee's intent was to hold Bitcoin for the long term, a substituted judgment petition can produce a court order authorizing long-term holding consistent with that intent.

The Five-Document Stack That Prevents Conservatorship

Conservatorship is almost entirely preventable with proper planning. The five documents that make conservatorship unnecessary for Bitcoin holders:

  1. Durable Power of Attorney with RUFADAA Digital Asset Authority
    Names a financial agent with explicit authority to manage Bitcoin, access exchange accounts, manage wallets, and operate Bitcoin mining LLCs. The agent can act immediately upon your incapacity — no court involvement required. See our complete POA guide.
  2. Healthcare Proxy / Advance Directive
    Names a healthcare agent for medical decisions. Prevents guardianship of the person in parallel with the POA preventing conservatorship of the estate. See our healthcare directive guide.
  3. Funded Revocable Living Trust
    A revocable trust with a successor trustee is the gold standard for incapacity planning — the successor trustee takes over management seamlessly, with no court involvement, no bond, no public filings. If you fund a revocable trust with your Bitcoin (by titling exchange accounts and LLCs to the trust), the successor trustee manages everything without a POA or conservatorship.
  4. Letter of Instruction with Incapacity Protocol
    Tells your agent and successor trustee where the Bitcoin is, how to access it, who to call for technical assistance, and what NOT to do in the first 48 hours. Legal authority without operational information is useless. See our letter of instruction guide.
  5. LLC Operating Agreement with Successor Manager
    Designates a successor manager for any Bitcoin mining or holding LLC, so the operating authority transfers cleanly upon your incapacity without conservatorship or court intervention.
The Bottom Line

A conservatorship is a $50,000–$500,000 problem that a $3,000–$8,000 estate planning package prevents. The documents take a few hours to execute. The conservatorship, if it happens, takes years to resolve and may force the liquidation of your Bitcoin position at a time and price you never would have chosen. Execute the documents while you are competent. There is no later.

State-Specific Conservatorship Considerations

Conservatorship law varies by state, but the core structure — court petition, hearing, appointment, bond, inventory, annual accounting — is consistent across all U.S. jurisdictions. Key state-specific variations:

  • California: Probate Code §§1800–2893; combined "conservatorship of the person and estate"; Alameda and Los Angeles counties have Probate Courts with established experience; costs tend to be higher than average due to attorney fee market
  • New York: Mental Hygiene Law Article 81; separate "guardian of the person" and "guardian of the property"; New York courts have specific cryptocurrency guidelines emerging from recent cases
  • Texas: Estates Code Chapter 1201; "guardianship of the estate"; Texas courts have used substituted judgment doctrine more liberally than some other states
  • Florida: Florida Statutes Chapter 744; "guardianship of the property"; Florida has an active Guardianship Association and well-developed professional guardian infrastructure
  • Wyoming: W.S. §§3-3-101 through 3-3-502; limited conservatorship available; Wyoming courts' familiarity with digital assets (Digital Asset Act) may provide a more favorable forum for Bitcoin-related conservatorship disputes
